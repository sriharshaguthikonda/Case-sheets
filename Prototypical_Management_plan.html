Examination

INITIAL INVESTIGATIONS

  • Consent (ensure informed, check understanding)
  • Blood work:
    • Myeloma Screen: Serum protein electrophoresis & immunofixation, Serum Free Light Chains (SFLC), FBC, U&E, LFTs, Bone profile (Calcium), LDH, Beta-2 microglobulin
  • Imaging:
    • Urine dip stick & Bence-Jones Protein analysis
    • Whole-body MRI (or Skeletal Survey if unavailable)
  • Further Investigations
    • Specialized tests:
      • Bone Marrow Aspirate & Biopsy (for diagnosis, cytogenetics)

TREATMENT PLAN

  • Shared Decision-Making
    • Haematologist-led discussion of treatment pathway
    • Discuss transplant eligibility (based on age and fitness)
    • Discuss risks vs benefits of treatment regimens
  • Specific treatment:
    • first line
      • Induction Therapy (Chemotherapy): Based on transplant eligibility.
        • Common regimens include combinations of Proteasome Inhibitors (e.g., Bortezomib), Immunomodulatory drugs (IMiDs, e.g., Lenalidomide), and Corticosteroids (e.g., Dexamethasone).

    • Second line
    • Third line
    • Fourth line
    • .
  • Supportive Care (Crucial alongside specific treatment):
    • Analgesia for bone pain (WHO ladder, often requires opiates)
    • Bisphosphonates (e.g., Zoledronic acid) for bone protection & hypercalcemia
    • Management of renal impairment (hydration, avoid nephrotoxins like NSAIDs)
    • Management of anemia (blood transfusion, consider EPO)
    • Infection prophylaxis (vaccinations [no live vaccines], consider prophylactic antibiotics)
    • VTE prophylaxis (essential with certain treatments like IMiDs)
  • Consent
  • Non-Medical:
    • Psychological support
    • Nutritional support / Dietitian input
    • Social Prescribing (referral to link workers, support groups)
  • Surgical
    • Vertebroplasty/Kyphoplasty for vertebral compression fractures
    • Orthopaedic fixation for pathological fractures
    • Autologous Stem Cell Transplant (ASCT) for eligible patients after induction

Red flags Warning Signs

  • Not improve :
    •  New or worsening severe back pain, leg weakness, numbness, or bladder/bowel changes (Spinal Cord Compression)
  • Cx of Dz
    • Drowsiness, confusion, severe thirst, or constipation (Hypercalcemia)
    • Fever, chills, or any sign of serious infection (Neutropenic Sepsis)
  • Emergency Contacts:
