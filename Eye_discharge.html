1ST COMPLAINT: EYE DISCHARGE

  • S: Site & Laterality "Is the discharge coming from one eye or both?"
    • UNILATERAL (One Eye): Blocked Tear Duct (Dacrostenosis), Foreign Body, Cellulitis
    • BILATERAL (Both Eyes): Viral or Allergic Conjunctivitis is common. Can be Bacterial.
  • O: Onset "When did you first notice the discharge? And how old is your child?"
    • Age of Onset is Critical:
      • Within the first month of life -> Ophthalmia Neonatorum (Red Flag)
      • Within the first few months -> Congenital Blocked Tear Duct
    • Triggered by sick contacts (daycare, school), recent cold, or new environmental exposure?
  • D: Duration "How many days has this been happening?"
  • A/R: Aggravating & Relieving Factors "Does anything seem to make it worse, like rubbing the eyes? Does wiping it away help for a while?"
  • C: Character "Can you describe the discharge? What color is it, and is it watery, sticky, or stringy?"
    • NATURE OF DISCHARGE
      • Watery (clear, tearing): Viral Conjunctivitis, Allergic Conjunctivitis, Blocked Tear Duct
      • Purulent (pus-like, thick, yellow/green): Bacterial Conjunctivitis
      • Mucoid (stringy, white, ropey): Hallmark of Allergic Conjunctivitis
    • ASSOCIATED EYE SYMPTOMS
      • Itching / Eye Rubbing: Dominant feature of Allergic Conjunctivitis
      • Redness (Conjunctival Injection): Is it mild (pink) or fiery red?
      • Eyelid Matting/Sticking: Especially upon waking. Classic for Bacterial Conjunctivitis.
  • R: Radiation (of Symptoms) "Is there any redness or swelling on the eyelids or around the eye itself?"
    • Swelling/redness of the eyelids is a red flag for periorbital cellulitis.
  • A: Associated Symptoms "Besides the eye issue, has your child had any other symptoms like a fever, cough, runny nose, or ear pain?"
    • Upper Respiratory Symptoms (Cough, Coryza): Strongly suggests Viral Conjunctivitis (adenovirus).
    • Ear Pain / Ear Infection: Conjunctivitis-Otitis Syndrome (often H. influenzae).
    • Fever: Suggests a systemic infection.
    • History of Atopy (Eczema, Asthma, Allergic Rhinitis): Increases suspicion for Allergic Conjunctivitis.
    • Vesicles (blisters) on the skin around the eye or face? -> Concern for Herpes (HSV/VZV)
  • P: Progression "Has it gotten worse since it started? Did it begin in one eye and then spread to the other?"
    • Progression from one eye to the other is classic for viral conjunctivitis.
  • P: Pattern "Is it worse at a particular time of day, like in the morning?"
  • E: Effect on Life "How is your child doing overall? Are they irritable, feeding and playing normally? Have they been sent home from daycare?"
  • R: Red Flags "I just need to ask some specific questions to make sure we're not missing anything serious."
    • SIGNIFICANT PAIN or PHOTOPHOBIA (light sensitivity).
    • CHANGE IN VISION (as reported by parent or older child).
    • Swelling, redness, and tenderness of the Eyelids (Periorbital/Orbital Cellulitis).
    • A cloudy cornea or abnormally shaped pupil.
    • ANY purulent discharge in the first month of life (Ophthalmia Neonatorum).
    • Blisters on the skin near the eye (Herpes).
  • S: Severity / Bother Scale "On a scale of 1 to 10, how much does this seem to be bothering your child?"
