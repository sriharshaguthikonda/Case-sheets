• S: Site/Sensory Modality (for Hallucinations) - Auditory (voices, sounds), Visual (formed, unformed), Olfactory, Tactile (e.g., formication), Gustatory?
  • O: Onset - When first experienced? Sudden or gradual? Context?
  • D: Duration - How long do they last? How long has the problem been present?
  • A/R: Aggravating/Relieving Factors
    1. Aggravated by: Stress? Isolation? Substance use? Specific times?
    2. Relieved by: Distraction? Medication? Social interaction? Reality testing?

  • C: Character/Type
    1. Hallucinations:
      • Auditory: Voices (1st/2nd/3rd person), running commentary, command hallucinations, derogatory, content? Other sounds?
      • Visual: Formed (people, objects) or unformed (flashes, patterns)?
      • Olfactory/Gustatory: Pleasant/unpleasant smells/tastes?
      • Tactile: Sensation of being touched, things crawling on/under skin?
    2. Delusions (Fixed, false beliefs, not culturally sanctioned):
      • Persecutory (most common)?
      • Grandiose?
      • Referential (ideas of reference)?
      • Somatic?
      • Religious?
      • Nihilistic?
      • Erotomanic?
      • Thought insertion/withdrawal/broadcasting?
      • Delusions of control?
      • Bizarreness of delusion? Systematized?
  • R: Radiation - Not applicable.
  • A: Associated Symptoms
    1. Disorganized speech (loose associations, tangentiality, word salad)?
    2. Disorganized or catatonic behavior?
    3. Negative symptoms (affective flattening, alogia, avolition)?
    4. Mood changes (depression, mania, anxiety)?
    5. Sleep/appetite disturbances?
    6. Social withdrawal?
    7. Impairment in functioning?
    8. Lack of insight?
  • P: Pattern (Timing/Frequency) - How often? Constant or intermittent?
  • P: Progression - Worsening, improving, stable?
  • E: Effect on life - Impact on reality testing, behavior, relationships, self-care, safety.
  • S: Severity/Scale - Degree of conviction in delusions, intrusiveness/distress from hallucinations, functional impairment, risk.
