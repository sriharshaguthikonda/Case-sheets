    • How long have you had the fever?
  4. ASSOCIATED SYMPTOMS
    • Chills or rigors
    • Night sweats
    • Headache
    • Body aches or myalgia
    • Fatigue or weakness
    • Loss of appetite
    • Nausea or vomiting
    • Diarrhea or constipation
    • Cough or difficulty breathing
    • Rash or skin changes
    • Sore throat
    • Joint pain or swelling
  5. CHARACTER
    • Continuous
    • Intermittent
    • Remittent
    • Hectic (wide swings)
    • Relapsing (periods of fever separated by normal temperatures)
  6. RADIATION
    • Does the fever seem to spread to any particular area?
    • Are there symptoms in other body systems?
  7. AGGRAVATING FACTORS
    • Time of day (e.g., evening rise)
    • Activity level
    • Environmental temperature
    • Medication non-compliance
  8. RELIEVING FACTORS
    • Rest
    • Medications (e.g., paracetamol, NSAIDs)
    • Cooling measures
    • Fluid intake
  9. PROGRESSION
    • How has the fever changed since it started?
    • Are symptoms getting better, worse, or staying the same?
    • Have there been any new symptoms?

  11. RED FLAGS
    • Temperature > 40°C (104°F)
    • Altered mental status
    • Neck stiffness
    • Petechial/purpuric rash
    • Severe headache
    • Seizures
    • Hypotension
    • Respiratory distress
    • Severe abdominal pain
    • Immunocompromised state
  12. SEVERITY
    • Low grade
    • High grade
    • Highest recorded temperature
    • Impact on daily activities
    • Need for medical attention
