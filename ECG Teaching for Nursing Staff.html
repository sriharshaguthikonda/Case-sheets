B. BASIC CARDIAC PHYSIOLOGY & ECG WAVES (Video 2:02)

  • Brief Heart Anatomy & Blood Flow (Optional, if learner is unfamiliar): (Video 2:02-2:27)
    • Four chambers: two atria (upper), two ventricles (bottom).
    • Simplified blood flow: Deoxygenated blood to RA -> RV -> Lungs (purification) -> LA -> LV -> Aorta -> Body.
  • Utilizing an ECG Strip for Explanation: It's helpful to use an actual ECG strip. (Video 2:36-2:42)
  • ECG Waveforms: (Video 2:45)
    • P Wave: Represents atrial depolarization (atrial contraction). (Video 2:52-3:00)
    • PR Interval: (Video 3:03)
      • Measures from the beginning of the P wave to the beginning of the QRS complex. (Video 3:06-3:19)
      • Represents the time taken for electrical activity to travel from atria to ventricles.
      • Normal duration: Should be less than 0.2 seconds (or 5 small squares). (Video 3:19-3:29)
    • QRS Complex: Represents ventricular depolarization (ventricular contraction). (Video 3:31-3:38)
    • T Wave: Represents ventricular repolarization (ventricular relaxation). (Video 3:38-3:46)
    • Atrial Repolarization: Typically not visible as it's masked by the larger QRS complex. (Video 3:50-4:00)
    • ST Segment: The segment between the end of the QRS complex and the beginning of the T wave. (Video 4:17-4:19)

C. ECG PAPER & RATE CALCULATION (Video 4:22)

  • ECG Paper Speed: Standard speed is 25 mm/second. (Video 4:22-4:27)
  • ECG Grid: (Video 4:45)
    • One big box contains 5 small boxes.
    • At 25 mm/sec: 1 small box = 0.04 seconds; 1 big box = 0.2 seconds. (Video 4:55-5:01)
    • Therefore, 5 big boxes = 1 second. (Video 5:01-5:08)
    • And 300 big boxes = 60 seconds (1 minute). (Video 5:08-5:10)
  • Calculating Heart Rate: (Video 4:58)
    • For Regular Rhythms: (Video 5:07-5:12)
      • Method: 300 divided by the number of big boxes between two consecutive R waves (RR interval). (Video 5:18-5:25)
      • Example: If RR interval is 4 big boxes, Heart Rate = 300 / 4 = 75 bpm. (Video 5:25-5:30 (example given with 4 boxes at 6:16))
    • For Irregular Rhythms: (Video 6:25-6:27)
      • Method: Count the number of QRS complexes in 30 big boxes (which represents 6 seconds). (Video 6:27-6:50)
      • Multiply this number by 10 to get the approximate beats per minute. (Video 6:55-7:06 (example counting 30 boxes and QRS complexes))
      • Example: If 8 QRS complexes in 30 big boxes, Heart Rate = 8 x 10 = 80 bpm. (Video 7:01-7:21 (demonstration leading to 8 QRS) -> Video 7:38-7:49 (calculation))

D. SIX KEY POINTS FOR BASIC ECG INTERPRETATION (Video 8:03-8:08)

  1. Each P wave should be followed by a QRS complex. (Video 8:25-8:35)
  2. PR interval should be less than 0.2 seconds (less than 1 big box). If longer, it might indicate a heart block. (Video 8:40-8:50)
  3. QRS complex duration should be less than 0.12 seconds (less than 3 small boxes). If wider, it could suggest bundle branch block or ventricular tachycardia. (Video 8:52-9:10)
  4. QRS complexes should be equidistant (regular rhythm). If not, the rhythm is irregular. (Video 9:08-9:19)
  5. T wave should be upright and rounded (in most leads). (Video 9:22-9:25)
  6. Rate should be between 60-100 bpm. Less than 60 is bradycardia; more than 100 is tachycardia. (Video 9:25-9:37)

Focus on these basics for teaching nursing staff; avoid overly complex interpretations like specific bundle branch blocks unless necessary. (Video 9:39-9:50)

E. BRIEF IDENTIFICATION OF ABNORMAL ECGs (Examples shown) (Video 9:53)

  • Ventricular Tachycardia (VT): Wide QRS complexes, fast rate. (QRS > 0.12s). (Video 10:00-10:29)
  • ST-Elevation Myocardial Infarction (STEMI): ST segment elevation. (Video 10:41-10:50)
  • Atrial Fibrillation (AF): Absent P waves, irregularly irregular ventricular rhythm. (Video 11:06-11:28)
  • Atrial Flutter: "Sawtooth" appearance of flutter waves. (Video 11:32-11:41)

Recap the key teaching points: wave identification, rate calculation (regular/irregular), and the six essential interpretation points. (Video 11:54-12:32)
