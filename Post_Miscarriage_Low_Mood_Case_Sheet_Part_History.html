PRIMARY COMPLAINT – LOW MOOD AFTER EARLY MISCARRIAGE

  1. S Site / Situation “Can you describe in which situations the low mood feels worst?”
  2. O Onset “When did you first notice feeling down?”
    •  Acute (< 2 weeks) 
    •  Sub‑acute (2‑4 weeks) 
    •  Chronic (> 4 weeks) 
  3. D Duration / Daily Variation “Is it there all day or does it vary?”
  4. A Aggravating Factors “Is there anything that tends to make you feel worse?”
  5. R Relieving Factors “Anything that lifts your mood, even briefly?”
  6. C Character “How would you describe the feeling – sadness, emptiness, irritability?”
  7. R Radiation (Social Spread) “Has this feeling affected other areas of your life – work, relationships?”
  8. A Associated Symptoms (SIGECAPS)
    • Sleep – insomnia / hypersomnia?
    • Interest – loss of pleasure?
    • Guilt / worthlessness?
    • Energy – fatigued?
    • Concentration – poor focus?
    • Appetite / weight change?
    • Psychomotor – slowed / agitated?
    • Suicidal thoughts or self‑harm?
  9. P Progression / Pattern “Is it getting better, worse or staying the same?”
  10. E Effect on Life & Function “How is this affecting your everyday life?”
  11. R Red‑flags / Risk
    • Thoughts of suicide or self‑harm
    • Harm to others (rare but check)
    • Neglect of self‑care
  12. S Severity / Scale 0‑10 “On a scale of 0 to 10, how low do you feel at your worst?”

SECONDARY COMPLAINT – EARLY MISCARRIAGE DETAILS

  1. GTPAL history “Can you tell me about previous pregnancies or losses?”
  2. Bleeding pattern & pain “How heavy was the bleeding? Any cramps?”
  3. Management received Expectant / medical / surgical?
  4. Complications Infection, ongoing bleeding?

PAST PSYCHIATRIC HISTORY

  • Previous episodes of depression / anxiety / other mental illness
  • Any past self‑harm or suicide attempts
  • Previous treatments – therapy, medication, hospital admissions

PAST MEDICAL & SURGICAL HISTORY

  • Chronic illnesses (thyroid, diabetes, chronic pain, etc.)
  • Allergies – drugs / latex / food
  • Operations / hospitalisations

MEDICATION HISTORY

  • Current prescribed & over‑the‑counter meds
  • Herbal / supplements
  • Recreational substance use (alcohol, drugs)

FAMILY HISTORY

  • Mental health disorders in first‑degree relatives
  • Miscarriages / fertility issues in close relatives

SOCIAL HISTORY

  • Living situation & support network
  • Relationship with partner after miscarriage
  • Occupation – days missed, occupational stressors
  • Alcohol – units/week
  • Smoking & substances
  • Financial or housing worries

ICE – IDEAS, CONCERNS, EXPECTATIONS

  • Ideas: “Why do you think you’re feeling this way?”
  • Concerns: Fear of recurrence, guilt, feeling broken
  • Expectations: Emotional support, information on recurrence risk, counselling

SYSTEMS REVIEW (targeted)

  • Thyroid – weight change, intolerance to heat/cold
  • Cardiovascular – palpitations, chest discomfort
  • Respiratory – breathlessness
  • Genitourinary – ongoing vaginal bleeding, discharge, pelvic pain
  • Fever, infection symptoms

Management Plan Overview

  1. Risk Assessment & Immediate Safety
    • Screen for suicidal or self‑harm thoughts (Ask directly, document response).
    • If risk > moderate: Same‑day senior review & urgent mental‑health referral (CRHTT).
  2. Diagnostic Confirmation
    • Use PHQ‑9 & GAD‑7 to quantify mood/anxiety baseline.
    • Differentiate normal grief vs adjustment disorder vs major depression.
  3. Baseline Medical Checks
    • TSH, T4 (thyroid dysfunction can mimic low mood).
    • FBC (anaemia), ferritin, B12, folate.
    • CRP if ongoing bleeding/infection suspicion.
  4. Post‑miscarriage After‑care
    • Confirm miscarriage process complete (scan/hCG if indicated).
    • Check Rhesus status; give anti‑D if Rhesus‑negative & gestation ≥ 12 weeks or significant bleeding.
    • Arrange Early Pregnancy Assessment Unit (EPAU) follow‑up if complications.
  5. Psychological Interventions (NICE NG222 §1.5)
    • Offer guided self‑help or low‑intensity CBT via **NHS Talking Therapies (IAPT)**.
    • Bereavement counselling / Miscarriage Association support groups.
    • Consider couple’s counselling to address shared grief.
  6. Pharmacological Options
    • If PHQ‑9 ≥ 10 or functional impairment persists > 2–4 weeks, discuss SSRI (e.g. sertraline first‑line) per NICE NG222 algorithm; review risks, benefits, pregnancy planning.
    • Initial review 2 weeks after starting; monitor for increased agitation or suicidality (particularly in first 2 weeks).
  7. Lifestyle & Social Prescribing
    • Gradual return‑to‑work plan; Med 3 fit‑note if absence > 7 days.
    • Sleep hygiene, regular meals, graded activity scheduling.
    • Signpost local leisure centre ‘exercise‑on‑prescription’ scheme.
  8. Safety‑Netting & Red Flags
    • Seek urgent help via 999/A&E or crisis line if suicidal thoughts escalate, intent forms, or hallucinations develop.
    • Return sooner if severe bleeding, abdominal pain, fever, or foul discharge (complications of miscarriage).
  9. Follow‑up
    • GP review in 2 weeks (earlier if risk identified).
    • Repeat PHQ‑9 at 4–6 weeks to gauge response.
    • If no improvement by 6–8 weeks → escalate to secondary‑care perinatal/CMHT.
  10. Documentation
    • Record ICE, risk assessment, score sheets, referrals, safety‑net advice.
  11. Teach‑back
    • Ask patient to summarise: cause, management steps, follow‑up and red‑flags.
