    • Place of injury:

  1. SITE (S):
    1. Which knee is painful (Right/Left/Both)?
    2. Can you point with one finger to the exact spot of maximum pain?
    3. Is the pain felt:
      1. Anteriorly (front of the knee, e.g., over the patella, patellar tendon)?
      2. Medially (inner side of the knee)?
      3. Laterally (outer side of the knee)?
      4. Posteriorly (back of the knee)?
      5. Deep inside the joint?
    4. Is it a localized pain or more diffuse around the knee?
  2. ONSET (O):
    1. When did the knee pain first start? (Specific date/time if possible)
    2. Was the onset sudden (e.g., after a specific injury, twist, fall, impact) or gradual (insidious)?
    3. If sudden, describe the mechanism of injury:
      1. What were you doing? (e.g., sports, walking, climbing stairs)
      2. Did you hear or feel a "pop," "snap," or "tear"?
      3. Was there immediate swelling?
      4. Could you continue the activity or bear weight immediately after?
    4. If gradual, has it been slowly worsening over time?
    5. Any preceding illness, fever, or new activities before the onset?
  3. DURATION (D):
    1. How long have you been experiencing this knee pain? (Days, weeks, months, years)
    2. Is the pain constant or does it come and go (intermittent)?
    3. If intermittent, how long does each episode typically last, and how frequent are they?
  4. AGGRAVATING/RELIEVING FACTORS (A/R):
    1. Aggravating Factors: What makes the pain worse?
      1. Weight-bearing activities (walking, standing, running)?
      2. Stairs (going up or down)? Which is worse?
      3. Squatting or kneeling?
      4. Twisting or pivoting movements?
      5. Prolonged sitting ("movie-goer's sign" or "theatre sign")?
      6. Rising from a seated position?
      7. Specific movements like bending or straightening the knee?
      8. At night or when resting?
    2. Relieving Factors: What makes the pain better?
      1. Rest?
      2. Pain medications (OTC or prescribed)? Which ones and how effective?
      3. Ice or heat application?
      4. Bracing or supports?
      5. Specific positions (e.g., keeping the knee straight or bent)?
      6. Activity modification?
  5. CHARACTER/TYPE (C):
    1. How would you describe the pain?
      1. Sharp, stabbing?
      2. Dull, aching, throbbing?
      3. Burning?
      4. Grinding or grating sensation?
      5. Deep boring pain?
  6. RADIATION (R):
    1. Does the pain spread or radiate anywhere else? (e.g., up the thigh, down the shin/calf, into the foot, to the hip or ankle)
  7. ASSOCIATED SYMPTOMS (A):
    1. Swelling in or around the knee? (Timing: immediate or delayed after injury? Constant or intermittent?)
    2. Stiffness in the knee? (Especially in the morning or after inactivity? How long does it last?)
    3. Locking of the knee (unable to bend or straighten it)?
    4. Catching or a sensation of something getting stuck in the knee?
    5. Giving way or instability (feeling like the knee will buckle)?
    6. Clicking, popping, or grinding (crepitus) with movement?
    7. Reduced range of motion (difficulty bending or straightening fully)?
    8. Redness or warmth over the knee?
    9. Bruising around the knee?
    10. Fever, chills, general malaise (suggests infection)?
    11. Numbness or tingling in the leg or foot?
    12. Weakness in the leg muscles?
    13. Pain in other joints?
  8. PROGRESSION (P):
    1. How has the pain and any associated symptoms changed since they first started? (Getting worse, better, staying the same?)
    2. Has the pattern of pain or functional limitation changed?
    3. If treatment was received, how did it respond? Any changes since then?
  9. PATTERN (P):
    1. Is the pain continuous or does it occur in waves/episodes?
    2. Any specific times of day when it's worse or better (e.g., morning stiffness, night pain)?
    3. Relationship to activity (e.g., pain during, after, or unrelated to activity)?
  10. EFFECT ON LIFE (E):
    1. How is the knee pain affecting your daily activities (walking, climbing stairs, dressing, bathing)?
    2. Has it affected your ability to work, participate in sports, or hobbies?
    3. Does it disturb your sleep?
    4. Has it affected your mood or independence?
  11. RED FLAGS (R):
    1. Signs of Infection (Septic Arthritis):
      1. Acute onset of severe pain, swelling, redness, warmth.
      2. Fever, chills, malaise.
      3. Inability to bear weight or move the joint.
    2. Signs of Fracture or significant acute injury:
      1. History of significant trauma.
      2. Immediate severe pain and inability to bear weight.
      3. Obvious deformity.
      4. Rapid, significant swelling (hemarthrosis).
    3. Signs of Tumor (rare):
      1. Persistent night pain unrelated to movement.
      2. Unexplained weight loss.
      3. Palpable mass.
      4. History of cancer.
    4. Neurovascular compromise:
      1. Numbness, tingling, weakness in the lower leg/foot.
      2. Cold, pale, or pulseless foot (especially after trauma/dislocation).
    5. Locked knee (true mechanical block to extension).
  12. SCALE (S) (Severity):
    1. On a scale of 0 to 10, with 0 being no pain and 10 being the worst pain imaginable, how would you rate your knee pain:
      1. Right now?
      2. At its worst?
      3. At its best?
      4. On average?
      5. During specific activities (e.g., walking, stairs)?
