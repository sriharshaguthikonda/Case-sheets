Systems Review / DD Checklist:

  • Psychiatric: Depression, Anxiety
  • Endocrine: Hypothyroidism, Diabetes, Adrenal insufficiency
  • Haematology: Anemia, Malignancy
  • Cardio-Respiratory: Heart failure, COPD
  • Infectious: Chronic infection (e.g., EBV), Post-viral fatigue
  • Oncology: Any
  • Genito-Urinary
  • Neuromuscular
  • Musculoskeletal: Fibromyalgia, Chronic Fatigue Syndrome (ME/CFS)
  • Gastrointestinal: Malabsorption (e.g., Celiac disease)
    • S: Severity & Sleep Quality
    • SLEEP HYGIENE:
      • How many hours do you sleep? Is it restorative (do you feel fresh upon waking)?
      • Do you have trouble falling asleep (onset insomnia) or staying asleep (maintenance)?
      • Snoring / Apnea: Do you snore or stop breathing? (Partner's report).
    • FUNCTIONAL SEVERITY:
      • "Can you do your normal daily activities, or do you have to stop and rest?"
    "How are you sleeping at night? Even after a full night's sleep, do you still wake up feeling exhausted?"
    O: Onset
    • SUDDEN:
      • Post-viral (e.g., Flu, COVID, EBV).
      • Acute life stressor / Grief.
      • Cardiac event.
    • GRADUAL:
      • Hypothyroidism, Diabetes.
      • Anemia.
      • Malignancy.
    • NEW MEDICATION? (Beta-blockers, Antihistamines, Antidepressants, Sedatives).
    "When did you start feeling this way? Did it start after a specific illness, or did it creep up on you slowly?"
    D: Duration
    • Acute (< 1 month) vs. Prolonged (1-6 months) vs. Chronic (> 6 months e.g., CFS).
    "How long has this been going on?"
    A/R: Aggravating & Relieving Factors
    • AGGRAVATING:
      • Mental effort ("Brain Fog").
      • Post-Exertional Malaise: Severe crash after minor physical activity (Classic ME/CFS).
      • Heavy meals (Post-prandial somnolence - Insulin resistance).
    • RELIEVING:
      • Does rest actually help? (In organic disease/cancer, rest often doesn't help).
    "Does resting make you feel better? How do you feel after you try to exercise?"
    C: Character / Type
    • TRUE FATIGUE: "Heavy limbs," "No energy," "Washed out."
    • SOMNOLENCE (Sleepiness): "Fighting to stay awake," falling asleep driving/reading. (Suggests Sleep Apnea/Narcolepsy).
    • DYSPNEA (Breathlessness): "I get tired walking up stairs" = might actually be air hunger. (Suggests Heart/Lung issue).
    • WEAKNESS: "I can't lift my arms." (Suggests Neuromuscular issue).
    "Is it that you feel sleepy and might doze off, or is it that your body feels heavy and weak? Or do you feel out of breath?"
    R: Systemic Review (Search for Cause)
    "Fatigue can be caused by many things, so I need to ask a broad range of questions."
    A: Associated Symptoms
    • CONSTITUTIONAL: Fever, Night sweats, Weight loss.
    • HEMATOLOGY (Anemia/Malignancy):
      • Easy bruising, bleeding gums, menorrhagia (heavy periods).
    • ENDOCRINE:
      • Thyroid: Feeling cold, hair loss, dry skin, constipation.
      • Diabetes: Polyuria (lots of urine), Polydipsia (thirst).
      • Addison's: Dizziness on standing, skin pigmentation.
    • PSYCHIATRIC:
      • Low mood, anhedonia (loss of pleasure), anxiety.
    • RESPIRATORY / SLEEP:
      • Snoring, witnessed apneas (stopping breathing).
      • Cough, shortness of breath.
    • GASTROINTESTINAL / LIVER:
      • Change in bowel habits (Celiac, IBD, Malignancy).
      • Black/Tarry stools (Melena - slow bleed).
      • Alcohol intake? (cirrhosis)
      • History of hepatitis B/C?
      • Early satiety, LUQ discomfort? (splenomegaly)
    • INFECTIOUS:
      • Lymph node swelling, sore throat (EBV/HIV).
      • Travel to tropics? (malaria, visceral leishmaniasis, schistosomiasis)
      • TB exposure / BCG status?
    • HAEMOLYTIC / SPLENIC:
      • Jaundice + dark urine + pallor? (haemolytic anaemia)
      • Family history of splenectomy / gallstones young age? (hereditary spherocytosis)
      • Ethnic origin - Mediterranean, African, Ashkenazi Jewish? (thalassaemia, sickle cell, Gaucher)
    "Have you noticed any change in your weight, your bowel movements, or your periods? Do you feel cold when others are warm?"
    P: Progression
    "Is the tiredness getting worse, better, or staying the same?"
    P: Pattern
    • Morning: Wakes up tired? (Sleep Apnea, Depression).
    • Evening: Worse as day goes on? (Myasthenia Gravis).
    • Fluctuating: Good days and bad days? (Autoimmune/CFS).
    "Is there a specific time of day when it hits you the hardest?"
    E: Effect on Life
    • Impact on work/driving safety.
    • Social isolation.
    "Are you safe to drive? How is this affecting your work and social life?"
    R: Red Flags
    • WEIGHT LOSS / LUMPS: Malignancy check.
    • BONE MARROW FAILURE: Fatigue + Bruising/Bleeding + Recurrent Infections (Leukemia).
    • GI BLEEDING: Black tarry stools (Melena) or vomiting blood.
    • SEVERE SLEEP APNEA: Falling asleep continuously, risk of accidents (driving/machinery).
    • NEUROLOGICAL DEFICIT: Slurred speech, weakness, balance issues (MS, Stroke, Space Occupying Lesion).
    • SIADH / Hyponatremia: Confusion + Fatigue (Lung Ca).
    • CHEST PAIN / EXERTIONAL DYSPNEA: Heart Failure or Ischemia.
    "I need to ensure there isn't something serious underlying this. Have you lost weight without trying? Have you noticed any unusual bleeding or lumps?"
    S: Scale
    • 0-10 Severity Scale.
    • Epworth Sleepiness Scale (for sleep apnea).
    "On a scale of 0 to 10, how severe is this fatigue?"
