MANAGEMENT PLAN – Suspected Encephalitis / Meningoencephalitis

OBO Urgency / Disposition

  • Immediate escalation to resus/AMU; A–E, oxygen if hypoxic, IV access, capillary glucose. ABN/BIA algorithm
  • If delay > 6h to LP/imaging or patient very unwell: start IV aciclovir 10 mg/kg 8-hourly now (adjust for eGFR) + robust IV hydration; monitor U&Es for crystal nephropathy. ABN/BIA dosing Review
  • If bacterial meningitis not excluded: give IV ceftriaxone (± ampicillin if >50 y/immunocompromised), follow NICE NG240.
  • Early senior/ICU, Neurology & Infectious Diseases input.

Investigations (initial)

  • Bloods: FBC, U&E, LFT, CRP, coag, glucose (paired with CSF), blood cultures; HIV test for all; pregnancy test where applicable. ABN/BIA
  • Swabs: throat/rectal (enterovirus), vesicle if present; respiratory sample if symptoms. Travel screen as indicated (malaria films/antigen). ABN/BIA

Lumbar Puncture (LP)

  • Do LP urgently unless contraindications to immediate LP → then CT first. NICE QS19
  • Contraindications prompting neuroimaging: reduced/fluctuating GCS <13, focal neurology, papilloedema, post-seizure until stabilised, immunocompromise, coagulopathy/anticoagulation, suspected raised ICP, etc. ABN/BIA table
  • CSF set: opening pressure; cell count + differential; protein; CSF/serum glucose (paired); MC&S; HSV & VZV PCR (first LP); consider lactate, oligoclonal bands. Save extra CSF. ABN/BIA

Imaging & EEG

  • MRI brain within 24–48 h (more sensitive) ± CT earlier if indicated pre-LP. ABN/BIA
  • EEG if diagnostic uncertainty, to detect non-convulsive status, or if psychiatric presentation. ABN/BIA

Empiric & Targeted Therapy

  • IV Aciclovir 10 mg/kg 8-hourly (≥12 y), 500 mg/m² 8-hourly (3 m–12 y), 20 mg/kg 8-hourly (neonate–3 m). Typical duration 14 days; use 21 days if immunocompromised or 3 m–12 y. Consider repeat LP at day 14; continue 7 days more if CSF PCR still positive. ABN/BIA dosing box
  • Hydrate well; check U&E/creatinine daily; dose-adjust in renal impairment. Review (Ellul 2018)
  • If bacterial meningitis not excluded: give ceftriaxone (± ampicillin). Reassess once CSF available. NICE NG240

Supportive / Complication Management

  • Seizures/status: benzodiazepines → levetiracetam/second line; manage airway/ventilation; escalate to ICU if refractory. Review
  • Fever control, euvolaemia, normoglycaemia, DVT prophylaxis, swallow & nutrition assessment, pressure care, therapy (PT/OT/SLT) and early rehab planning.
  • Raised ICP risk: head-up, avoid hypotension/hypercapnia; neuro-ICU liaison.

Aetiology Expansion / Public Health

  • HIV test all; travel/arbovirus risks; malaria tests if indicated; notify CCDC where required. ABN/BIA
  • If infectious work-up negative or limbic/psychiatric features: send autoimmune encephalitis panel; consider steroids/IVIG/PLEX in line with specialist advice after infection reasonably excluded. Graus 2016

Communication, Capacity, Safeguarding

  • Capacity check if fluctuating cognition; involve family/carers (with consent); document best-interest decisions if needed (GMC).
  • Safeguarding triggers (vulnerable adult/child) as appropriate.

Safety-Netting / Discharge & Follow-up

  • Clear red flags: fever/behavioural change, new seizures, worsening headache, photophobia, focal deficits, drowsiness, rash. Provide written info & rehab/contact numbers. NHS
  • Neurology clinic; cognitive & mood screening; DVLA advice if seizures; community therapy input.
