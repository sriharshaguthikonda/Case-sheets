Generic Surgical / Pre-Surgical Counselling – Case Sheet Template

Pre-operative assessment & counselling

  • Full history – symptoms
    • Clarify indication for surgery
      • Working diagnosis
      • Severity
      • Functional limitation
      • Any red-flag features
    • Character of main symptom (SODA-style)
      • Red-flag symptoms
        • Weight loss
        • Night pain
        • Systemic features
        • Neurological deficit (if relevant)
    • Previous non-surgical management
      • Analgesics / other medicines tried
      • Physiotherapy
      • Injections / procedures
      • Previous surgery for same problem
  • Background & comorbidities (PMAFTOSA style)
    • Past medical history – system based
      • Cardiovascular
        • Ischaemic heart disease
        • Heart failure
        • Arrhythmias
        • Hypertension
      • Respiratory
        • Asthma
        • COPD
        • Obstructive sleep apnoea
      • Endocrine
        • Diabetes
        • Thyroid disease
      • Renal
        • Chronic kidney disease
      • Neurological
        • Stroke / TIA
        • Epilepsy
      • Others
        • Chronic liver disease
        • Frailty
    • Past surgical & anaesthetic history
      • Previous operations – type and date
      • Any problems with anaesthetic
        • Difficult intubation
        • Prolonged ventilation / ICU
        • Malignant hyperthermia history
        • Severe postoperative nausea / vomiting
    • Drug history
      • Anticoagulants / antiplatelets
        • Warfarin
        • DOACs
        • Aspirin / clopidogrel / others
      • Diabetes medicines
        • Insulin
        • Tablets
      • Steroids
      • Immunosuppressants
      • Regular analgesia / opioids
      • Nephrotoxic medicines (e.g. NSAIDs)
    • Allergies / adverse reactions
      • Drug allergies
        • Agent
        • Type of reaction (rash / swelling / anaphylaxis)
      • Latex
      • Iodine / contrast
      • Chlorhexidine
    • Lifestyle
      • Smoking
        • Current / ex / never
        • Pack years (if possible)
      • Alcohol
        • Units per week
        • Binge drinking
      • Recreational drugs
      • Exercise tolerance
        • Can climb 1–2 flights of stairs?
        • > 4 METs or < 4 METs
      • Diet and weight
        • Recent weight loss / gain
      • Sleep
        • Snoring
        • Witnessed apnoeas
        • Daytime somnolence
    • Family history
      • Anaesthetic complications
      • Malignant hyperthermia
      • Bleeding / clotting disorders
      • Early cardiac deaths
    • Social & support
      • Who lives with patient
      • Carer availability
      • Stairs at home
      • Bathroom / toilet access
      • Work and benefits
      • Caring responsibilities (children / dependants)
  • Pre-operative examination
    • Observations (NEWS2)
      • Blood pressure
      • Heart rate
      • Respiratory rate
      • Temperature
      • Oxygen saturations
      • Capillary blood glucose (if relevant)
    • General assessment
      • Height
      • Weight
      • BMI
      • Frailty / mobility baseline
    • Respiratory examination
      • Inspection
      • Chest expansion
      • Percussion
      • Auscultation – wheeze / crackles
    • Cardiovascular examination
      • JVP
      • Heart sounds
      • Peripheral oedema
      • Peripheral pulses
    • Airway / oral cavity
      • Mouth opening
      • Dentition / dentures
      • Mallampati (if appropriate)
      • Neck movements
      • Neck masses / goitre
    • Local examination of operative site
      • Inspection
      • Scars
      • Swelling / deformity
      • Range of movement
      • Neurovascular status
  • Baseline investigations (NICE NG45)
    • Blood tests
      • Full blood count
      • Urea & electrolytes
      • Liver function tests (if indicated)
      • Clotting screen (if indicated)
      • Group & save / crossmatch (if risk of bleeding)
    • Diabetes / endocrine
      • HbA1c (if diabetic)
      • Thyroid function (if indicated)
    • ECG (if indicated by age / comorbidity / surgery grade)
    • Chest X-ray (only if specific indication)
    • Other
      • Pregnancy test (women of childbearing potential)
      • Additional tests specific to procedure
  • VTE risk (NICE NG89)
    • VTE risk assessment completed
    • Bleeding risk assessment completed
    • Prophylaxis plan
      • Mechanical
        • Anti-embolism stockings
        • Intermittent pneumatic compression
      • Pharmacological
        • LMWH / other as per protocol
        • Timing with respect to neuraxial anaesthesia
    • Duration of post-op prophylaxis discussed (if major / cancer / pelvic / orthopaedic)
  • Optimisation & peri-operative pathway (NICE NG180)
    • Optimise chronic disease
      • Blood pressure
      • Heart failure control
      • COPD / asthma control
      • Diabetes control
      • Treat anaemia
    • Smoking cessation advice given
    • Alcohol reduction / dependency support considered
    • Nutritional assessment and support if needed
    • Consider need for HDU / ICU / enhanced recovery bed
  • Anaesthetic-specific questions
    • Neck and spine
      • Previous neck injury
      • Restricted neck movement
    • Jaw / mouth
      • Jaw problems (TMJ)
      • Poor mouth opening
    • Dentition
      • Loose teeth
      • Crowns / bridges
      • Dentures / plates
    • Obstructive sleep apnoea features (STOP-BANG)
    • Reflux / hiatus hernia / aspiration risk
    • Previous severe postoperative nausea / vomiting
  • Shared decision making & consent
    • Check understanding so far
      • What have you been told?
      • What is your main concern?
      • What are you hoping this surgery will achieve?
    • Confirm capacity for this decision
    • Explain:
      • What the operation involves (basic steps)
      • Why it is being recommended
      • What happens if we do nothing
      • Reasonable alternatives
        • Conservative / non-surgical options
        • Other procedures (if applicable)
    • Discuss risks (tailored to patient and procedure)
      • Common minor risks
      • Serious but less common risks
      • Specific material risks for this patient
      • General anaesthetic risks, including death (very rare)
    • Use “teach-back”:
      • Ask patient to explain back in their own words
    • Provide written information / leaflets
    • Ensure time to consider before signing consent (if elective)
  • Pre-operative fasting & medicines
    • Fasting instructions
      • No solid food for 6 hours before anaesthetic
      • Clear fluids allowed up to 2 hours before (unless told otherwise)
    • Diabetes management
      • Adjust insulin dose as per local protocol
      • Adjust oral hypoglycaemics
      • Consider variable rate insulin infusion for major surgery
    • Anticoagulants / antiplatelets
      • When to stop before surgery
      • Need for bridging (if any)
    • Which regular medicines to continue on day of surgery
      • Most cardiac medicines
      • Check local policy on ACE-i / ARB

Day of surgery / operative points

  • Admission & final checks
    • Confirm identity
    • Confirm procedure and side / site
    • Check allergies again
    • Check consent form signed and still valid
    • Check observations and blood results
    • Confirm fasting status
    • Site marking done (if side-specific)
  • WHO Surgical Safety Checklist
    • Sign-in (before induction)
    • Time-out (before incision)
    • Sign-out (before leaving theatre)
  • Antibiotic prophylaxis / SSI prevention
    • Correct antibiotic chosen
    • Given at correct time before incision
    • Appropriate skin preparation used
    • Hair removal only if needed and with clippers
    • Normothermia maintained
  • Headline anaesthetic plan (for counselling)
    • Type of anaesthesia
      • General
      • Regional
      • Local with sedation
    • Pain management plan
      • Regional block / epidural / spinal
      • Patient-controlled analgesia
      • Regular oral analgesia
    • Fluids / blood
      • Expected blood loss
      • Transfusion plan if needed

Post-operative care & counselling

  • Immediate post-op (recovery)
    • Monitoring (obs, oxygen, pain score, nausea)
    • Lines and drains explained
      • IV cannulae
      • Catheters
      • Drains
    • Possible drowsiness / confusion after anaesthetic
  • Enhanced recovery – mobilisation & breathing
    • Deep breathing exercises
    • Coughing and supported cough
    • Sitting out of bed early
    • Early mobilisation with physio
    • VTE prophylaxis continued as per protocol
  • Pain, nausea & bowels
    • Pain management
      • Regular simple analgesia
      • Opioids only if required
      • Ask staff if pain not controlled
    • Nausea and vomiting
      • Antiemetics available
      • Seek review if persistent or severe
    • Constipation
      • Caused by opioids and reduced mobility
      • Use laxatives
      • Encourage fluids and mobilisation
  • Wound & infection
    • Wound care
      • How long dressings stay on
      • When they can shower
    • Infection red flags
      • Increasing pain
      • Redness / heat / swelling
      • Discharge or pus
      • Fever or feeling shivery / unwell
    • Drains / catheters / stomas
      • Purpose explained
      • Rough idea how long they stay
  • Post-operative complications – safety net
    • DVT
      • Calf swelling
      • Pain
      • Redness / warmth
    • PE
      • Sudden shortness of breath
      • Chest pain
      • Coughing blood
      • Collapse
    • Cardiac
      • Chest pain
      • Palpitations
      • Syncope
    • Abdominal
      • Persistent vomiting
      • Severe abdominal pain
      • Abdominal swelling / not passing wind
    • Clear instructions:
      • When to call ward / clinic
      • When to call 111 or GP
      • When to call 999 / attend ED
  • Functional recovery & restrictions
    • Movement restrictions (procedure specific)
      • Hip precautions (if relevant)
      • Lifting restrictions after abdominal surgery
    • Timeline expectations
      • Pain improvement
      • Mobility milestones
      • Return to driving
      • Return to work
      • Return to sport
  • Home set-up & support
    • Who is at home to help
    • Stairs / rails / equipment needed
    • Shopping / cooking / personal care cover
    • Community physio / OT / social care involvement if needed
  • Follow-up & documentation
    • Follow-up appointment arranged
      • Clinic date
      • Or telephone review
    • Suture / staple removal plan
    • Discharge summary includes:
      • Operation performed
      • Any intra-operative issues
      • Medication changes
      • VTE prophylaxis plan
      • Restrictions & safety-net advice
