1. SITE (S):
    1. Which ear is discharging (Right/Left/Both)?
  2. ONSET (O):
    1. When did the discharge first start?
    2. Was it sudden or did it begin gradually?
    3. Was it associated with ear pain, an infection (cold/flu, otitis media), swimming, trauma (e.g., Q-tip injury, head injury), or recent ear cleaning?
  3. DURATION (D):
    1. How long has the discharge been present (days, weeks, months)?
    2. Is it constant or intermittent? If intermittent, how often does it occur and for how long?
  4. AGGRAVATING/RELIEVING FACTORS (A/R):
    1. Aggravating Factors: Does anything make the discharge worse or more noticeable?
      1. Water entering the ear (swimming, showering)?
      2. Upper respiratory infections (colds, sinus infections)?
      3. Touching or attempting to clean the ear?
      4. Pressure changes (e.g., flying)?
    2. Relieving Factors: Does anything reduce or stop the discharge?
      1. Keeping the ear dry?
      2. Antibiotic ear drops or oral antibiotics (if used)? How effective?
      3. Specific ear cleaning techniques (if professionally advised)?
  5. CHARACTER/TYPE (C):
    1. What does the discharge look like?
      1. Color: Clear, watery, whitish, yellow, green, brown, bloody (frank blood or blood-tinged)?
      2. Consistency: Thin and watery, thick and mucoid (like mucus), purulent (pus-like), cheesy?
      3. Odor: Is there any foul or offensive smell?
      4. Amount: Scant (just a little moisture), moderate, copious (runs out of the ear, stains pillow)?
      5. Does it contain any debris or flakes?
  6. RADIATION (R): (Not applicable for discharge itself, but associated pain might radiate)
  7. ASSOCIATED SYMPTOMS (A):
    1. Ear pain (otalgia)? (Is the pain relieved, worsened, or unchanged by the onset of discharge?)
    2. Hearing loss or muffled hearing in the affected ear?
    3. Tinnitus (ringing or other noises in the ear)?
    4. Dizziness or vertigo?
    5. Itching in the ear canal or outer ear?
    6. Fever, chills, or general feeling of illness?
    7. Swelling, redness, or tenderness around the ear or in the ear canal?
    8. Pain or tenderness over the mastoid bone (behind the ear)?
    9. History of recurrent ear infections (otitis media, otitis externa)?
    10. History of a perforated eardrum or previous ear surgery (e.g., grommets)?
    11. Symptoms of a current cold, flu, or sinus infection (nasal congestion, sore throat, cough)?
    12. Skin conditions like eczema or psoriasis that might affect the ear canal?
    13. History of head trauma (especially if discharge is clear and watery, or bloody)?
    14. Facial weakness or asymmetry?
  8. PROGRESSION (P):
    1. Has the nature (color, consistency, odor, amount) of the discharge changed since it first started?
    2. Is it getting better, worse, or staying the same over time?
    3. Response to any treatments tried?
  9. PATTERN (P):
    1. Is the discharge continuous or does it occur in episodes?
    2. Is it more noticeable at certain times (e.g., morning, after lying down)?
  10. EFFECT ON LIFE (E):
    1. How bothersome is the ear discharge?
    2. Does it require frequent cleaning or the use of cotton wool/plugs?
    3. Does it cause any social embarrassment due to odor or appearance?
    4. Has it affected your hearing, sleep, or other activities?
    5. Do you have to avoid swimming or getting water in the ear?
  11. RED FLAGS (R):
    1. Foul-smelling, persistent discharge (may indicate cholesteatoma or chronic suppurative otitis media with complications).
    2. Bloody discharge, especially after head trauma or if unilateral and unexplained.
    3. Clear, watery discharge after head trauma (potential Cerebrospinal Fluid - CSF leak â€“ an emergency).
    4. Discharge associated with facial weakness, severe vertigo, or other neurological symptoms.
    5. Persistent discharge despite appropriate antibiotic treatment.
    6. Granulation tissue or a polyp visible in the ear canal.
    7. Discharge in an immunocompromised individual or diabetic, particularly if associated with severe pain or swelling (increased risk of malignant/necrotizing otitis externa).
    8. Significant swelling, redness, and tenderness behind the ear (mastoiditis).
