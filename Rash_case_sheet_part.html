1ST COMPLAINT: RASH

S: Site & Distribution
  • LOCALIZED
    • Fungal (Tinea)
    • Contact Dermatitis
  • GENERALIZED (Widespread)
    • Viral Exanthem (See specific patterns below)
    • Drug Eruption
    • Eczema Flare
  • SPECIFIC DISTRIBUTION PATTERNS
    • Flexures (bends of elbows/knees):
      • Atopic Dermatitis (Eczema)
    • Extensors (outside of elbows/knees):
      • Psoriasis
    • Dermatomal (one nerve strip):
      • Shingles (Herpes Zoster)
    • Face first, then down body (Cephalocaudal):
      • Measles, Rubella
    • Trunk first, then out:
      • Roseola, Varicella
    • "Slapped Cheeks":
      • Fifth Disease
    • Hands, Feet, and Mouth: HFMD
"Where did the rash start, and where has it spread?"
O: Onset
  • ACUTE (Minutes-Hours):
    • Urticaria
    • Contact Dermatitis
  • SUBACUTE (Days):
    • Viral Exanthems
    • Drug Reactions
  • CHRONIC (Weeks-Months):
    • Eczema, Psoriasis
"When did it appear? Suddenly over minutes, or gradually over days?"
D: Duration
"How long do individual spots last? Do they come and go, or stay put?"
A/R: Aggravating & Relieving Factors
"Does anything make it better or worse?"
C: Character (Morphology)
  • MORPHOLOGY
    • Maculopapular (red bumps/spots):
      • Measles, Rubella, Roseola
    • Vesicular (blisters):
      • Varicella, HFMD, Shingles
    • Pustular (pus-filled):
      • Folliculitis, Acne
    • Wheals (hives):
      • Urticaria
    • Lacy/Reticular:
      • Fifth Disease
    • Sandpaper-like:
      • Scarlet Fever
"What do the spots look like? Flat, bumpy, blisters? Are they itchy?"
R: Radiation (Spread)
"We've covered the spread, thank you."
A: Associated Symptoms
  • GENERAL
    • Joint Pain (HSP, Viral Arthritis)
    • New foods, medications, soaps?
  • DEHYDRATION SIGNS – dry nappies, lethargy, sunken eyes.
  • MEDICATION HISTORY (child) – paracetamol? Ibuprofen? steroids? any recent antibiotics.
  • FEVER PROFILE –
    • peak temp,
    • timing in relation to rash,
    • response to paracetamol;
    • high fever preceding rash that then falls ( ↘︎ ) suggests Roseola.
  • CLUES FOR VIRAL EXANTHEMS
    • Fever Timing:
      • High fever first, THEN rash appears as fever breaks? -> Roseola
      • Fever and rash at the same time? -> Measles, Rubella, Varicella
    • Prodrome (Symptoms BEFORE rash):
      • Cough, Coryza (runny nose), Conjunctivitis (3 C's)? -> Measles
      • Sore throat? -> Scarlet Fever
    • Mouth / Mucosal Signs:
      • Koplik spots (white spots in cheeks)? -> Measles
      • Mouth ulcers? -> HFMD / Herpangina
      • Strawberry tongue? -> Scarlet Fever / Kawasaki
    • Other Key Signs:
      • Swollen nodes behind ears? -> Rubella
  • PRURITUS (ITCH): Scabies, Eczema, Varicella are intensely itchy.
  • ITCH SEVERITY & SKIN CARE – quantify pruritus; ask about scratching, calamine, antihistamine, short nails, emollient baths.
  • PAIN: Shingles is classically painful.
  • Joint pains
    • Auto immune diseses for psorasis
    • other auto immune disesse
"Any other symptoms like fever, cough, joint pain, or mouth sores?"
M: Medical / Medication Background
  • Past Medical History – eczema, asthma, immunosuppression, prematurity.
  • Drug Allergies – especially antibiotics.
"Does your child have any other health problems or regular medicines? Any allergies?"
P: Progression
  • Multiple stages at once (spots, blisters, crusts)? → Varicella
"How have the spots changed over time since they first appeared?"
P: Pattern & People
  • SICK CONTACTS (school, nursery, home)
  • IMMUNISATION STATUS (MMR, Varicella)
  • HIGH‑RISK CONTACTS – pregnant household members, newborns, immunocompromised relatives (require separate advice).
"Is anyone else sick? Any recent travel? Are immunisations up to date?"
E: Effect on Life
  • Appears generally well -> Roseola, Fifth Disease
  • Appears miserable/very unwell -> Measles
"How is your child's overall energy? Are they playful, or very sick?"
R: Red Flags
  • NON-BLANCHING RASH (Petechiae/Purpura) + FEVER -> Meningococcemia
  • HIVES + Swelling/Difficulty Breathing -> Anaphylaxis
  • BLISTERING/PEELING with mucosal involvement -> SJS/TEN
  • FEVER > 5 days + Redness (eyes, mouth) + Swelling (hands/feet) -> Kawasaki Disease
  • Rash becoming red, hot or painful (secondary bacterial infection).
  • Ataxia / unsteady gait (possible varicella cerebellitis).
"I must ask some specific questions. Does the rash not fade with pressure? Any trouble breathing?"
S: Severity / Scale
"On a scale of 1 to 10, how itchy or bothersome is the rash?"
Parent / Carer Exposure
  • If non‑immune and exposure within 24 h → discuss adult vaccination or prophylactic aciclovir.
"Have you ever had chicken pox or been vaccinated? Are you or anyone at home pregnant, on cancer treatment, or taking medicines that weaken immunity?"
