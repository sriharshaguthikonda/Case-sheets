Limb Weakness

S: Site / Distribution
  • UNILATERAL (One Side - Hemiparesis):
    • Stroke, TIA, Brain Tumor, MS Plaque
  • BILATERAL - LEGS ONLY (Paraparesis):
    • Spinal Cord Lesion (Compression, Myelitis)
    • Guillain-Barré Syndrome (GBS)
  • ALL FOUR LIMBS (Quadriparesis/Tetraparesis):
    • High Cervical Cord Lesion, Brainstem Stroke
    • Advanced GBS, MND, Myopathy
  • PROXIMAL (Shoulders/Hips > Hands/Feet):
    • Myopathy (e.g., Polymyositis), Myasthenia Gravis
    • Thyroid disease, Cushing's
  • DISTAL (Hands/Feet > Shoulders/Hips):
    • Peripheral Neuropathy (e.g., Diabetes, CMT)
    • Motor Neurone Disease (MND)
"Where exactly is the weakness? Is it on one side of your body? Is it worse in your arms or legs? Is it harder to lift your arms up, or to grip things?"
O: Onset
  • HYPERACUTE (Seconds-Minutes):
    • Stroke (Vascular event), TIA, Seizure (Todd's paresis)
  • ACUTE (Hours-Days):
    • Spinal Cord Compression, Transverse Myelitis, Guillain-Barré Syndrome
  • SUBACUTE (Weeks-Months):
    • Brain/Spinal Tumor, Chronic Inflammatory Neuropathy (CIDP)
  • CHRONIC (Months-Years):
    • Motor Neurone Disease, Multiple Sclerosis, Muscular Dystrophy
"When did this weakness start? Did it come on in an instant, or has it developed over hours, days, or months?"
D: Duration
"How long has this been going on for?"
A/R: Aggravating & Relieving Factors
  • WORSE WITH REPETITION / END OF DAY (Fatigability):
    • Myasthenia Gravis (classic sign)
  • IMPROVES (briefly) WITH EXERCISE:
    • Lambert-Eaton Myasthenic Syndrome (LEMS)
  • WORSE WITH HEAT (Uhthoff's Phenomenon):
    • Multiple Sclerosis (MS)
"Is there anything that makes the weakness better or worse? Does it change with rest, activity, or time of day?"
C: Character of "Weakness"
  • TRUE WEAKNESS:
    • Inability to move (e.g., lift arm).
  • STIFF / SLOWNESS: 
    • Parkinsonism
  • INCOORDINATION / CLUMSINESS (Ataxia):
    • Cerebellar issue
  • SENSORY LOSS making limb feel "useless".
"When you say 'weakness,' what do you mean? Is it that you can't move the limb properly, or is it more like stiffness, clumsiness, or numbness?"
R: Radiation (Progression of Spread)
  • ASCENDING (from feet upwards):
    • Classic for Guillain-Barré Syndrome
  • FOCAL SPREAD (e.g., one hand, then arm, then leg):
    • Suggests Motor Neurone Disease
"Did the weakness start in one place and then spread? For example, did it start in your feet and move up your legs?"
A: Associated Symptoms
  • SENSORY SYMPTOMS (Numbness, tingling, pins/needles):
    • Suggests issue with nerve, spinal cord, or brain. Absence points to muscle or NMJ.
  • LOWER MOTOR NEURON (LMN) SIGNS:
    • Muscle Wasting (Atrophy), Twitches (Fasciculations). Suggests peripheral cause (e.g., MND, neuropathy).
  • UPPER MOTOR NEURON (UMN) SIGNS:
    • Spasticity (stiffness), brisk reflexes. Suggests central cause (Brain/Spinal Cord).
  • BULBAR SYMPTOMS: Difficulty swallowing (Dysphagia), slurred speech (Dysarthria), double vision (Diplopia).
    • Red Flag: Brainstem lesion, MND, Myasthenia Gravis. High aspiration risk.
  • PAIN: Back pain, shooting limb pain.
    • Nerve root compression (Radiculopathy)
"Have you had any other symptoms like numbness, tingling, muscle twitching, stiffness, pain, or any trouble with your speech or swallowing?"
P: Progression
  • STEADILY WORSENING: Neurodegenerative disease, Tumor
  • RELAPSING-REMITTING (episodes of weakness): 
    • Multiple Sclerosis,
    • Myasthenia Gravis
"Overall, has the weakness been getting progressively worse, or does it come and go in episodes?"
P: Pattern
  • FLUCTUATES during the day (worse in evening): 
    • Myasthenia Gravis
"Does the weakness change during the day? For example, is it better in the morning and worse by the evening?"
E: Effect on Life
  • Falls, tripping, difficulty walking.
  • Trouble with daily tasks (dressing, cooking, hygiene).
  • Impact on work/driving.
"How has this weakness affected your daily life? Are you having falls or trouble with activities like walking, dressing, or writing?"
R: Red Flags
  • CAUDA EQUINA / CORD COMPRESSION: Bowel/bladder dysfunction (incontinence/retention), saddle anesthesia (numbness in groin).
  • STROKE (F.A.S.T): Sudden onset with Face droop, Arm weakness, Speech difficulty.
  • RESPIRATORY DISTRESS: Shortness of breath, difficulty taking a deep breath. (GBS, MG Crisis, MND).
  • ACUTE BULBAR DYSFUNCTION: Sudden difficulty swallowing or handling saliva.
"I must ask some very important safety questions. Have you had any change in your bladder or bowel control? Any numbness in your groin area? Any difficulty breathing?"
S: Severity / Scale
"On a scale of 0 to 10, where 0 is no weakness and 10 is complete inability to move, how would you rate your weakness?"
