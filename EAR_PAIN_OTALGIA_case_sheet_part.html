1. SITE (S):
    1. Which ear is painful (Right/Left/Both)?
    2. Is the pain felt
      1. deep inside the ear,
      2. in the ear canal,
      3. on the outer ear (pinna), or
      4. behind the ear (mastoid area)?
    3. Can you point to the exact spot of maximum pain?
    4. Is it a
      1. localized pain or
      2. more diffuse?
  2. ONSET (O):
    1. When did the ear pain first start? (Specific date/time if possible)
    2. Was the onset sudden (e.g., after an injury, swimming, flying) or gradual?
    3. Was it associated with a cold, flu, sore throat, sinus infection, or dental problems?
    4. Any recent ear cleaning, foreign body insertion, or trauma?
  3. DURATION (D):
    1. How long have you been experiencing this pain? (Hours, days, weeks)
    2. Is the pain constant or does it come and go (intermittent)?
    3. If intermittent, how long does each episode typically last, and how frequent are they?
  4. AGGRAVATING/RELIEVING FACTORS (A/R):
    1. Aggravating Factors: What makes the pain worse?
      1. do you use any ear tips to clean
      2. how long do you use earpods earbuds hearing devices?
      3. Touching or pulling on the outer ear (pinna or tragus)? (Suggests otitis externa)
      4. Chewing, swallowing, or yawning? (May suggest TMJ, pharyngeal cause, or Eustachian tube dysfunction)
      5. Lying down, especially on the affected side?
      6. Exposure to cold air or wind?
      7. Water exposure (swimming, showering)?
    2. Relieving Factors: What makes the pain better?
      1. Pain medications (OTC or prescribed)? Which ones and how effective?
      2. Warm or cold compresses?
      3. Keeping the ear dry?
      4. Specific positions?
  5. CHARACTER/TYPE (C):
    1. How would you describe the pain?
      1. Sharp, stabbing, shooting?
      2. Dull, aching, throbbing, pounding?
      3. Burning, itching?
      4. Feeling of fullness or pressure in the ear?
      5. Is it a deep boring pain?
  6. RADIATION (R):
    1. Does the pain spread or radiate anywhere else? (e.g., to the jaw, teeth, neck, throat, temple)
  7. ASSOCIATED SYMPTOMS (A):
    1. Hearing loss or muffled hearing in the affected ear?
    2. Ear discharge (otorrhea)? (Color, consistency, odor, amount)
    3. Tinnitus (ringing or other noises in the ear)?
    4. Dizziness or vertigo (sensation of spinning)?
    5. Fever, chills, general malaise?
    6. Itching in the ear canal?
    7. Feeling of blockage or fullness in the ear?
    8. Swelling around the ear or lymph nodes in the neck?
    9. Sore throat, nasal congestion, cough?
    10. Pain or clicking in the jaw (TMJ symptoms)?
    11. Toothache?
    12. Recent upper respiratory tract infection?
  8. PROGRESSION (P):
    1. How has the pain changed since it first started? (Getting worse, better, staying the same?)
    2. If it's recurrent, are the episodes changing in nature or severity?
    3. If treatment was received, how did it respond? Any changes since then?
  9. PATTERN (P):
    1. Is the pain continuous or does it occur in waves/episodes?
    2. Any specific times of day when it's worse or better?
  10. EFFECT ON LIFE (E):
    1. How is the ear pain affecting your daily activities (sleep, work, concentration, eating)?
    2. Has it affected your hearing or balance, and how does that impact you?
    3. Has it affected your mood?
  11. RED FLAGS (R):
    1. Meningitis
      1. Rash
      2. neck stiff
      3. photophobia
      4. fever
    2. Sudden, severe hearing loss +- pain.
    3. Foul-smelling discharge, especially if bloody.
    4. Swelling and tenderness behind the ear (mastoiditis).
    5. Facial nerve - weakness or asymmetry on the side of the ear pain , Ramsay Hunt syndrome).
    6. Severe vertigo, nausea, vomiting with ear pain and hearing loss.
    7. Persistent pain unresponsive to simple analgesia, especially in diabetics or immunocompromised individuals (concern for malignant otitis externa).
    8. History of recent head trauma.
    9. Neurological deficits (e.g., other cranial nerve palsies, altered mental state).
  12. SCALE (S) (Severity):
    1. On a scale of 0 to 10, with 0 being no pain and 10 being the worst pain imaginable, how would you rate your pain:
      1. Right now?
      2. At its worst?
      3. On average?
