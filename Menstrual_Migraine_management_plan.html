MANAGEMENT PLAN – Menstrual Migraine

1) Confirm & Document

  • Positive diagnosis explained; give written info & support links. [NICE CG150]
  • Start/continue a headache diary to track frequency, severity, meds, and perimenstrual timing. [NICE CG150 1.3.1]
  • Screen for red flags and atypical aura (motor weakness, monocular visual symptoms, reduced consciousness) – refer if present. [NICE CG150 1.2.4]

2) Acute Attack Plan

  • First line: combination therapy taken early:
    • Oral triptan + NSAID (e.g. sumatriptan + ibuprofen/naproxen) or triptan + paracetamol.
    • If monotherapy preferred: triptan or NSAID or paracetamol (aspirin 900 mg is another option in adults).
    • Add an anti-emetic (metoclopramide or prochlorperazine) even if no nausea.
    [NICE CG150 1.3.10–1.3.15]
  • If one triptan fails in ≥3 attacks, try a different triptan; match route to symptoms. [NICE CG150 1.3.12]
  • Avoid opioids/ergots. [NICE CG150 1.3.16]
  • Consider rimegepant for acute treatment if triptans failed/contraindicated per TA919 (specialist pathways). [NICE CG150 1.3.15]

3) Mini-Prevention for Predictable Menstrual Attacks

  • If attacks occur in a predictable perimenstrual window and standard acute therapy is inadequate, offer short-term prophylaxis:
    • Frovatriptan 2.5 mg twice daily on expected migraine days (start just before, continue through window).
    • Zolmitriptan 2.5 mg twice to three times daily on expected days.
    (off-label use acknowledged in guideline) [NICE CG150 1.3.31]

4) Longer-term Prevention (if frequent/disabling)

  • Discuss benefits/risks; trial one at a time: propranolol, amitriptyline, or topiramate (with MHRA PPP if of child-bearing potential). Review at 3–6 months. [NICE CG150 1.3.17–1.3.19, 1.3.27] [MHRA Topiramate PPP 2024]
  • If ≥3 preventives failed/not tolerated:
    • Atogepant (oral CGRP antagonist) per NICE TA973 (specialist-led pathways).
    • Rimegepant for prevention per NICE TA906 (episodic; criteria apply).
    • CGRP mAbs (erenumab, fremanezumab, galcanezumab, eptinezumab) per NICE TAs; Botulinum toxin A for chronic migraine.
    [NICE CG150 1.3.24–1.3.26] [NICE TA973 Atogepant] [NICE TA906 Rimegepant]

5) Contraception & Pregnancy

  • If migraine with aura, do not offer combined hormonal contraception (CHC). [NICE CG150 1.3.30] [FSRH UKMEC]
  • Pregnancy (or trying): acute plan is paracetamol first-line; consider triptan/NSAID only after discussion; seek specialist advice for prevention. [NICE CG150 1.3.32–1.3.33]
  • Avoid topiramate in pregnancy; if considered in women who could conceive, use only under the MHRA Pregnancy Prevention Programme. [MHRA 2024]

6) Medication Overuse & Self-management

  • Explain MOH and cap acute meds (typical advice: ≤2 days/week); set stop rules in the written plan. [NICE CG150 1.3.6]
  • Trigger management: regular meals, hydration, sleep hygiene; limit caffeine/alcohol; exercise and stress-reduction.

7) Follow-up & Referral

  • Review response and diary in 6–8 weeks for acute/miniprophylaxis; 3–6 months for preventives; adjust or switch if ineffective/tolerability issues.
  • Refer if red flags/atypical aura, diagnostic uncertainty, or inadequate control after stepped therapy / considering CGRP options. [NICE CG150]
