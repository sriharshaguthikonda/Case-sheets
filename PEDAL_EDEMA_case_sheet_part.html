PEDAL EDEMA

Pedal Edema

1ST COMPLAINT: LEG SWELLING (Pedal Edema)

S: Site & Character
  • UNILATERAL (One Leg)
    • Deep Vein Thrombosis (DVT)
    • Cellulitis
    • Lymphedema
    • Trauma / Musculoskeletal injury
    • Chronic Venous Insufficiency (can be asymmetrical)
  • BILATERAL (Both Legs)
    • Congestive Heart Failure
    • Nephrotic Syndrome / Renal Failure
    • Liver Failure (Cirrhosis)
    • Medication-induced (e.g., CCBs, NSAIDs)
    • Myxedema (Hypothyroidism)
  • PITTING vs NON-PITTING
    • Pitting: Suggests fluid overload (Cardiac, Renal, Hepatic)
    • Non-Pitting: Suggests Lymphedema or Myxedema
"Is the swelling in one leg or both? Can you press on it, and does it leave a dent?"
O: Onset
  • SUDDEN / ACUTE
    • Deep Vein Thrombosis (DVT)
    • Acute Decompensated Heart Failure
    • Cellulitis
  • GRADUAL / CHRONIC
    • Chronic Heart Failure
    • Chronic Kidney Disease
    • Chronic Liver Disease
    • Venous Insufficiency / Lymphedema
"When did you first notice the swelling? Did it appear suddenly or develop over time?"
D: Duration & Pain
  • PAINFUL SWELLING
    • DVT
    • Cellulitis
  • PAINLESS SWELLING
    • Most systemic causes (Cardiac, Renal, Hepatic)
"How long has it been there? Is the swelling painful?"
A/R: Aggravating & Relieving Factors
  • WORSE with prolonged standing/sitting (dependency)
  • BETTER with leg elevation
  • WORSE with high salt intake
"Does anything make the swelling better or worse, like your position or diet?"
C: Character (Associated Skin Changes)
  • Red, hot, tender skin -> Cellulitis
  • Brownish discoloration (hemosiderin) -> Chronic Venous Insufficiency
  • Tense, shiny skin; weeping -> Severe edema
  • Ulcers (especially around medial malleolus) -> Venous Ulcers
"Have you noticed any changes to the skin over the swollen areas?"
R: Radiation (of associated pain)
"If there is pain, does it travel anywhere?"
A: Associated Symptoms
  • CARDIO-RESPIRATORY
    • Shortness of breath on exertion (Dyspnea)
    • Shortness of breath when lying flat (Orthopnea)
    • Waking up breathless at night (PND)
    • Chest pain, Palpitations
  • RENAL
    • Frothy urine (Proteinuria)
    • Reduced urine output (Oliguria)
    • Blood in urine (Hematuria)
  • HEPATIC
    • Yellowing of skin/eyes (Jaundice)
    • Abdominal swelling (Ascites)
    • Easy bruising or bleeding
  • ENDOCRINE
    • Fatigue, weight gain, cold intolerance, dry skin (Hypothyroidism)
  • GENERAL
    • Recent weight gain
    • Medication History: Check for Calcium Channel Blockers, NSAIDs, Gabapentin
"Have you had any shortness of breath, changes in urination, or abdominal swelling?"
P: Progression
"Has the swelling been getting worse? Is it moving further up your legs?"
P: Pattern
"Is the swelling worse at a particular time of day, for example, in the evening?"
E: Effect on Life
"How is the swelling affecting you? For example, with your mobility or fitting into shoes?"
R: Red Flags
  • SUDDEN ONSET UNILATERAL SWOLLEN, PAINFUL CALF
    • Possible DVT. Ask about associated chest pain/SOB (PE)
  • SEVERE SHORTNESS OF BREATH
    • Acute Pulmonary Edema (from heart failure)
  • HOT, RED, TENDER LEG with FEVER/SYSTEMIC UNWELLNESS
    • Severe Cellulitis / Sepsis
"I need to ask some specific questions to rule out anything serious."
S: Severity / Scale
"On a scale of 1 to 10, how much is this swelling bothering you?"
