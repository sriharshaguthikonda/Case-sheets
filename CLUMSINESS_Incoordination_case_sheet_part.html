1. SITE & LATERALITY:
    1. Which limbs affected? (Upper/Lower/Both, Right/Left/Bilateral)
    2. Is it more on one side?
    3. Does it affect fine motor skills more or gross motor skills?
  2. ONSET (O):
    1. When did you first notice the clumsiness?
    2. Was it sudden or gradual?
    3. Any specific event that triggered it? (e.g., illness, medication change, trauma)
  3. DURATION (D):
    1. How long have you had this problem? (Days/Weeks/Months/Years)
    2. Is it constant or intermittent? If intermittent, how long do episodes last?
  4. PROGRESSION (P):
    1. Is the clumsiness getting worse, better, or staying the same?
    2. If worsening, how quickly?
  5. AGGRAVATING/RELIEVING FACTORS (A/R):
    1. Is it worse when you are tired or stressed?
    2. Does anything make it better (e.g., concentrating hard, specific posture)?
    3. Is it worse with eyes open or closed? (Less relevant for pure cerebellar, but good to ask)
  6. ASSOCIATED SYMPTOMS:
    1. Weakness in the affected limbs?
    2. Numbness or tingling?
    3. Tremor? (Resting, intention/action tremor - e.g., when reaching for an object)
    4. Difficulty with specific tasks?
      1. Writing (handwriting changed?)
      2. Buttoning clothes, tying shoelaces
      3. Using cutlery, drinking from a cup (spilling?)
      4. Knitting, sewing, playing musical instruments (as in video example)
      5. Difficulty picking up small objects?
    5. Speech changes? (Slurred, slow, difficult to understand - dysarthria)
    6. Swallowing difficulties (dysphagia)?
    7. Vision changes? (Blurred vision, double vision, nystagmus - "eyes jumping")
    8. Balance problems / Unsteadiness? (See next section)
    9. Headache, vomiting, altered consciousness? (Signs of ↑ICT)
  7. IMPACT ON DAILY LIFE:
    1. How has this affected your daily activities (e.g., self-care, work, hobbies)?
    2. Are you worried about it?
