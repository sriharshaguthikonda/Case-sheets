Hematuria (Blood in Urine)

1ST COMPLAINT: BLOOD IN URINE

S: Site / Source (Timing in Stream)
  • INITIAL (at start of urination):
    • Urethral source (Urethritis, stricture)
  • TERMINAL (at end of urination):
    • Bladder neck or prostate source (Cystitis, BPH)
  • TOTAL (throughout urination):
    • Bladder, Ureter, or Kidney source
  • PAIN LOCATION:
    • Loin/Flank: Kidney stone, Pyelonephritis, Renal cell carcinoma
    • Suprapubic: Cystitis (UTI)
"When do you see the blood during urination—at the beginning, the end, or all the way through? Are you having any pain anywhere, like in your back or lower belly?"
O: Onset
  • SUDDEN:
    • Kidney stone (often with severe pain)
    • UTI
    • Trauma / Injury
  • GRADUAL / DISCOVERED ON TESTING (Incidental):
    • Bladder or Kidney cancer
    • Glomerulonephritis (kidney filter disease)
    • Benign Prostatic Hyperplasia (BPH)
"When did you first notice the blood? Did it appear suddenly, or was it found on a routine urine test?"
D: Duration
  • SINGLE EPISODE vs. RECURRENT / PERSISTENT
"How long has this been happening? Was it just once, or does it keep coming back?"
A/R: Aggravating & Relieving Factors
  • AGGRAVATING:
    • Vigorous exercise (Exercise-induced hematuria)
    • Recent URTI (sore throat) -> IgA Nephropathy, Post-strep glomerulonephritis
    • Medications (Anticoagulants, Cyclophosphamide)
"Does anything seem to trigger it, like heavy exercise or being unwell recently?"
C: Character of Urine
  • COLOR:
    • Pink or Bright Red: Suggests lower urinary tract source (bladder, prostate).
    • "Cola-Colored" or Brown: Suggests upper tract / glomerular source (blood altered by acidic urine).
  • CLOTS PRESENT:
    • Suggests a non-glomerular source. Significant red flag.
    • Stringy/worm-like clots: may indicate a ureteric source.
  • MICROSCOPIC vs. MACROSCOPIC (GROSS):
    • Is the blood visible to the naked eye (macroscopic) or only on a dipstick test (microscopic)?
"What does the urine look like? Is it pink, bright red, or more like a cola color? Have you seen any clots?"
R: Radiation of Pain
  • Loin-to-groin radiation is classic for a kidney stone passing down the ureter.
"If you have pain, does it travel anywhere, for instance from your back down to your groin?"
A: Associated Symptoms
  • LOWER URINARY TRACT SYMPTOMS (LUTS):
    • Storage (Irritative): Frequency, Urgency, Nocturia, Dysuria (painful urination) -> UTI, Bladder stone, Bladder cancer
    • Voiding (Obstructive): Hesitancy, poor stream, straining, terminal dribbling -> BPH, Prostate Cancer
  • SYSTEMIC SYMPTOMS:
    • Fever, chills, loin pain -> Pyelonephritis (kidney infection)
    • Edema (swelling), frothy urine, high blood pressure -> Nephritic/Nephrotic Syndrome
    • Rash, joint pain -> Vasculitis, SLE (Lupus)
    • Unintentional weight loss, loss of appetite -> Malignancy
"Along with the blood, have you had any pain when you urinate, a fever, any new swelling in your legs, or any rashes?"
P: Progression
"Has the amount of blood changed over time?"
P: Pattern
  • Constant or Intermittent?
"Is it there every time you go to the toilet, or does it come and go?"
E: Effect on Life
  • Anxiety, concern about cancer.
  • Impact of pain or other urinary symptoms on daily activities.
"How has this been affecting you? Is it causing you much worry?"
R: Red Flags
  • PAINLESS, VISIBLE (GROSS) HEMATURIA: Urological malignancy until proven otherwise.
  • AGE > 40 with smoking history.
  • Presence of BLOOD CLOTS.
  • Signs of KIDNEY FAILURE: Swelling (edema), decreased urine output, frothy urine.
  • Associated SYSTEMIC SYMPTOMS: Unexplained weight loss, fever, night sweats.
"I must ask some specific safety questions. Have you lost any weight without trying? Have you noticed any blood clots?"
S: Severity / Scale
"On a scale of 1 to 10, how much is this problem concerning you?"
