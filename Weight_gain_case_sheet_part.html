1ST COMPLAINT: WEIGHT GAIN

  1. SITE & DISTRIBUTION                              - Where on your body have you noticed the weight gain?
    1. CENTRAL / TRUNCAL (Abdomen, face, neck, upper back)
      1. Cushing's Syndrome ("moon face", "buffalo hump")
      2. Metabolic Syndrome / Insulin Resistance
    2. PERIPHERAL (Legs, ankles, hands, abdomen)
      1. Edema from Congestive Heart Failure (CHF)
      2. Edema from renal disease (Nephrotic Syndrome)
      3. Ascites/Edema from Liver Disease
      4. Lymphedema (often asymmetric)
    3. GENERALIZED (All over the body)
      1. Increased adipose tissue from excess caloric intake
      2. Hypothyroidism

  2. ONSET & DURATION                                  - When did the weight gain start and how quickly did it occur?
    1. GRADUAL (Months to Years) (Suggests adipose tissue gain)
      1. Lifestyle changes (decreased activity, increased caloric intake)
      2. Hypothyroidism
      3. Polycystic Ovary Syndrome (PCOS)
      4. Medication side effect (chronic use)
    2. SUDDEN/RAPID (Days to Weeks) (Suggests fluid retention)
      1. Acute Decompensated Heart Failure
      2. Nephrotic Syndrome / Acute Kidney Injury
      3. Liver Cirrhosis with rapid Ascites formation
      4. Initiation of medications like high-dose steroids
  3. AGGRAVATING FACTORS / DIETARY & ACTIVITY CHANGES
    1. INCREASED APPETITE (POLYPHAGIA)
      1. Changes in dietary habits (larger portions, high-calorie foods, snacking)
      2. Atypical Depression, Binge Eating Disorder, Stress
      3. Medications (e.g., mirtazapine, olanzapine, steroids)
    2. DECREASED PHYSICAL ACTIVITY
      1. Change to a sedentary job or lifestyle
      2. Injury, arthritis, or pain limiting mobility
      3. Depression (anergia, lack of motivation)
  4. RELIEVING FACTORS / ATTEMPTS TO LOSE WEIGHT
    1. SUCCESSFUL with lifestyle changes (Suggests lifestyle cause)
    2. NO RESPONSE to diet/exercise (Suggests pathologic cause like endocrine/fluid overload)

  5. CHARACTER & NATURE OF GAIN
    1. FIRM, NON-PITTING (Suggests fat/adipose tissue)
      1. General obesity from lifestyle
      2. Cushing's Syndrome, Hypothyroidism
    2. SOFT, PITTING EDEMA (Suggests fluid retention)
      1. Pressing a finger leaves an indentation (CHF, renal disease)
      2. Hypoalbuminemia (from liver or kidney disease)
    3. TENSE, FLUID-FILLED (Ascites)
      1. Distended abdomen with a fluid wave (Liver Disease)

  6. ASSOCIATED SYMPTOMS
    1. FATIGUE / LETHARGY (Hypothyroidism, Depression, CHF)
    2. COLD INTOLERANCE, CONSTIPATION, DRY SKIN, HAIR LOSS (Hypothyroidism)
    3. MENSTRUAL IRREGULARITIES, HIRSUTISM, ACNE (PCOS, Cushing's)
    4. PURPLE STRIAE, EASY BRUISING, MUSCLE WEAKNESS (Cushing's Syndrome)
    5. SHORTNESS OF BREATH (especially on exertion or lying flat), SWELLING IN LEGS (Congestive Heart Failure)
    6. FROTHY URINE, PUFFINESS AROUND EYES (Nephrotic Syndrome)
    7. ABDOMINAL SWELLING, YELLOWING OF SKIN/EYES (JAUNDICE) (Liver Disease)
    8. LOW MOOD, ANHEDONIA, SLEEP DISTURBANCE (Depression)
  7. PROGRESSION, SEVERITY & IMPACT
    1. MILD / SLOWLY PROGRESSIVE (e.g. 5-10 lbs over a year)
      1. Likely lifestyle-related, mild hypothyroidism
    2. MODERATE / STEADILY PROGRESSIVE (e.g. >10% body weight in 6 months)
      1. Uncontrolled endocrine disorders (Hypothyroidism, Cushing's)
      2. Side effect of medication
      3. Depression, Binge Eating Disorder
    3. SEVERE / RAPID (e.g. 5-10 lbs in a week)
      1. Acute fluid overload (CHF)
      2. Acute renal failure, new onset nephrotic syndrome
      3. Rapidly developing ascites
      4. Impact of high-dose steroids
  8. RED FLAGS (REQUIRE URGENT EVALUATION)
    1. CARDIOVASCULAR RED FLAGS
      1. Rapid gain with severe shortness of breath or chest pain (Acute Decompensated Heart Failure)
      2. Syncope or palpitations
    2. ENDOCRINE RED FLAGS
      1. Signs of severe Cushing's (hypertension, hypokalemia)
      2. Signs of severe hypothyroidism (bradycardia, altered mental status - myxedema coma)
    3. FLUID OVERLOAD RED FLAGS
      1. No urine output (anuria) or very low output (oliguria) (Acute Kidney Injury)
      2. Tense, painful abdomen with difficulty breathing (Liver Failure)
    4. PSYCHIATRIC RED FLAGS
      1. Suicidal ideation or self-harm (Severe Depression)
  • PERTINENT MEDICATION HISTORY (New or changed dose)
    1. CORTICOSTEROIDS (e.g., Prednisone)
    2. ANTIPSYCHOTICS (e.g., Olanzapine), ANTIDEPRESSANTS (e.g., Mirtazapine)
    3. DIABETES MEDICATIONS (e.g., Insulin, Sulfonylureas)
