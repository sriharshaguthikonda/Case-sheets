• S: Site
    • Which nipple is affected? (Right/Left/Bilateral)
    • Is the entire nipple retracted or just a portion?
  • O: Onset
    • When was the retraction first noticed?
      • Congenital (present since birth or noted around puberty - developmental).
      • Recent/Acquired (concerning for pathological cause like Cancer, Duct Ectasia, Periductal Mastitis, TB).
  • D: Duration
    • If acquired, how long has it been present?
  • A/R: Aggravating / Relieving Factors
    • Aggravating Factors: Does it become more pronounced with certain movements (e.g., raising arm, pectoral contraction)?
    • Relieving Factors: Can the nipple be everted (manually or spontaneously)? Or is it fixed?
  • C: Character/Type
    • Describe the retraction: Slit-like, circumferential, dimple-like.
    • Is there any tethering of the surrounding skin or areola?
  • R: Radiation (Not applicable to retraction itself).
  • A: Associated Symptoms
    • Palpable lump or thickening in the breast (as per 1st complaint).
    • Breast pain (as per 2nd complaint).
    • Nipple discharge (as per 3rd complaint).
    • Skin changes on the breast or areola (dimpling, puckering, redness, rash, eczema of nipple for Paget's disease).
    • History of loss of weight (concerning for Ca. breast, TB of breast).
    • History of TB of chest wall (potentially leading to retromammary abscess - rare).
    • Previous breast infections or inflammation.
  • P: Progression
    • If acquired, has the retraction worsened or changed over time?
  • P: Pattern
    • Is the retraction constant?
  • E: Effect on life
    • Cosmetic concerns.
    • Difficulty with breastfeeding (if applicable).
    • Anxiety about the underlying cause.
