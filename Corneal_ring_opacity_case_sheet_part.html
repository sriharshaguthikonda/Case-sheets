1ST COMPLAINT: RING / OPACITY ON CORNEA

O: Onset & Discovery
  • Patient noticed visual changes vs. Incidental finding on examination
  • Sudden or gradual appearance?
"How was this discovered? Did you notice it yourself, or was it pointed out by an optometrist or doctor?"
C: Character of Symptoms
  • PAIN: (Critical Differentiator)
    • Painful, Red Eye -> Infectious Keratitis / Corneal Ulcer (Ophthalmic Emergency)
    • Painless -> Suggests a depositional ring (Arcus, Kayser-Fleischer)
  • VISUAL SYMPTOMS:
    • Blurred vision, glare, or halos around lights?
    • Is it just a cosmetic change with no effect on vision?
  • APPEARANCE (if known from referral/patient):
    • White/Grey Ring (Arcus Senilis)
    • Golden-Brown/Greenish Ring (Kayser-Fleischer Ring)
    • White spot/opacity on the cornea (Infiltrate/Ulcer)
"Is this causing any problems with your vision? And crucially, is there any pain?"
A: Associated Symptoms
  • LOCAL EYE SYMPTOMS
    • Redness, discharge, grittiness
    • Light sensitivity (Photophobia)
    • History of contact lens wear (especially overnight)?
    • Any recent trauma to the eye?
  • CARDIOVASCULAR CLUES (for Arcus Senilis)
    • Any history of high cholesterol?
    • Personal or strong family history of heart attacks or strokes at a young age?
    • Pain in your calves when walking (claudication)?
    • Any yellowish bumps on your skin, especially eyelids (xanthelasma) or tendons?
  • NEUROLOGICAL & LIVER CLUES (for Kayser-Fleischer Ring / Wilson's Disease)
    • Any tremor, clumsiness, or changes in your coordination or handwriting?
    • Any changes in your mood, personality, or behaviour?
    • Have you ever been told you have a liver problem? Any history of jaundice (yellow skin/eyes)?
    • Any abdominal swelling?
"The ring on the eye can sometimes be a sign of a general health issue. I need to ask some questions about your overall health."
S: Site & Laterality
  • BILATERAL: Arcus Senilis, Kayser-Fleischer Rings are typically bilateral.
  • UNILATERAL: Infectious Keratitis is most often unilateral.
"Is this affecting one eye or both?"
D: Duration
"How long has this been present, to your knowledge?"
P: Progression
"Has the ring or opacity changed in size or density since it was first noticed?"
P: Pattern
"Are the symptoms always there, or do they fluctuate?"
E: Effect on Life
"How has this affected your daily life? Has it impacted your ability to drive, read, or work?"
R: Red Flags
  • Any corneal opacity associated with a painful, red eye.
  • A brown/green ring (Kayser-Fleischer), as this mandates urgent investigation for Wilson's Disease.
  • A white ring (Arcus Senilis) in a person under the age of 50.
  • Any new or progressive loss of vision.
"I just need to summarize the key warning signs I'm looking for."
S: Severity / Bother Scale
"On a scale of 1 to 10, how much do any symptoms from this condition bother you?"
