1ST COMPLAINT: HEART MURMUR (Clinical Finding)

S: Setting & Status
  • Incidental finding vs. Found during investigation of symptoms
  • Is this a new murmur, or have you been told about it before?
"I understand a heart murmur has been found. Could you tell me how this was discovered? Was it during a routine check-up, or because you were feeling unwell?"
A: Associated Symptoms
  • HEART FAILURE SYMPTOMS (Fluid Overload / Poor Output)
    • Shortness of Breath (Dyspnea):
      • On exertion? (How many stairs? How far on the flat?)
      • When lying flat (Orthopnea)? (How many pillows do you use?)
      • Waking you from sleep gasping for air (PND)?
    • Fluid Retention:
      • Swelling in your ankles or legs (Peripheral Edema)? Abdominal bloating?
      • ie., gain excessive weight?
    • Fatigue: Feeling unusually tired or having reduced exercise tolerance?
  • ISCHEMIA / INADEQUATE FLOW SYMPTOMS
    • Chest Pain / Angina: Any tightness, pressure, or pain in your chest, especially on exertion?
    • Syncope / Pre-syncope: Have you had any blackouts or dizzy spells, particularly when you exert yourself?
  • EMBOLIC / INFECTIVE SYMPTOMS
    • Neurological Events: Any episodes of weakness, numbness, or speech disturbance (TIA/Stroke)?
    • Constitutional Symptoms: Unexplained fevers, chills, drenching night sweats, weight loss (Infective Endocarditis)?
    • Painful spots on fingers/toes, splinter hemorrhages under nails?
  • OTHER SYMPTOMS
    • Palpitations (feeling your heart race or skip a beat)?
    • Hoarseness of voice (can be due to left atrial enlargement compressing a nerve)?
"The most important thing is to understand how you have been feeling. Have you experienced any shortness of breath, chest pain, or dizziness?"
R: Risk Factors & PMH
  • History of heart attack, hypertension, high cholesterol, or diabetes?
  • History of Rheumatic Fever as a child?
  • Any congenital heart problems from birth (e.g., bicuspid aortic valve)?
  • History of IV Drug Use?
  • Recent dental procedures or other infections?
  • Family history of sudden cardiac death or valvular heart disease?
"I need to ask about your past health to understand the context of this murmur."
O: Onset (of Symptoms)
"When did you first start noticing any of these symptoms we've discussed?"
P: Progression
"Have your symptoms been getting progressively worse over time?"
E: Effect on Life
  • (Clinician note: Quantify functional status, e.g., NYHA Class I-IV)
"How has this been impacting your daily life? Has it stopped you from doing things you would normally do?"
R: Red Flags
  • Syncope on exertion.
  • New onset or rapidly worsening symptoms of heart failure.
  • Signs of systemic infection (fever, night sweats) in the context of a new murmur (Infective Endocarditis).
  • Associated new neurological symptoms (suggesting an embolic event).
"I'm just going to quickly summarize some of the key warning signs I'm looking for."
S: Severity / Bother Scale
"On a scale of 1 to 10, how much are these symptoms affecting your quality of life?"
