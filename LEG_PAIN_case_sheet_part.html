1ST COMPLAINT: LEG PAIN

  • S: Site "Where exactly is the pain? Can you point with one finger?"
    • UNILATERAL [DVT, Cellulitis, Trauma, Sciatica, PAD]
    • BILATERAL [Peripheral Neuropathy, Spinal Stenosis, Bilateral PAD]
    • CALF [DVT, Muscle strain, Ruptured Baker’s Cyst, Claudication]
    • JOINT [Arthritis - OA/RA/Septic/Gout]
  • O: Onset "When did the pain start? Did it come on suddenly or gradually?"
    • SUDDEN [DVT, Acute Limb Ischemia, Muscle/Tendon rupture, Compartment Syndrome, Trauma]
    • GRADUAL [Chronic Limb Ischemia (PAD), Neuropathy, Arthritis, Chronic Venous Insufficiency]
  • D: Duration "How long does the pain last when it comes on? Is it there for seconds, minutes, or hours?"
  • A: Aggravating Factors "Is there anything that seems to make the pain worse?"
    • WITH WALKING (EXERTION) [Vascular Claudication, Neurogenic Claudication]
    • WITH STANDING [Venous Insufficiency, Spinal Stenosis]
    • WITH TOUCH [Cellulitis, Compartment Syndrome]
  • R: Relieving Factors "Does anything make it better?"
    • REST [Vascular Claudication]
    • LEANING FORWARD / SITTING [Neurogenic Claudication]
    • ELEVATING THE LEG [Venous Insufficiency]
    • HANGING LEG DOWN (DEPENDENCY) [Critical Limb Ischemia / PAD]
  • C: Character "What does the pain feel like? Is it sharp, dull, burning...?"
    • CRAMPING / ACHING [Claudication, Muscle cramps, DVT]
    • BURNING / TINGLING / SHOOTING [Neuropathic - Sciatica, Peripheral Neuropathy]
    • THROBBING [Infection - Cellulitis, Septic Arthritis]
  • R: Radiation "Does the pain seem to travel anywhere else from where it starts?"
    • DOWN BACK OF LEG TO FOOT [Sciatica - L5/S1 radiculopathy]
    • DOWN FRONT/SIDE OF THIGH [L2/L3/L4 radiculopathy]
  • A: Associated Symptoms "Have you noticed anything else along with the pain?"
    • SWELLING [DVT, Cellulitis, Venous Insufficiency, Heart/Kidney/Liver failure]
    • SKIN CHANGES [Redness, Pallor, Shininess, Hairlessness, Ulcers]
    • NUMBNESS or WEAKNESS [Nerve issue, Severe Ischemia]
    • FEVER or feeling generally unwell [Infection]
  • P: Progression "Since it first started, would you say the pain has been getting better, worse, or staying about the same?"
  • P: Pattern "Is the pain there constantly, or does it tend to come and go?"
    • CONSTANT [Infection, Fracture, Critical Limb Ischemia, DVT]
    • INTERMITTENT [Claudication]
    • WORSE AT A PARTICULAR TIME (e.g., Night) [Ischemic rest pain, Bone tumour]
  • E: Effect on Life "How has this pain been affecting your daily activities, like walking or sleeping?"
  • R: Red Flags "I just need to ask a few specific questions to make sure we're not missing anything serious."
    • ACUTE LIMB ISCHEMIA (The 6 P's) [Pain, Pallor, Pulselessness, Perishingly Cold, Paraesthesia, Paralysis]
    • PULMONARY EMBOLISM [Associated chest pain or shortness of breath]
    • CAUDA EQUINA SYNDROME [Numbness around your bottom, or any trouble with your bladder or bowels]
    • SYSTEMIC INFECTION / CANCER [Fever, night sweats, or any unplanned weight loss]
  • S: Severity / Scale "On a scale of 1 to 10, with 10 being the worst pain you can imagine, how would you rate it?"
