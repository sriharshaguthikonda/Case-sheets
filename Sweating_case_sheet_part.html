1ST COMPLAINT: EXCESSIVE SWEATING

S: Site
  • GENERALIZED (all over): Endocrine causes (Thyroid, Menopause), Infection, Malignancy, Medications
  • LOCALIZED / FOCAL:
    • Palms, Soles, Axillae -> Primary Focal Hyperhidrosis
    • Face (while eating) -> Gustatory sweating (Frey's Syndrome)
"Where on your body do you notice the sweating the most? Is it all over, or in specific places like your hands, feet, or armpits?"
O: Onset
  • Since puberty -> Suggests primary focal hyperhidrosis
  • Recent onset -> Suggests a secondary cause (new illness, medication)
"When did you first notice this excessive sweating? Was it a sudden change or has it been gradual?"
D: Duration
"How long has this been a problem for you?"
A/R: Aggravating & Relieving Factors
"Does anything seem to trigger the sweating, like stress or certain foods?"
C: Character (Timing & Triggers)
  • NIGHT SWEATS
    • "Drenching" sweats? (Requiring changing of clothes or bedsheets) -> This is a B-Symptom.
    • hot flushes? -> Menopause
  • DAYTIME SWEATING
    • Associated with eating? -> Gustatory sweating
    • Constant feeling of being hot and sweaty? -> Hyperthyroidism
"This is a key question: when does the sweating happen? Is it mainly at night, during the day, or in specific situations?"
R: Radiation
(Not applicable)
A: Associated Symptoms
  • CONSTITUTIONAL ("B SYMPTOMS")
    • Unexplained Fever
      • Infection (TB, HIV, Endocarditis)
    • Drenching Night Sweats
    • Unexplained Weight Loss
      • Leukemia, TB
    • Lymph node swelling ?
  • ENDOCRINE
    • Palpitations, anxiety, tremor, diarrhea, heat intolerance -> Hyperthyroidism
    • Episodic headache, palpitations, and sweating -> Pheochromocytoma Triad
    • Hot flushes, menstrual changes -> Menopause
    • Sweating during hunger spells -> Hypoglycemia
  • CARDIOVASCULAR
    • Associated with chest pain or shortness of breath -> Acute Coronary Syndrome
    • Orthopnea, paroxysmal nocturnal dyspnea -> Heart Failure
  • INFECTIOUS
    • Chronic cough, hemoptysis, travel/contact history -> Tuberculosis (TB)
    • High-risk behaviors, other infections -> HIV
    • New heart murmur, IV drug use -> Endocarditis
"Along with the sweating, have you experienced any other symptoms like fever, weight loss, or palpitations?"
P: Progression
"Has the sweating been getting worse over time?"
P: Pattern
"Is there any pattern to the sweating throughout the day or month?"
E: Effect on Life
  • Social embarrassment / avoidance
  • Need to change clothes frequently
  • Disrupted sleep
  • Difficulty with tasks (e.g., gripping objects if palmar)
"How has this sweating been impacting your life? For example, your work, social activities, or sleep?"
R: Red Flags
  • The "B-Symptoms": Drenching night sweats, unexplained fever, significant weight loss.
  • Sweating associated with chest pain or severe shortness of breath.
  • Episodic sweating with severe headaches and palpitations.
"I'm just going to summarize the key warning signs I'm looking for."
S: Severity / Bother Scale
"On a scale of 1 to 10, how much does this sweating bother you and interfere with your life?"
