1ST COMPLAINT: URINARY FREQUENCY

S: Site of Associated Pain
  • Pain on urination (Dysuria) -> UTI
  • Suprapubic pain -> Bladder distension / Cystitis
  • Flank pain -> Pyelonephritis / Stones
"Do you have any pain associated with this? In your lower abdomen, back, or when you pass urine?"
O: Onset
  • ACUTE: UTI, New onset Diabetes Mellitus (DKA)
  • GRADUAL: Benign Prostatic Hyperplasia (BPH), Overactive Bladder, Pelvic organ prolapse
"When did you first notice you were needing to go to the toilet more often? Was it a sudden change?"
D: Duration
"How long has this been a problem for you?"
A/R: Aggravating & Relieving Factors
  • AGGRAVATING: Caffeine, alcohol, high fluid intake, cold weather.
  • RELIEVING: Fluid restriction (caution advised).
"Does anything seem to make it worse, for example, drinking coffee or alcohol?"
C: Character (Volume & Timing)
  • High Volume (Polyuria): Passing large amounts each time.
    • Diabetes Mellitus (osmotic diuresis)
    • Diabetes Insipidus
    • Psychogenic Polydipsia
  • Low Volume: Passing small amounts frequently.
    • Bladder irritation (UTI)
    • Incomplete emptying / Obstruction (BPH)
    • Overactive Bladder (OAB)
  • Nocturia: Waking from sleep to urinate? (How many times?)
"When you go to the toilet frequently, are you passing a large amount of urine each time, or just small amounts? And is this happening at night as well?"
R: Radiation (of Pain)
"If you have pain, does it travel or spread anywhere?"
A: Associated Symptoms
  • OTHER URINARY SYMPTOMS (LUTS)
    • Urgency: A sudden, desperate need to pass urine?
    • Hesitancy: Trouble starting the flow?
    • Poor Stream: Is the flow weak or intermittent?
    • Terminal Dribbling: Leaking after you've finished?
    • Incontinence: Any leakage? (With cough/sneeze - Stress vs. with urgency - Urge)
    • Incomplete Emptying: Feeling like the bladder isn't empty after you go?
  • ENDOCRINE / METABOLIC
    • Polydipsia: Are you excessively thirsty?
    • Polyphagia: Increased appetite?
    • Unexplained weight loss, fatigue, blurred vision? (Diabetes Mellitus)
  • URINE APPEARANCE
    • Cloudy or foul-smelling? (UTI)
    • Blood in the urine (Hematuria)?
    • Frothy urine? (Proteinuria)
  • GYNAECOLOGICAL
    • Feeling of a lump or pressure down below? (Prolapse)
    • Possibility of pregnancy?
"Along with the frequency, have you noticed any other changes in your urination or your general health?"
P: Progression
"Since this started, have the symptoms been getting worse?"
P: Pattern
"Is the frequency more of a problem during the day or at night?"
E: Effect on Life
  • Impact on sleep due to nocturia
  • "Toilet mapping" / Social restriction
  • Impact on work and travel
"How has this been affecting your daily life? Is it disrupting your sleep or making it difficult to go out?"
R: Red Flags
  • Cauda Equina Syndrome: New back pain with bilateral leg weakness/sciatica, saddle anesthesia (numbness around groin/buttocks), or new bowel/bladder incontinence.
  • Painless gross hematuria (visible blood in urine) -> Malignancy
  • Signs of DKA (vomiting, abdominal pain, confusion)
  • Fever, chills, and flank pain (Pyelonephritis)
  • Acute urinary retention (inability to pass any urine)
"I just need to ask some specific questions to make sure we're not missing anything serious."
S: Severity / Bother Scale
"On a scale of 1 to 10, how much does this urinary frequency bother you?"
