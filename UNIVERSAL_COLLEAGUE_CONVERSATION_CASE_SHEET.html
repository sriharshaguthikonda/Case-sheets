UNIVERSAL COLLEAGUE CONVERSATION CASE SHEET

INTRODUCTIONS

    greet
    "Hi there, Dr.Reba, how are you?"
    I am good ...thank you for asking!
    Intro yourself and roles
    "Hi, I’m Dr. [Your Name], you might have seen around."
    Thank you
    "Thank you for meeting me on short notice."
    build rapport "I’m glad we could have this chat to discuss something important."
    "How’s your day going so far?"
    Pause!
    Introduce purpose "I’ve noticed something that concerns me, and I want to discuss it to support you."
    "Do you have any idea what I’d like to talk about today?"

SCENARIO DESCRIPTION

    Issue,
    "I noticed [behavior, e.g., you seemed under the influence during the ward round]."
    context,
    witnesses "Others, like [nurse/team], also observed this. Can you share what happened?"

CONVERSATION FRAMEWORK (SPIRES)

    S: Seek Information - Behavior details,
    "Can you tell me more about [behavior, e.g., why you were late]?"
    frequency,
    "How often has this happened,
    reasons and is there a reason behind it?"
      Behavior "How much and how often do you use them?"
      "Have you used any substances, like alcohol or drugs, recently?"
      Social media
      when,
      what platform?
      visibility setting?
      viralness?
      Mental health check "Have you had thoughts of harming yourself?"
      "How’s your mood been lately? Any stress or challenges?"
      Personal circumstances "Are things okay at home or with friends?"
      "Who do you live with, and how’s your support system?"
    P: Patient Safety
      Impact on work
      "Could this [behavior] affect your work, like decision-making?"
      Actions taken
      "Have you prescribed meds or made decisions today? I’ll need to review them."
    I: Insight
      Awareness
      "Do you think this [behavior] might impact patient care or your role?"
      Willingness to change
      "Have you considered cutting down or addressing this issue?"
    R: Red Flags
    "I need to check for serious concerns to keep everyone safe."
      Substance use at work
      "Have you used any substances before or during work hours?"
      Confidentiality breach
      "Have you shared patient info, like on social media, without consent?"
      Patient safety incidents
      "Are you aware of any incidents caused by [behavior]?"
    E: Escalate
      Senior involvement
      "I’ll need to inform a senior to ensure we handle this professionally."
      Consequences
      "You may need to speak with a manager or take a test, depending on the issue."
    S: Support
      Resources
      "You can talk to your GP or supervisor for confidential help."
      Follow-up
      "I’m here to support you, and we’ll check in again soon."
      Immediate actions "How will you get home safely today?"
      "For now, please avoid clinical duties until we resolve this."
      Long-term support "Let’s schedule a follow-up to see how you’re doing."
      "Consider a time-management course or counseling if needed."

NOTES

    Key outcomes, commitments "I’ll document this and share next steps with you."
    "Let’s summarize what we’ve agreed on."
