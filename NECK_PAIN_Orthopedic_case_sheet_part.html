III. HISTORY OF PRESENT ILLNESS (HPI)

    MECHANISM OF INJURY (If trauma/whiplash)

    • Date/time of injury:
    • Mechanism: (MVA, fall, sports, assault)
    • For MVA:
      • Speed and direction of impact
      • Position in vehicle
      • Seatbelt/airbag use
      • Headrest position
    • Immediate symptoms: (pain location, headache, dizziness, LOC)
    • Initial treatment: (collar, ER visit, imaging)
  1. SITE (S):
    1. Exact location of pain: (C-spine level, unilateral/bilateral)
    2. Pain radiates to: (shoulder, arm, head, between shoulder blades)
    3. Worse with movement? (flexion, extension, rotation, lateral bending)
  2. ONSET (O):
    1. Sudden (trauma) or gradual onset?
    2. If trauma, could you move neck immediately after?
    3. If whiplash, when did pain start post-injury?
    4. Any prior episodes?
  3. DURATION (D):
    1. Acute (<6w), subacute (6-12w), or chronic (>12w)?
    2. Constant or intermittent?
    3. Progressive or stable?
  4. CHARACTER (C):
    1. Quality: (aching, sharp, burning, stabbing, throbbing)
    2. Severity: (0-10 scale)
    3. Morning stiffness duration: (minutes/hours)
    4. Any "clicks" or "pops" with movement?
  5. AGGRAVATING/RELIEVING (A):
    1. Worse with: (movement, certain positions, stress)
    2. Better with: (rest, heat, ice, medications)
    3. Any position that relieves pain?
  6. RED FLAGS (R):
    1. Trauma (especially if age >50, osteoporosis, steroid use)
    2. Neurological symptoms: (arm/hand weakness, numbness, clumsiness)
    3. Constitutional symptoms: (fever, weight loss, night sweats)
    4. History of cancer or immunosuppression
    5. Severe, unrelenting pain
  7. ASSOCIATED SYMPTOMS (A):
    1. Headache (occipital, tension-type, migraine)
    2. Arm pain/radicular symptoms
    3. Paresthesias/numbness/weakness in extremities
    4. Dizziness/vertigo
    5. Visual disturbances
    6. Difficulty swallowing
    7. Jaw pain/TMJ symptoms
  8. TREATMENT (T):
    1. Medications: (NSAIDs, muscle relaxants, opioids, response)
    2. Physical therapy: (when, duration, response)
    3. Injections: (type, location, response)
    4. Immobilization: (collar use, duration)
  9. WHIPLASH-SPECIFIC (WAD):
    1. Grade I: Neck pain/stiffness only
    2. Grade II: Neck symptoms + musculoskeletal signs
    3. Grade III: Neck symptoms + neurological signs
    4. Grade IV: Neck symptoms + fracture/dislocation
    5. Delayed onset of symptoms? (hours/days)
    6. Post-concussive symptoms?
  10. FUNCTIONAL IMPACT (F):
    1. Work limitations
    2. ADL difficulties: (driving, reading, computer use)
    3. Sleep disturbances
    4. Exercise tolerance
