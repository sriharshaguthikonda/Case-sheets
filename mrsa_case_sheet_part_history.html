MRSA Infection

1ST COMPLAINT: MRSA INFECTION

  • O: Onset "When did he first develop symptoms like fever, cough, or increased breathlessness?"
    • Date and time of first symptoms
    • Sudden vs gradual onset
  • C: Character "Can you describe his symptoms – does he have fevers, chills, sweats, or cough with sputum? What colour and amount?"
    • Fever pattern (continuous/intermittent)
    • Cough characteristics
    • Sputum description
    • Associated dyspnea severity
  • R: Risk factors & Sources "Has he had any wounds, IV lines, catheters, or recent procedures that could be sources of MRSA?"
    • Skin lesions or ulcers
    • Indwelling devices
    • Recent surgeries or hospital admissions
  • H: History of previous infections "Has he ever had MRSA or other antibiotic-resistant infections before?"
    • Past MRSA colonization or infection
    • Previous antibiotic treatments
  • A: Associated symptoms "Apart from cough or fever, has he experienced any new pain, confusion, or changes in skin around the nose or nares?"
    • Skin redness or discharge
    • Altered consciousness or confusion
    • General malaise or weakness
  • A: Allergies "Does he have any known allergies to antibiotics or other medications?"
    • Penicillin allergy
    • Other antibiotic reactions
  • C: Comorbidities & Immunosuppression "Does he have diabetes, kidney issues, or take steroids/immunosuppressants?"
    • Diabetes mellitus
    • Chronic kidney disease
    • Immunosuppressive therapies
  • T: Timing "Are his symptoms continuous or do they come and go? Any pattern?"
    • Continuous vs intermittent
    • Time of day variation
  • S: Severity "On a scale of 0–10, how severe are his symptoms – like breathlessness or fever?"
    • Impact on daily activities
    • Need for additional oxygen or support
