MRSA Infection

1ST COMPLAINT: MRSA INFECTION

    O: Onset
    • Date and time of first symptoms
    • Sudden vs gradual onset
    "When did he first develop symptoms like fever, cough, or increased breathlessness?"
    C: Character
    • Fever pattern (continuous/intermittent)
    • Cough characteristics
    • Sputum description
    • Associated dyspnea severity
    "Can you describe his symptoms – does he have fevers, chills, sweats, or cough with sputum? What colour and amount?"
    R: Risk factors & Sources
    • Skin lesions or ulcers
    • Indwelling devices
    • Recent surgeries or hospital admissions
    "Has he had any wounds, IV lines, catheters, or recent procedures that could be sources of MRSA?"
    H: History of previous infections
    • Past MRSA colonization or infection
    • Previous antibiotic treatments
    "Has he ever had MRSA or other antibiotic-resistant infections before?"
    A: Associated symptoms
    • Skin redness or discharge
    • Altered consciousness or confusion
    • General malaise or weakness
    "Apart from cough or fever, has he experienced any new pain, confusion, or changes in skin around the nose or nares?"
    A: Allergies
    • Penicillin allergy
    • Other antibiotic reactions
    "Does he have any known allergies to antibiotics or other medications?"
    C: Comorbidities & Immunosuppression
    • Diabetes mellitus
    • Chronic kidney disease
    • Immunosuppressive therapies
    "Does he have diabetes, kidney issues, or take steroids/immunosuppressants?"
    T: Timing
    • Continuous vs intermittent
    • Time of day variation
    "Are his symptoms continuous or do they come and go? Any pattern?"
    S: Severity
    • Impact on daily activities
    • Need for additional oxygen or support
    "On a scale of 0–10, how severe are his symptoms – like breathlessness or fever?"
