TEACHING SUBCUTANEOUS HEPARIN INJECTION

A. SCENARIO & INITIAL APPROACH (Video 0:21)

  • Scenario: You (FY2 doctor) need to teach a third-year medical student (Larry) how to administer a subcutaneous heparin injection. The student should not perform the procedure themselves in this scenario. (Video 0:21-0:33)
  • Standard Teaching Station Format: (Video 0:33-0:35)
    • Build rapport.
    • Assess the student's existing knowledge.
    • Understand what they specifically want to learn.
  • Indications for Subcutaneous Injections (Examples): (Video 0:39-0:47)
    • Insulin for diabetes.
    • Heparin (e.g., for anticoagulation).

B. EQUIPMENT REQUIRED (Video 0:49-0:50)

  • Gloves and Apron. (Video 0:54)
  • Equipment tray. (Video 0:56)
  • Syringes. (Video 0:56)
  • Needles (Important Point - Two Needles): (Video 0:56-1:01)
    • One needle for drawing up the medication from the vial/bottle. (Video 1:08-1:11)
    • A different, new needle for injecting the medication into the patient. (Video 1:11-1:19)
    • After drawing up, the first needle is discarded into the sharps bin, and the new injection needle is attached to the syringe. (Video 1:19-1:29)
  • Gauze/Cotton swab. (Video 1:31)
  • Sharps container. (Video 1:35)
  • Medication to be administered (check name and expiry date). (Video 1:35-1:37, 1:40-1:45)
  • Patient's prescription chart. (Video 1:37-1:38)

C. APPROPRIATE SITES FOR SUBCUTANEOUS INJECTION (Video 2:01)

  • Abdomen: (Video 2:07-2:09)
    • Avoid injecting within a 2-inch radius around the umbilicus (belly button). Otherwise, the abdomen is a suitable site. (Video 2:09-2:18)
  • Upper outer aspect of the arm. (Video 2:18-2:22)
  • Outer aspect of the upper thigh. (Video 2:22-2:26)
  • Upper buttock. (Video 2:26)

D. CONSIDERATIONS FOR REPEATED INJECTIONS & SITE ROTATION (Video 2:31)

  • If giving repeated injections (e.g., daily insulin): (Video 2:34-2:38)
    • Advise using different sites for each injection (site rotation). (Video 2:41-2:44, 2:50-2:58)
    • Do not use a site if it is scarred, inflamed, irritated, or bruised. (Video 2:44-2:50)

E. INJECTION TECHNIQUE (Video 3:02)

  • Hold the syringe like a pen. (Video 3:05-3:08)
  • Cleaning the Site: (Video 3:10-3:15)
    • For subcutaneous injections, cleaning the area with an alcohol swab is not always mandatory, but can be done. It's acceptable if not cleaned. (Video 3:15-3:28)
  • Pinching the Skin: Pinch the skin at the injection site with the thumb and index finger. (Video 3:28-3:32)
  • Injecting the Medication: Insert the needle into the pinched skin.
  • Aspiration (Pulling the Plunger): (Video 3:32-3:38)
    • For subcutaneous injections, it is not necessary to aspirate (pull back the plunger) before injecting. (Video 3:44-3:50)
    • This is because there are no major blood vessels in the subcutaneous tissue. (Video 3:50-3:53)
    • Aspiration is typically done for intramuscular (IM) injections to ensure a major blood vessel hasn't been entered. (Video 4:01-4:12)
  • Simply push the plunger to administer the medication. (Video 3:52-3:56)

F. POST-INJECTION PROCEDURE (Video 3:58)

  • Withdraw the needle.
  • Immediately dispose of the needle and syringe into the sharps bin. (Video 4:01-4:23)
  • Apply a sticker/dressing to the site if needed (though often just gentle pressure with gauze is sufficient). (Video 4:23-4:28)
  • Document the administration in the patient's file. (Video 4:28-4:34)

ADMINISTERING SUBCUTANEOUS INJECTIONS: STEP-BY-STEP GUIDE

A. EQUIPMENT PREPARATION (Video 0:22)

  • Ensure the kidney tray is ready with all necessary items. (Video 0:22-0:25)
  • Medication Checks: (Video 0:29)
    • Confirm the type of medication. (Video 0:31)
    • Confirm the dose of medication. (Video 0:31)
    • Confirm the route of medication (subcutaneous). (Video 0:33)
    • Check the expiry date of the medication. (Video 0:35)
  • Needles (Two Needles Required):
    • Drawing-up needle: Used to withdraw medication from the vial. (Video 0:37)
    • Injection needle: A separate, new needle used to administer the injection to the patient. (Video 0:40-0:44)
  • Syringe. (Video 0:44-0:46)
  • Gauze piece. (Video 0:46-0:48)
  • Sharps bin (yellow bin mentioned). (Video 1:11-1:19)

B. DRAWING UP MEDICATION (Video 0:48)

  • Attach the drawing-up needle to the syringe.
  • Withdraw the required dose of medication from the vial. (Video 0:52)
  • Change Needles: After drawing up, carefully remove the drawing-up needle and dispose of it immediately into the sharps bin. (Video 1:06-1:19)
  • Attach the new, sterile injection needle to the syringe.

C. PATIENT PREPARATION & SITE SELECTION (Video 1:31)

  • Confirm Patient Identity: (Video 1:37-1:43)
    • Ask for name (e.g., "Are you Mr. John Smith?"). (Video 1:43-1:46)
    • Confirm age or Date of Birth (e.g., "Could you please confirm your age for me? 27"). (Video 1:46-1:52)
  • Check for Allergies: "Are you allergic to any medication?" (Patient: "No"). (Video 1:52-1:54)
  • Appropriate Injection Sites for Subcutaneous Injection: (Video 1:54-1:58)
    • Abdomen: Avoid injecting within a 2-inch radius around the umbilicus. (Video 2:01-2:04)
    • Upper outer aspect of the arm. (Video 2:09) (Demonstrated site in video Video 2:19-2:22)
    • Outer aspect of the upper thigh. (Video 2:13)
    • Upper buttock. (Video 2:16)

D. INJECTION TECHNIQUE (Video 2:22)

  • Remove Air Bubbles: Ensure there are no air bubbles in the syringe before injecting. (Video 2:28-2:32)
  • Cleaning the Site: For subcutaneous injections, cleaning the area with an alcohol swab is not strictly necessary. Direct injection is acceptable. (Video 2:42-2:50)
  • Pinch the Skin: Using the thumb and index finger, gently pinch a fold of skin at the selected injection site. (Video 2:50-2:54)
  • Angle of Injection: Insert the needle at a 45 to 90-degree angle into the subcutaneous tissue. (Video 2:54-3:00)
  • Aspiration: It is not necessary to aspirate (pull back the plunger) for subcutaneous injections, as there are no large blood vessels in this tissue layer. (Video 3:02-3:13)
  • Inject Medication: Slowly and steadily inject the medication. (Video 3:13-3:16)
  • Withdraw Needle: Remove the needle after injection. (Video 3:16)

E. POST-INJECTION PROCEDURE (Video 3:20)

  • Sharps Disposal: Immediately dispose of the used needle and syringe into the sharps bin. (Video 3:20-3:23)
  • Apply Gauze: Offer the patient a gauze piece to hold over the injection site if needed. (Video 3:23-3:30)
  • Thank the patient. (Video 3:30-3:32)

MASTERING INTRAMUSCULAR (IM) INJECTIONS: STEP-BY-STEP GUIDE

A. EQUIPMENT PREPARATION (Video 0:21)

  • Medication Checks: (Video 0:27)
    • Confirm the type of medication. (Video 0:28)
    • Confirm the dose of medication. (Video 0:30)
    • Confirm the route (intramuscular). (Video 0:31)
    • Check the expiry date. (Video 0:31)
  • Syringe and Needles:
    • Syringe. (Video 0:34)
    • Drawing-up needle (to withdraw medication from vial). (Video 0:37)
    • Injection needle (a separate, new needle for administering to the patient). (Video 0:37-0:40)
  • Gauze piece. (Video 0:40)
  • Alcohol swab. (Video 0:42)
  • Sharps bin (essential for needle disposal). (Video 0:45-0:48)

B. DRAWING UP MEDICATION (Video 0:48)

  • Attach the drawing-up needle to the syringe.
  • Withdraw the required dose of medication from the vial. (Video 0:52)
  • Change Needles: After drawing up, carefully remove the drawing-up needle and dispose of it immediately into the sharps bin (yellow bin mentioned). (Video 1:06-1:18)
  • Attach the new, sterile injection needle to the syringe.

C. PATIENT PREPARATION & SITE SELECTION (Video 1:32)

  • Confirm Patient Identity: (Video 1:37-1:42)
    • Ask for name (e.g., "Are you Mr. John Smith?"). (Video 1:42-1:45)
    • Confirm age or Date of Birth (e.g., "Could you please confirm your age once for me? 27"). (Video 1:45-1:51)
  • Check for Allergies: "Are you allergic to any medication?" (Patient: "No"). (Video 1:51-1:54)
  • Appropriate IM Injection Site (Deltoid Muscle - example given): (Video 1:54-1:57)
    • Locate the lower edge of the acromial process (bony tip of the shoulder). (Video 2:00-2:03)
    • The injection site is approximately 2.5 cm (or 2-3 finger-breadths) below this point, into the bulk of the deltoid muscle. (Video 2:03-2:06)

D. INJECTION TECHNIQUE (Video 2:11)

  • Clean the Site: Clean the selected injection site with an alcohol swab and allow it to air dry. (Video 2:19-2:26)
  • Skin Traction (Z-track or Tautening): Gently stretch the skin taut at the injection site (demonstrated by pulling the skin slightly). (Video 2:29-2:34)
  • Warn the Patient: Inform the patient they will feel a "sharp scratch." (Video 2:34-2:38)
  • Angle of Injection: Insert the needle at a 75 to 90-degree angle to the skin, into the muscle. (Video 2:26-2:29)
  • Aspiration: (Video 2:41-2:46)
    • After inserting the needle, gently pull back on the plunger (aspirate) for a few seconds to check if the needle has entered a blood vessel. (Video 2:44-2:49)
    • If no blood appears in the syringe, proceed with injection.
    • If blood appears, withdraw the needle, discard it safely, and prepare a new injection.
  • Inject Medication: Slowly and steadily inject the medication into the muscle. (Video 2:49-2:55)
  • Withdraw Needle: Quickly and smoothly withdraw the needle at the same angle it was inserted. (Video 3:02)

E. POST-INJECTION PROCEDURE (Video 3:02)

  • Sharps Disposal: Immediately dispose of the used needle and syringe into the sharps bin. (Video 3:02-3:05)
  • Apply Gauze: Offer the patient an alcohol swab or gauze piece to apply gentle pressure to the injection site. (Video 3:05-3:11)
  • Thank the patient. (Video 3:11-3:13)
