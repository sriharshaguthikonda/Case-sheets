Cardiovascular System

Amlodipine (Calcium Channel Blocker)
  • Ankle swelling (peripheral edema)
  • Flushing
  • Headache
  • Dizziness
  • Palpitations
  • Gingival hyperplasia (gum swelling)
Aspirin (Antiplatelet)
  • GI irritation
  • Dyspepsia
  • Peptic ulcers
  • Increased risk of bleeding
  • Tinnitus (in high doses)
  • Bronchospasm in sensitive asthmatics
Atorvastatin (Statin)
  • Myalgia (muscle pain)
  • Myopathy
  • Headache
  • GI disturbances
  • Deranged Liver Function Tests (LFTs)
  • Rare: Rhabdomyolysis
Bisoprolol (Beta-blocker)
  • Fatigue
  • Dizziness
  • Bradycardia (slow heart rate)
  • Cold extremities (Raynaud's phenomenon)
  • Bronchospasm in asthmatics
  • Erectile dysfunction
Clopidogrel (Antiplatelet)
  • Increased risk of bleeding, bruising
  • Dyspepsia
  • Abdominal pain
  • Diarrhea
  • Rash
Furosemide (Loop Diuretic)
  • Dehydration, hypotension
  • Electrolyte imbalance (hypokalemia, hyponatremia)
  • Gout precipitation
  • Ototoxicity (hearing loss) in high doses/rapid IV infusion
Ramipril (ACE Inhibitor)
  • Dry, persistent cough
  • Hyperkalemia (high potassium)
  • First-dose hypotension
  • Angioedema (rare but serious)
Nitrofurantoin (Antibiotic)
  • Nausea
  • Vomiting
  • Pulmonary fibrosis (chronic use)
  • Hepatitis (rare)
Trimethoprim (Antibiotic)
  • Rash
  • Can cause a benign rise in creatinine (competes for secretion)
  • Bone marrow suppression (with long-term use or in folate deficiency)
Amiodarone (Antiarrhythmic)
  • Pulmonary fibrosis (can be fatal)
  • Thyroid dysfunction (hypo- or hyperthyroidism)
  • Deranged LFTs / Hepatitis
  • Corneal microdeposits
  • Peripheral neuropathy
  • Photosensitivity / Slate-grey skin
Doxazosin (Alpha-blocker)
  • Postural hypotension (especially first dose)
  • Dizziness
  • Syncope (fainting)
  • Nasal congestion
Spironolactone (Potassium-sparing Diuretic)
  • Hyperkalemia
  • Gynaecomastia (breast tenderness/enlargement in men)
  • Menstrual irregularities
Warfarin (Anticoagulant)
  • Bleeding (major and minor)
  • Skin necrosis (rare)
  • Numerous drug and food interactions

Respiratory System

Beclometasone (Inhaled Corticosteroid)
  • Oral candidiasis (thrush)
  • Hoarse voice (dysphonia)
  • Systemic effects with high doses (rare)
Carbocisteine (Mucolytic)
  • GI intolerance
  • Skin rash
Montelukast (Leukotriene Receptor Antagonist)
  • Headache
  • Abdominal pain
  • Neuropsychiatric effects (agitation, sleep disturbances)
Salmeterol (Long-Acting Beta Agonist)
  • Tachycardia
  • Palpitations
  • Tremor
  • Muscle cramps
Salbutamol (Short-Acting Beta Agonist)
  • Tachycardia (fast heart rate)
  • Palpitations
  • Fine tremor (especially in hands)
  • Headache
  • Anxiety
  • Hypokalemia (with high doses)
Tiotropium (Long-Acting Muscarinic Antagonist)
  • Dry mouth (very common)
  • Constipation
  • Blurred vision
  • Urinary retention

Gastrointestinal System

Lactulose (Osmotic Laxative)
  • Flatulence
  • Abdominal cramps
  • Bloating
  • Diarrhea
Mebeverine (Antispasmodic)
  • Rash
  • Angioedema (rare)
Metoclopramide (Antiemetic)
  • Drowsiness
  • Restlessness
  • Extrapyramidal side effects (dystonia, tardive dyskinesia)
Omeprazole (Proton Pump Inhibitor)
  • Headache
  • GI disturbances (nausea, diarrhea)
  • Long-term use: Increased risk of fractures, hypomagnesemia, C. difficile infection
Ondansetron (5-HT3 Antagonist)
  • Headache
  • Constipation
  • QT prolongation (with high doses)
Cyclizine (Antihistamine Antiemetic)
  • Drowsiness
  • Dry mouth
  • Urinary retention
Loperamide (Antidiarrheal)
  • Constipation (intended effect, but can be excessive)
  • Abdominal cramps
  • Dizziness
Mesalazine (5-ASA)
  • Nausea
  • Diarrhea
  • Headache
  • Interstitial nephritis (rare)
Senna (Stimulant Laxative)
  • Abdominal cramps
  • Melanosis coli (harmless bowel pigmentation with chronic use)

Endocrine System

Dapagliflozin (SGLT2 Inhibitor)
  • Genital fungal infections (thrush)
  • Urinary tract infections
  • Euglycemic Diabetic Ketoacidosis (DKA) (rare but serious)
  • Increased urination
Gliclazide (Sulfonylurea)
  • Hypoglycemia (low blood sugar)
  • Weight gain
  • GI upset
Levothyroxine (Thyroid Hormone Replacement)
  • Symptoms of hyperthyroidism if dose is too high (tachycardia, anxiety, tremor, weight loss)
Metformin (Biguanide)
  • GI side effects (diarrhea, nausea, abdominal pain)
  • Lactic acidosis (rare but serious, especially with renal impairment)
  • Vitamin B12 deficiency (long-term use)
Prednisolone (Systemic Corticosteroid)
  • Insomnia
  • Mood changes
  • Increased appetite
  • Hyperglycemia
  • Long-term: Osteoporosis, weight gain, Cushing's syndrome, adrenal suppression, immunosuppression
Insulin (e.g., Aspart, Glargine)
  • Hypoglycemia
  • Lipohypertrophy at injection sites
  • Weight gain
Sitagliptin (DPP-4 Inhibitor)
  • Headache
  • Pancreatitis (rare)
  • Joint pain (arthralgia)

CNS & Psychiatry

Amitriptyline (Tricyclic Antidepressant)
  • Drowsiness
  • Dry mouth
  • Constipation
  • Blurred vision
  • Urinary retention
  • Postural hypotension
  • Cardiotoxicity in overdose
Diazepam (Benzodiazepine)
  • Drowsiness
  • Sedation
  • Ataxia
  • Dependence and withdrawal symptoms
  • Anterograde amnesia
Levetiracetam (Antiepileptic)
  • Somnolence
  • Asthenia (weakness)
  • Mood changes (irritability, agitation, depression)
Lithium (Mood Stabilizer)
  • Narrow therapeutic index - requires regular monitoring
  • Fine tremor
  • Nephrogenic Diabetes Insipidus (polyuria, polydipsia)
  • Hypothyroidism
  • Nausea
  • Diarrhea
  • Worsening of psoriasis or acne
  • Signs of Toxicity (coarse tremor, ataxia, confusion, seizures)
Morphine (Opioid Analgesic)
  • Drowsiness
  • Respiratory depression
  • Nausea
  • Vomiting
  • Constipation
  • Dependence
Olanzapine (Atypical Antipsychotic)
  • Significant weight gain
  • Metabolic syndrome (hyperglycemia, dyslipidemia)
  • Sedation
  • Postural hypotension
Sertraline (SSRI)
  • Nausea
  • Diarrhea
  • GI upset (common initially)
  • Insomnia or somnolence
  • Sexual dysfunction
  • Increased anxiety initially
Gabapentin / Pregabalin (GABA Analogue)
  • Dizziness
  • Somnolence (sleepiness)
  • Peripheral edema
  • Weight gain
Lamotrigine (Antiepileptic)
  • Severe rash (Stevens-Johnson Syndrome - SJS) - requires slow titration
  • Dizziness
  • Headache
Sodium Valproate (Antiepileptic)
  • Nausea
  • Stomach pain
  • Hepatotoxicity / Pancreatitis (rare)
  • Thrombocytopenia
  • Highly teratogenic - requires pregnancy prevention programme

Infectious Diseases

Allopurinol (Xanthine Oxidase Inhibitor)
  • Can precipitate acute gout attack upon initiation
  • Rash
  • Severe hypersensitivity reactions (e.g., SJS/TEN) (rare)
Amoxicillin (Penicillin Antibiotic)
  • Rash (non-allergic rash common in glandular fever)
  • Diarrhea
  • Allergic reactions (from mild rash to anaphylaxis)
Clarithromycin (Macrolide Antibiotic)
  • GI upset (nausea, vomiting, diarrhea)
  • Taste disturbance
  • QT prolongation
Ciprofloxacin (Quinolone Antibiotic)
  • Nausea
  • Diarrhea
  • Tendon damage/rupture (especially Achilles)
  • Seizures (lowers threshold)
Co-amoxiclav (Penicillin + Beta-lactamase inhibitor)
  • Diarrhea (more common than with amoxicillin alone)
  • Cholestatic jaundice (can be delayed)
  • Allergic reactions
Doxycycline (Tetracycline Antibiotic)
  • Photosensitivity (increased risk of sunburn)
  • GI upset
  • Esophagitis (advise to take with water, sitting upright)
  • Tooth discoloration in children < 8
Fluconazole (Antifungal)
  • Headache
  • Abdominal pain
  • Deranged LFTs
Metronidazole (Antibiotic)
  • Disulfiram-like reaction with alcohol (severe nausea/vomiting)
  • Metallic taste in mouth
  • GI upset

Pain & Rheumatology

Co-codamol (Paracetamol & Codeine)
  • Constipation (very common)
  • Drowsiness
  • Dizziness
  • Nausea
  • Risk of opioid dependence
Ibuprofen (NSAID)
  • GI irritation
  • Dyspepsia
  • Peptic ulcers
  • Renal impairment
  • Increased risk of cardiovascular events (long-term use)
  • Bronchospasm in asthmatics
Methotrexate (DMARD)
  • Nausea
  • Mucositis
  • Myelosuppression (bone marrow suppression)
  • Hepatotoxicity (liver damage)
  • Pulmonary fibrosis (rare)
Colchicine (Anti-inflammatory for Gout)
  • Diarrhea (very common, dose-limiting)
  • Nausea
  • Vomiting
Hydroxychloroquine (DMARD)
  • Retinopathy (requires regular eye screening)
  • Nausea
  • Rash
Tramadol (Atypical Opioid Analgesic)
  • Nausea
  • Dizziness
  • Drowsiness
  • Lowers seizure threshold
Paracetamol
  • Generally very few side effects at therapeutic doses
  • Hepatotoxicity in overdose

Urology, Gynae & Obstetrics

Combined Oral Contraceptive Pill
  • Increased risk of VTE (DVT/PE) and stroke
  • Hypertension
  • Headaches, mood changes
  • Breakthrough bleeding
Solifenacin (Antimuscarinic for OAB)
  • Dry mouth
  • Constipation
  • Blurred vision
  • Drowsiness
Tamsulosin (Alpha-blocker for BPH)
  • Postural hypotension, dizziness, syncope
  • Ejaculation disorders
  • Nasal congestion
Tranexamic Acid (Antifibrinolytic)
  • Nausea
  • Diarrhea
  • Increased risk of VTE (use with caution)
