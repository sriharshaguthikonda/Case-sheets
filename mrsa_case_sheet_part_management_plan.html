MANAGEMENT PLAN

Urgency

  • Immediate isolation and contact precautions
  • Start IV antibiotic therapy without delay

Initial Assessment & Investigations

  • Review MRSA-positive nasal swab result
  • Obtain blood cultures prior to antibiotic administration
  • FBC, CRP, U&Es, LFTs to assess infection severity and organ function
  • Chest X-ray to evaluate for pneumonia or empyema

Treatment Plan

  1. Antibiotic Therapy
    • IV Vancomycin (dose adjusted for renal function)
    • Consider Linezolid if vancomycin contraindicated or MIC>2 µg/mL
    • Decolonisation: nasal Mupirocin BID + daily Chlorhexidine body wash ×5 days
  2. Monitor vancomycin levels and renal function daily

Infection Control Measures

  • Maintain strict PPE (gloves, gown) and hand hygiene
  • Notify Infection Control team and complete MRSA surveillance form
  • Complete three negative swabs before de-isolation per local policy

Supportive Care

  • Bronchodilators and steroids for COPD exacerbation
  • Oxygen therapy and respiratory physiotherapy

Specialist Referrals

  • Infectious Diseases consultation for antibiotic guidance
  • Respiratory team review for COPD management

Patient Education & Safety Netting

  • Teach-back: ask wife to explain the plan and precautions
  • Red flags: fever >38°C, increasing breathlessness, confusion—seek urgent review or call 999
  • Provide MRSA information leaflet for home reference

Follow-Up

  • Microbiology review at 48–72 hours for antibiotic adjustment
  • Plan outpatient review with Infectious Diseases post-de-isolation

Documentation

  • Record plan in contemporaneous medical notes
  • Complete MRSA surveillance notification

Summary of the Plan

  • Doctor repeats key steps to confirm understanding
