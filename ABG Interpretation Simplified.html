ARTERIAL BLOOD GAS (ABG) SAMPLING: PROCEDURE AND CONSIDERATIONS

A. SCENARIO INTRODUCTION & INITIAL PATIENT INTERACTION (Video 0:20)

  • Typical Scenario: Patient admitted (e.g., for COPD), and ABG sampling is requested. (Video 0:24-0:31)
  • Primary Task: Perform ABG sampling as instructed. (Video 0:31-0:38)
  • Brief Patient Interaction (Optional for Rapport): (Video 0:38-0:52)
    • If context is known (e.g., COPD patient), ask a few related questions:
      • When admitted?
      • Symptoms at admission?
      • Current feeling?
      • Inhaler compliance?
    • Avoid excessive detailing if the primary task is clearly sampling. (Video 0:52-1:01)

B. PRE-SAMPLING CHECKS & CONTRAINDICATIONS (Video 1:03)

  • Gather all necessary instruments. (Video 1:03-1:05)
  • Ask about Contraindications: (Video 1:05-1:12)
    • Fistula: Do not perform ABG on an arm with an existing AV fistula. (Video 1:15-1:19)
    • Planned Fistula: Avoid the arm where a fistula is planned. (Video 1:22-1:28)
      • Reiterate: Confirm no fistula or planned fistula in the chosen arm. (Video 1:30-1:37)
    • Trauma: Inquire about any recent trauma to the arm. (Video 1:43-1:48)
    • Site Selection: If no contraindications, any arm can be used, but the non-dominant arm is preferable. (Video 1:50-1:57)

C. MODIFIED ALLEN'S TEST (Video 2:00)

  • This test must be performed before radial artery sampling. (Video 2:02-2:06)
  • Demonstration: Can be performed on a suitable mannequin or demonstrated to the patient/examiner. (Video 2:09-2:28)
  • Purpose: To ensure adequate collateral circulation to the hand via the ulnar artery in case the radial artery is compromised during or after the procedure. (Video 2:35-2:58)
  • Procedure: (Video 3:00-3:49)
    1. Apply pressure with fingers over both the radial and ulnar arteries at the wrist. (Video 3:03-3:08)
    2. Ask the patient to make a fist (or clench and open the hand several times) to exsanguinate the hand. (Video 3:08-3:10)
    3. Maintain pressure; the hand will appear pale/blanched. (Video 3:12-3:17)
    4. Ask the patient to open their hand (palm should be blanched). (Video 3:19-3:24)
    5. Release pressure from the ulnar artery ONLY, while maintaining pressure on the radial artery. (Video 3:24-3:32)
    6. Observe for return of color (reperfusion) to the hand. This should occur within 7 seconds. (Video 3:32-3:39)
    7. A positive test (color returns quickly) indicates adequate ulnar collateral circulation, making it safe to proceed with radial artery puncture. (Video 3:41-3:49)

D. ABG SAMPLING TECHNIQUE NOTES (Video 3:57)

  • No Tourniquet Needed: Unlike venous sampling, a tourniquet is not used for ABG. (Video 3:57-4:00)
  • Syringe Filling: The ABG syringe is designed to fill automatically due to arterial pressure/pulsation. (Video 4:00-4:08)

E. POST-SAMPLING PROCEDURE & DOCUMENTATION (Video 4:14)

  • Sample Handling:
    • Inform the examiner: "I will take this sample myself to the ABG machine and get the results." (This is different from other blood samples sent to the lab). (Video 4:17-4:29)
    • ABG machines are commonly found in A&E, ICU, and respiratory units. (Video 4:32-4:42)
  • Contingency for Non-Functional Machine: (Video 4:49-4:52)
    • "If the ABG machine is not working, I will take the sample to another ward (e.g., ICCU, A&E with a working machine)." (Video 4:52-4:56)
    • "Alternatively, I can keep this sample in an ice box for some time to preserve it." (Video 4:56-5:02)
  • Oxygen Status: Note if the patient is on oxygen, as this information is often required by the ABG machine. (Video 5:04-5:12)
  • Documentation: "Once I get the report, I will put it in the patient's file and document it." (Video 5:12-5:20)

ARTERIAL BLOOD GAS (ABG) TEST: PROCEDURE GUIDE

A. PATIENT EXPLANATION & PREPARATION (Video 0:09)

  • Explain the Procedure: (Video 0:12-0:20)
    • "I'm going to take blood from one of your arteries, preferably the radial artery (in the wrist)."
  • Manage Expectations:
    • "It's going to feel like a sharp scratch." (Video 0:23-0:24)
    • "I may have to do it once more if I'm unable to get it in my first attempt." (Video 0:28-0:32)
  • Arm Preference & Contraindications: (Video 0:32)
    • Ask for arm preference (left or right). (Video 0:35-0:37)
    • Ask about AV fistula: "Do you have any kind of fistula, or are we planning for any kind of fistula in the next few days?" - Avoid that arm. (Video 0:42-0:52)
  • Modified Allen's Test: Must be performed before radial artery puncture. (Video 0:52-0:54)
    • Procedure Explanation: (Video 0:59)
      1. Tell the patient to make a fist. (Video 1:02-1:04)
      2. Occlude (block) both the radial and ulnar arteries at the wrist. (Video 1:07-1:12)
      3. With arteries occluded, the hand will become blanched (pale) as blood flow is restricted. (Video 1:14-1:19)
      4. Tell the patient to open the fist; the hand should appear blanched. (Video 1:19-1:23)
      5. Release pressure from the ulnar artery ONLY, while maintaining pressure on the radial artery. (Video 1:25-1:28)
      6. Observe for the return of blood supply (color) to the hand. This indicates collateral circulation is working. (Video 1:28-1:34)
      7. If color returns promptly, it's safe to proceed with the ABG from the radial artery. (Video 1:34-1:37)

B. EQUIPMENT & SITE PREPARATION (Video 1:41)

  • Gathered Equipment (shown on tray): (Video 1:50)
    • ABG syringe (with heparin).
    • Gauze piece.
    • Alcohol swab.
    • Sharps bin. (Video 1:57)
  • Syringe Preparation: (Video 2:02)
    • Open ABG syringe packaging. The syringe often comes with a cap/stopper. (Video 2:07-2:10)
  • Locate Pulse & Clean Site:
    • Palpate the radial pulse with three fingers, identifying the point of maximal pulsation with the middle finger. (Video 2:18-2:29)
    • Clean the identified site with an alcohol swab in a circular motion. (Video 2:22-2:31)
    • Do not touch the cleaned area again. (Video 2:31-2:34)

C. ABG SAMPLING PROCEDURE (Video 2:42)

  • Hold the ABG syringe like a pen. (Video 2:42-2:47)
  • Warn the patient about the sharp scratch. (Video 2:47)
  • Insert the needle at an appropriate angle (often 30-45 degrees for radial artery) into the artery.
  • The syringe should fill on its own due to arterial pressure; do not pull the plunger. (Video 2:55-2:57)
  • Once sufficient sample is collected, withdraw the needle.
  • Immediately apply firm pressure to the puncture site with a gauze piece. (Video 3:00-3:05)

D. POST-SAMPLING NEEDLE SAFETY & SAMPLE HANDLING (Video 3:08)

  • Needle Safety:
    • Activate the needle safety mechanism if present, or carefully recap using a one-handed scoop technique if safety device is not integrated or use the provided needle lock/stopper. (Video shows a specific needle lock mechanism at Video 3:11-3:14)
    • Remove the needle from the syringe. (Video 3:16-3:19)
    • Dispose of the needle immediately into the sharps bin. (Video 3:22)
  • Cap the Syringe: Apply the cap or stopper to the syringe to prevent air exposure. (Video 3:27-3:29)
  • Label the Sample. (Video 3:29)
  • Transport to ABG Machine:
    • Take the sample yourself to the ABG machine (usually located in specific wards like respiratory, A&E, or ICU). (Video 3:32-3:42)
    • If the machine is not working, the sample can be placed in an ice box for a short period before testing. (Video 3:42-3:47)
  • Patient on Oxygen: If the patient is receiving oxygen therapy, note the FiO2 or flow rate, as this information may be needed for the ABG machine settings/interpretation. (Video 3:47-3:52)
  • Thank the Patient. (Video 3:52)
