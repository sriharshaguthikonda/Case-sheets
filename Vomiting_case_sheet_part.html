1ST COMPLAINT: VOMITING

  • S: Site of Associated Pain "Do you have any pain associated with the vomiting? If so, where is it?"
    • Epigastric pain -> Gastritis, Peptic Ulcer Disease (PUD), Pancreatitis
    • Right Upper Quadrant pain -> Cholecystitis, Biliary Colic
    • Central abdominal pain -> Bowel Obstruction, Pancreatitis
    • Severe Headache -> Raised Intracranial Pressure (ICP), Meningitis, Migraine
    • Chest Pain -> Myocardial Infarction
  • O: Onset "When did the vomiting start? Did it come on suddenly after eating something, or has it been more gradual?"
    • ACUTE: Gastroenteritis, Food Poisoning, Bowel Obstruction, Medication side-effect
    • CHRONIC: Gastroparesis, Eating disorder, Malignancy, Chronic raised ICP
    • Triggered by: Recent travel, sick contacts, new medication, alcohol binge?
  • D: Duration & Frequency "How long has this been going on? And how many times have you vomited in the last 24 hours?"
  • A/R: Aggravating & Relieving Factors "Does anything trigger the vomiting, like food or movement? Does anything make it better?"
    • Worse with food -> Obstruction, Gastritis, PUD
    • Worse with head movement -> Vestibular cause (e.g., Labyrinthitis), Vertigo
    • Relieved after vomiting -> Often seen in obstruction (temporarily)
  • C: Character of Vomitus "What does the vomit look like? Is there food in it? What color is it? Is there any blood?"
    • NON-BILIOUS (clear, contains undigested food): Suggests obstruction before the duodenum (e.g., Pyloric Stenosis)
    • BILIOUS (yellow/green): Indicates the problem is distal to the duodenum; most common type
    • HEMATEMESIS (Blood):
      • Bright Red -> Active bleed (e.g., Varices, Mallory-Weiss tear, PUD)
      • Coffee Grounds -> Altered blood (e.g., slower bleed in PUD)
    • FECULENT (smells like feces): Pathognomonic for distal bowel obstruction
  • R: Radiation (of Pain) "If you have pain, does it travel or spread anywhere?"
    • e.g., To the back (Pancreatitis) or shoulder tip (Diaphragmatic irritation)
  • A: Associated Symptoms "Along with the vomiting, have you had diarrhea, fever, headache, or any other symptoms?"
    • GI SYMPTOMS: Diarrhea (Gastroenteritis), Absolute constipation (Obstruction), Abdominal bloating/distension (Obstruction)
    • CNS SYMPTOMS: Headache, photophobia, neck stiffness (Meningitis/ICP), Vertigo, tinnitus, hearing loss (Labyrinthitis)
    • SYSTEMIC/METABOLIC: Fever, chills (Infection), Polyuria, polydipsia (DKA), Confusion (Hypercalcemia)
    • PREGNANCY: Missed period, breast tenderness
    • DEHYDRATION: Dizziness, reduced urine output, dry mouth
  • P: Progression "Since it started, has the vomiting been getting more frequent or severe?"
  • P: Pattern "Is there a pattern to it? For example, does it only happen in the mornings, or a long time after eating?"
    • EARLY MORNING VOMITING: Pregnancy, Raised ICP, Alcohol withdrawal
    • POST-PRANDIAL (after eating):
      • Immediately after -> Gastritis, PUD
      • Hours after -> Gastroparesis, Gastric outlet obstruction
  • E: Effect on Life "How has this affected you? Have you been able to keep any food or fluids down? Have you lost weight?"
  • R: Red Flags "I just need to ask some specific questions to make sure we're not missing anything serious."
    • Vomiting blood (hematemesis) or coffee grounds
    • Vomiting that looks or smells like feces (feculent)
    • Severe, unrelenting abdominal pain with abdominal distension
    • Associated severe headache, neck stiffness, or new confusion
    • Signs of severe dehydration (e.g., unable to keep fluids down, dizziness, minimal urine)
    • Associated chest pain or shortness of breath
    • Unexplained weight loss
  • S: Severity / Bother Scale "On a scale of 1 to 10, how much is this vomiting affecting you?"
