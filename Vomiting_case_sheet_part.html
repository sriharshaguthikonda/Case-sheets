1ST COMPLAINT: VOMITING

S: Site of Associated Pain
  • Epigastric pain -> Gastritis, Peptic Ulcer Disease (PUD), Pancreatitis
  • Right Upper Quadrant pain -> Cholecystitis, Biliary Colic
  • Central abdominal pain -> Bowel Obstruction, Pancreatitis
  • Severe Headache -> Raised Intracranial Pressure (ICP), Meningitis, Migraine
  • Chest Pain -> Myocardial Infarction
"Do you have any pain associated with the vomiting? If so, where is it?"
O: Onset
  • ACUTE: 
    • Gastroenteritis, Food Poisoning, Bowel Obstruction, Medication side-effect
  • CHRONIC: 
    • Gastroparesis, Eating disorder, Malignancy, Chronic raised ICP
  • Trigger
    • Recent travel, sick contacts, new medication, alcohol binge?
"When did the vomiting start? Did it come on suddenly after eating something, or has it been more gradual?"
D: Duration & Frequency
"How long has this been going on? And how many times have you vomited in the last 24 hours?"
A/R: Aggravating & Relieving Factors
  • Worse with food -> Obstruction, Gastritis, PUD
  • Worse with head movement -> Vestibular cause (e.g., Labyrinthitis), Vertigo
  • Relieved after vomiting -> Often seen in obstruction (temporarily)
  • Iron tablets?

"Does anything trigger the vomiting, like food or movement? Does anything make it better?"
C: Character of Vomitus
  • NON-BILIOUS (clear, contains undigested food):
    • Suggests obstruction before the duodenum (e.g., Pyloric Stenosis)
  • BILIOUS (yellow/green):
    • Indicates the problem is distal to the duodenum; most common type
  • HEMATEMESIS (Blood):
    • Bright Red -> Active bleed (e.g., Varices, Mallory-Weiss tear, PUD)
    • Coffee Grounds -> Altered blood (e.g., slower bleed in PUD)
  • FECULENT (smells like feces): 
    •  distal bowel obstruction
"What does the vomit look like? Is there food in it? What color is it? Is there any blood?"
R: Radiation (of Pain)
  • e.g., To the back (Pancreatitis) or shoulder tip (Diaphragmatic irritation)
"If you have pain, does it travel or spread anywhere?"
A: Associated Symptoms
  • GI SYMPTOMS: 
    • Diarrhea (Gastroenteritis), 
    • constipation (Obstruction), 
    • bloating/distension (Obstruction)
  • CNS SYMPTOMS: 
    • Meningitis
    • Headache,
    • photophobia,
    • neck stiffness (Meningitis/ICP), 
    • Labyrinthitis
    • Vertigo, tinnitus, hearing loss (Labyrinthitis)
  • SYSTEMIC/METABOLIC: Fever, chills (Infection), Polyuria, polydipsia (DKA), Confusion (Hypercalcemia)
  • PREGNANCY: Missed period, breast tenderness
  • DEHYDRATION: Dizziness, reduced urine output, dry mouth
"Along with the vomiting, have you had diarrhea, fever, headache, or any other symptoms?"
P: Progression
"Since it started, has the vomiting been getting more frequent or severe?"
P: Pattern
  • EARLY MORNING VOMITING: 
    • Pregnancy, 
    • Raised ICP, 
    • Alcohol withdrawal
  • POST-PRANDIAL (after eating):
    • Immediately after -> Gastritis, PUD
    • Hours after -> Gastroparesis, Gastric outlet obstruction
"Is there a pattern to it? For example, does it only happen in the mornings, or a long time after eating?"
E: Effect on Life
"How has this affected you? Have you been able to keep any food or fluids down? Have you lost weight?"
R: Red Flags
  • blood (hematemesis) or coffee grounds
  • Vomiting that looks or smells like feces (feculent)
  • Severe, unrelenting abdominal pain with abdominal distension
  • Associated severe 
    • headache,
    • neck stiffness, or
    • new confusion
  • Signs of severe dehydration (e.g.,
    • thirsty
    • unable to keep fluids down,
    • dry lips
    • dizziness,
    • color urine
    • minimal urine)

  • Associated chest pain or shortness of breath
  • Unexplained weight loss
"I just need to ask some specific questions to make sure we're not missing anything serious."
S: Severity / Bother Scale
"On a scale of 1 to 10, how much is this vomiting affecting you?"
