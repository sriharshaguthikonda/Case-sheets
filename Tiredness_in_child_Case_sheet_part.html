Tiredness / Lethargy in a Child

1ST COMPLAINT: "MY CHILD IS ALWAYS TIRED" / LETHARGY

S: Severity & Activity Level
  • ACTIVITY LEVEL (The Pediatric Functional Score):
    • Does the child still play? (A child who stops playing is sick).
    • Do they refuse to walk or want to be carried?
    • Are they napping more than usual for their age?
  • SCHOOL PERFORMANCE:
    • Falling asleep in class? Declining grades? Trouble concentrating?
  • SLEEP QUALITY:
    • How many hours does he sleep at night? Does he wake rested or still exhausted?
    • Difficulty falling asleep or staying asleep?
    • Snoring or pauses in breathing reported by parents (possible sleep apnea).
"Is he still playing with his friends? Does he ask to be carried more often? Have the teachers mentioned him falling asleep in class?"
O: Onset
  • ACUTE (Days):
    • Viral illness (Flu, EBV/Mono, Hepatitis).
    • DKA (Type 1 Diabetes): Rapid onset with weight loss.
  • GRADUAL (Weeks/Months):
    • Anemia (Iron deficiency, Thalassemia).
    • Hypothyroidism.
    • Leukemia/Lymphoma.
    • Depression / Bullying.
  • SINCE BIRTH/INFANCY:
    • Congenital Heart Disease.
    • Metabolic Disorders / Cystic Fibrosis.
"Did this tiredness start suddenly after a flu, or have you noticed him slowing down over the last few months?"
D: Duration
  • Acute vs Chronic:
    • Acute: days to a few weeks (often viral).
    • Chronic: > 3 months of persistent tiredness.
  • Post-viral fatigue can last weeks but should gradually improve.
  • Chronic fatigue > 3 months, or worsening over time, warrants investigation.
"How long has he been like this?"
A/R: Aggravating & Relieving Factors
  • AGGRAVATING:
    • Physical exertion (Asthma, Heart defect).
    • School days vs. Weekends (School refusal/Bullying).
    • Certain foods (Celiac disease).
    • Prolonged screen time or late bedtimes worsening next-day tiredness.
    • Post-exertional crash: severe wipe-out after minor activity (think chronic fatigue / post-viral states).
  • RELIEVING:
    • Does sleep help? (If sleep is non-restorative -> Sleep Apnea).
    • Short rests or naps improving tiredness (more functional/benign fatigue).
"Is he just as tired on the weekends as he is on school days? Does he feel better after a nap?"
C: Character (Physiological vs Psychological)
  • TRUE LETHARGY: "He just lies on the couch all day." (Organic illness).
  • EXERTIONAL INTOLERANCE: "He runs for a minute then has to sit down/squat." (Tetralogy of Fallot / Heart Failure).
  • SOMNOLENCE: Falling asleep at odd times (Sleep Apnea / Narcolepsy).
  • BOREDOM/WITHDRAWAL: "He stays in his room." (Depression).
  • BREATHLESSNESS vs TIREDNESS: "He gets tired walking up stairs" may actually mean air hunger (heart/lung cause) rather than pure low energy.
  • WEAKNESS: "He can't lift his arms / gets floppy" (neuromuscular cause).
"Is it that he wants to run but gets out of breath, or does he just have no interest in moving?"
R: Review of Systems (Growth & Development)
  • GROWTH: Failure to Thrive? (Dropping off growth centiles). This is a massive red flag in pediatrics (Celiac, Hypothyroid, Malignancy, Cardiac).
  • DEVELOPMENT: Regression of milestones? (Metabolic/CNS).
"Is he growing out of his clothes and shoes as expected? Has he lost any weight?"
A: Associated Symptoms
  • INFECTIOUS:
    • Fever, swollen glands (EBV), sore throat.
  • HEMATOLOGY (Anemia/Leukemia):
    • Pallor (Pale skin).
    • Easy bruising, bleeding gums, nosebleeds.
    • Pica (eating ice/dirt - Iron Deficiency).
  • ENDOCRINE (Type 1 Diabetes):
    • Polyuria (wetting bed again), Polydipsia, Weight loss.
  • RESPIRATORY (Sleep Apnea):
    • Snoring, mouth breathing, large tonsils.
  • GASTROINTESTINAL:
    • Chronic diarrhea, tummy pain, bloating (Celiac Disease).
  • PSYCHOSOCIAL:
    • Sadness, irritability, social withdrawal (Depression/Abuse).
"Has he been drinking a lot of water or wetting the bed recently? Have you noticed any bruises or that he looks very pale?"
P: Progression
"Is his energy getting worse every week?"
P: Pattern
  • Morning Headaches + Tiredness: Brain Tumor (ICP) or Sleep Apnea.
  • Afternoon Slump: Poor sleep or nutrition.
  • Evening Worsening: fatigue mainly towards evening can suggest neuromuscular problems (e.g., myasthenia) or simple overexertion.
  • Fluctuating Days: good days and bad days (consider autoimmune disease or chronic fatigue patterns).
"Does he wake up with headaches?"
E: Effect on life
  • Social isolation (not seeing friends).
  • Inability to participate in sports.
  • Family stress.
  • Impact on school attendance and concentration in class.
  • Effect on hobbies and usual play (e.g., stops going to clubs or activities he previously enjoyed).
"Is he missing out on fun activities because he's too tired?"
R: Red Flags
  • WEIGHT LOSS + NIGHT SWEATS + BONE PAIN: Leukemia/Lymphoma.
  • MORNING HEADACHES + VOMITING: Intracranial Tumor.
  • POLYDIPSIA + POLYURIA + WEIGHT LOSS: Type 1 Diabetes (DKA risk).
  • CYANOSIS / SQUATTING after exercise: Congenital Heart Disease.
  • FAILURE TO THRIVE / GROWTH ARREST.
  • SIGNS OF NON-ACCIDENTAL INJURY (Abuse).
"I need to ask some important safety questions. Does he wake up at night sweating? Does he complain of bone or leg pain that isn't from growing pains?"
S: Scale
  • Simple 0–10 scale (for older children): "If 0 is no tiredness and 10 is the worst you can imagine, what number would you give it?"
  • Comparison to peers/siblings. "Does he keep up with the other kids?"
"Compared to his siblings or classmates, how much energy does he have?"
