FOCUSED HISTORY – SWEATING (SODA-CRAPPERS)

Structure adapted from your prototypical case-sheet; classes follow your systems map; SODA-CRAPPERS expansion included.

  1. S – Site / Setting / Distribution "Is the sweating all over or in specific areas (armpits, palms, face, feet)? Is it one-sided? Does it happen at night, during the day, or both?"
    • Generalised vs focal (axillae/palms/soles/face) – consider NICE CKS: Hyperhidrosis & NHS: Excessive sweating.
    • Context: at rest vs exertion; ambient temperature; occupational heat exposure.
    • Night-only (night sweats) – think infection/malignancy; see NICE NG12 & NICE NG33 TB.
  2. O – Onset "When did this start? Sudden or gradual? Any trigger or illness around that time?"
    • Post-infection onset (viral/bacterial).
    • Postpartum/thyroiditis window; perimenopausal transition.
  3. D – Duration "How long has this been going on? Is it getting more frequent or severe?"
  4. A – Aggravating / R – Relieving "What brings it on or makes it worse (heat, exercise, stress, spicy foods, alcohol, caffeine)? What helps (cool room, shower, antiperspirant/medications)?"
    • Stress/anxiety; panic symptoms.
    • Gustatory triggers (spicy/acidic foods).
    • Response to topical agents (aluminium chloride), anticholinergics, or other treatments tried (per NICE CKS Hyperhidrosis).
  5. C – Character / Severity "How severe is it? Are clothes or bed-linen soaked? Do you need to change them?"
    • Drenching vs mild clamminess (helps triage urgency and differentials).
  6. R – Radiation / Distribution detail "Does it stay in one area or spread? Any asymmetry?"
  7. A – Associated Symptoms (SYSTEMS REVIEW)
    • Fever/rigors, cough ± haemoptysis, weight loss, fatigue, travel/exposure (TB, malaria) – see NICE NG33 & NHS: Malaria.
    • Unintentional weight loss, pruritus, lumps/lymph nodes, persistent night sweats (B-symptoms) – safety-net per NICE NG12.
    • Thyrotoxicosis: palpitations, tremor, heat intolerance, diarrhoea, anxiety, eye symptoms – NICE NG145.
    • Hypoglycaemia (if diabetic or on insulin/SUs): shakiness, confusion, hunger – NHS: Hypoglycaemia.
    • Perimenopause/menopause: hot flushes, irregular or stopped periods, sleep disturbance, urogenital symptoms – NICE NG23.
    • Sleep apnoea clues: loud snoring, witnessed apnoeas, unrefreshing sleep, morning headaches.
    • Panic/anxiety: racing heart, breathlessness, sense of doom.
    • Autonomic symptoms, flushing (rare endocrine causes e.g., pheochromocytoma) – see NHS: Pheochromocytoma.
  8. P – Progression "Is it getting better, worse, or staying the same? Any new symptoms since it began?"
  9. P – Pattern
    • Continuous vs intermittent; time-of-day pattern; relation to menstrual cycle or medications.
  10. E – Effect on life "How is this affecting work, social life, relationships, confidence, and sleep?"
  11. R – Red Flags (urgent action)
    • Sepsis indicators: fever/rigors, confusion, very fast HR/RR, cool/mottled skin, hypotension – follow NICE NG51.
    • Drenching night sweats + weight loss/fever/lymphadenopathy – consider 2ww per NICE NG12.
    • TB red flags: persistent cough, haemoptysis, pleuritic pain, exposure risk – NICE NG33.
    • Severe thyrotoxicosis features (resting tachycardia, agitation, fever) – urgent assessment (NG145).
    • Any rapid clinical deterioration or new confusion – treat as urgent.

Background History

  • PMH: thyroid disease, diabetes (type & control, recent hypos), malignancy, chronic infections (TB/HIV risk), peri-/post-menopause.
  • Surgical: sympathectomy or dermatological interventions for hyperhidrosis (past responses).
  • Drug history:
    • Insulin/sulfonylureas; levothyroxine dose changes.
    • SSRIs/SNRIs, antipsychotics; withdrawal states (alcohol/opioids) – see NICE CG100.
    • Tamoxifen, tramadol, caffeine/stimulants; herbal/OTC.
  • Allergies and ADRs.
  • Family history: thyroid disease; early menopause; TB household contacts; haematological malignancy.
  • Social:
    • Occupation/heat exposure; antiperspirant use; clothing/fabric.
    • Alcohol units; last drink; caffeine intake; recreational drugs.
    • Housing/crowding; recent travel (TB/malaria risk); immigration from high-TB areas (NG33).
    • Snoring/witnessed apnoeas; daytime somnolence (OSA suspicion).
  • Sexual history (when relevant): risk of acute HIV seroconversion; offer testing per UK guidance (link local policy/clinic pathways; consider NICE NG60 on HIV testing uptake).

ICE & Safeguarding

  • Ideas: “Do you think it could be your hormones/thyroid/stress?”
  • Concerns: TB/cancer; social embarrassment; sleep loss.
  • Expectations: tests, immediate relief options, work notes.
  • Capacity & safeguarding considered if red flags, weight loss, or neglect concerns (GMC Good Medical Practice).

Provisional DifferentiaI Map (for examiner thinking, not said verbatim)

  • Primary hyperhidrosis (focal/generalised) – NICE CKS.
  • Thyrotoxicosis – NG145.
  • Perimenopause/menopause – NG23.
  • Infection incl. TB, endocarditis, malaria – NG33 / NHS.
  • Malignancy (e.g., lymphoma) – safety-net using NG12.
  • Anxiety/panic; substance use/withdrawal – CG100.
  • Hypoglycaemia (diabetes treatment) – NHS.
  • Drug-induced sweating (SSRIs/SNRIs, opioids, tamoxifen, levothyroxine, caffeine/stimulants).

MANAGEMENT – SWEATING (Hyperhidrosis & Secondary Causes)

  1. Immediate assessment & red flags
    • If acutely unwell → follow NICE NG51 Sepsis.
    • Drenching night sweats + weight loss/fever/lymph nodes → NICE NG12 2ww.
    • TB symptoms/exposure → CXR & TB service per NICE NG33.
    • Thyrotoxicosis → beta-blocker for symptoms; endocrine review (NG145).
    • Hypoglycaemia (on insulin/SU): treat with 15–20 g fast-acting carbs (Diabetes UK).
  2. Focused examination
    • Vitals, BMI, hydration; tremor/thyroid eye signs.
    • Lymphadenopathy; pruritus.
    • Chest exam if infective symptoms.
    • Distribution map (axillae/palms/soles/face); irritation/maceration.
    • Perimenopause features.
    • Anxiety/panic features.
  3. Investigations
    • FBC, U&Es, LFTs, CRP/ESR, TSH/FT4, HbA1c/fasting glucose, urinalysis.
    • TB risk → CXR ± sputum/IGRA (NG33).
    • B-symptoms/nodes → 2ww (NG12).
    • Thyrotoxicosis workup & interim beta-blocker (NG145).
    • Menopause management (NG23).
  4. Primary hyperhidrosis – stepwise therapy (NICE CKS; BNF)
    1. Self-care & trigger avoidance; breathable clothing.
    2. Topical aluminium chloride 20% (first-line).
    3. Iontophoresis (palms/soles).
    4. Botulinum toxin A (axillae) – specialist.
    5. Oral anticholinergic (consider specialist advice).
    6. Refractory focal cases → consider ETS (specialist counselling).
    7. Dermatology referral if QoL impairment persists.
  5. Menopause/perimenopause – vasomotor sweats (NG23)
    • Offer HRT first-line after risk assessment; discuss alternatives if declined/contraindicated.
  6. Thyrotoxicosis – interim management (NG145)
    • Beta-blocker for symptom control.
    • Consider antithyroid while waiting for specialist if confirmed hyperthyroidism.
  7. Education & safety-net
    • Explain diagnosis/uncertainty; share written leaflets.
    • Urgent return advice per NG51 and NG12.
  8. Documentation
    • Record red-flag screen, tests, treatment, safety-netting.
