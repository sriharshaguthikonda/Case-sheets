1ST COMPLAINT: SOLE PAIN (Plantar Foot Pain)

  • S: Site "Can you point with one finger to the area of your sole that hurts the most?"
    • HEEL (underside): Plantar Fasciitis (most common), Fat Pad Atrophy, Calcaneal Stress Fracture
    • ARCH: Plantar Fasciitis, Posterior Tibial Tendon Dysfunction (flat foot)
    • BALL OF FOOT (Forefoot): Morton's Neuroma, Metatarsalgia, Stress Fracture, Rheumatoid Arthritis
    • DIFFUSE / RADIATING: Tarsal Tunnel Syndrome, Lumbar Radiculopathy (S1)
  • O: Onset "How did the pain start? Was there a specific injury, or did it come on gradually?"
    • ACUTE / TRAUMATIC:
      • Rare for most conditions, but possible Plantar Fascia rupture (audible pop)
      • Acute gout attack
    • GRADUAL / INSIDIOUS (No trauma):
      • Change in footwear, activity level, or walking surface? (Plantar Fasciitis, Stress Fracture)
      • No preceding event? (Inflammatory arthritis)
  • D: Duration "How long has the sole of your foot been painful?"
  • A/R: Aggravating & Relieving Factors "What makes the pain worse or better?"
    • AGGRAVATING:
      • First steps in the morning, walking barefoot on hard surfaces (Plantar Fasciitis)
      • Wearing narrow or high-heeled shoes (Morton's Neuroma)
      • Prolonged standing or walking
    • RELIEVING:
      • Rest, supportive footwear/orthotics, calf stretches, ice
  • C: Character "What does the pain feel like? Is it a sharp stabbing, a dull ache, or burning?"
    • SHARP / STABBING (especially with first steps): Classic for Plantar Fasciitis
    • DULL ACHE: Stress fracture, arthritis
    • BURNING / TINGLING / NUMBNESS: Nerve involvement (Morton's Neuroma, Tarsal Tunnel, Radiculopathy)
    • "LIKE A PEBBLE or folded sock in my shoe": Classic for Morton's Neuroma
  • R: Radiation "Does the pain travel anywhere, for example, up your leg or into your toes?"
    • Pain radiating from the back/buttock down the leg -> Lumbar Radiculopathy
    • Pain radiating from the forefoot into the toes -> Morton's Neuroma
    • Pain radiating from the inner ankle into the sole -> Tarsal Tunnel Syndrome
  • A: Associated Symptoms "Besides the pain, have you noticed any swelling, stiffness, or other issues?"
    • LOCAL SYMPTOMS
      • Mild swelling in the heel
      • Clicking sensation in the forefoot when walking (Mulder's Click - Morton's Neuroma)
      • Stiffness (especially morning stiffness)
    • SYSTEMIC / INFLAMMATORY SYMPTOMS
      • Fever, chills (Infection - rare)
      • Pain or swelling in other joints (e.g., hands, wrists)?
      • Pain in other tendon insertion points (enthesitis)?
      • Associated skin rashes (Psoriatic Arthritis) or back pain (Ankylosing Spondylitis)?
  • P: Progression "Since it started, has the pain been getting better, worse, or staying the same?"
  • P: Pattern "Is the pain worse at any particular time of day?"
    • Worse with first steps in the morning or after a period of rest -> Pathognomonic for Plantar Fasciitis
    • Worse with increased activity / as the day goes on -> Stress Fracture, Metatarsalgia
    • Constant pain, worse at night, not relieved by rest -> Red flag (Infection, Tumor, Nerve entrapment)
  • E: Effect on Life "How has this pain affected your ability to walk, work, or exercise?"
    • Limitation of walking distance
    • Inability to participate in sports
    • Need to modify footwear or activities
  • R: Red Flags "I need to ask some specific questions to make sure we're not missing anything serious."
    • HOT, RED, SWOLLEN JOINT (e.g., big toe) -> Septic Arthritis / Gout
    • NEW WEAKNESS (e.g., foot drop) or associated bladder/bowel changes with back pain -> Cauda Equina Syndrome
    • SEVERE NIGHT PAIN, unexplained weight loss, history of cancer -> Malignancy / Metastasis
    • INABILITY TO BEAR WEIGHT after minimal trauma -> Fracture
    • BURNING PAIN, numbness, and color changes in a diabetic patient -> Diabetic Neuropathy / Foot at risk
  • S: Severity / Scale "On a scale of 1 to 10, how would you rate your pain right now, and at its worst?"
