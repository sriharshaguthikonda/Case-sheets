1ST COMPLAINT: DIARRHEA

S: Site of Discomfort
  • Generalized cramping
    • Gastroenteritis
  • Lower Left Quadrant
    • Diverticulitis
  • Lower Right Quadrant
    • Infectious enterocolitis (Yersinia, Campylobacter),
    • Crohn's Disease
  • Periumbilical pain
    • Small bowel cause,
    • gastroenteritis
"Do you have any abdominal pain or cramping with the diarrhea? If so, where is it located?"
O: Onset
  • ACUTE (< 2 weeks): 
    • Infectious (viral, bacterial), 
    • Medication-induced (antibiotics, metformin), Dietary indiscretion
    • Chemo-induced diarrhea
    • Radio enteritis/colitis (post-radiation therapy)
    • Immunodeficiency-related (HIV/AIDS, immunosuppression)
  • CHRONIC (> 4 weeks):
    • IBD (Crohn's, Ulcerative Colitis)
      • ,
    • IBS-D
    • Microscopic Colitis
      • ,
    • Malabsorption (Celiac)
    • Bile acid malabsorption (post-cholecystectomy, ileal resection)
    • Post-surgical (gastrectomy, vagotomy, bowel resection)
    • Rare genetic disorders (Microvillus inclusion disease, Glucose-galactose malabsorption)
  • Triggered by recent travel, new food, sick contacts, or starting a new medication?
"When did the diarrhea start? Did it come on suddenly, and was there any specific trigger you can think of?"
D: Duration & Frequency
"How long has this episode been going on for? And how many times a day are you having to go to the toilet?"

  • BLOODY (Hematochezia):

"Can you describe the stool? Is it watery, bloody, greasy, or does it contain mucus?"
R: Radiation (of Pain)
"If you have pain, does it travel or spread anywhere?"
A: Associated Symptoms
  • SYSTEMIC: 
    • Fever, chills, malaise (Infection)
      • ,
    • Unexplained weight loss (Malignancy, IBD, Malabsorption)
  • GASTROINTESTINAL: 
    • Nausea, vomiting (Gastroenteritis)
    • Tenesmus (feeling of incomplete evacuation) (Proctitis/IBD)
    • Urgency, Incontinence
    • Anxiety - IBS
  • EXTRA-INTESTINAL (IBD clues): 
    • Joint / spine
      • Joint pain / peripheral arthritis.
      • Back stiffness (axial spondyloarthritis, ankylosing spondylitis, sacroiliitis).
    • Eyes
      • Redness, pain, photophobia (uveitis, episcleritis).
    • Skin
      • Erythema nodosum.
      • Pyoderma gangrenosum.
    • Hepatobiliary
      • Primary sclerosing cholangitis (PSC).
      • Fatty liver.
    • Renal
      • Renal stones.
  • Thyroid
    • Hyperthyroidism (increased GI motility)
    • Anxiety, tremor
  • Other Endocrine Causes:
    • VIPoma (Verner-Morrison syndrome) - watery diarrhea, hypokalemia, achlorhydria
    • Carcinoid syndrome - diarrhea, flushing, wheezing
    • Diabetes mellitus (autonomic neuropathy, bacterial overgrowth)
    • Addison's disease
"Along with the diarrhea, have you experienced anything else like fever, vomiting, or weight loss?"
P: Progression
"Since it started, has the diarrhea been getting better, worse, or staying about the same?"
P: Pattern
  • NOCTURNAL DIARRHEA (wakes from sleep): 
    • organic disease (e.g., IBD, Microscopic Colitis), NOT typical for IBS
  • ALTERNATING with constipation:
    • Suggests IBS
    • Malignancy
  • POST-PRANDIAL (after eating): 
    • Gastrocolic reflex,
    • malabsorption,
    • dumping syndrome
"Is the diarrhea constant, or does it come and go? Do you have it at night?"
E: Effect on Life
"How has this been impacting your daily life, work, or ability to go out?"
R: Red Flags
  • Unexplained weight loss
  • Rectal bleeding or iron-deficiency anemia
  • New onset of symptoms in a person > 50 years old
  • Strong family history of bowel cancer or IBD
  • Nocturnal symptoms waking the patient from sleep
  • Severe dehydration (dizziness, confusion, low urine output)
  • High fever with severe abdominal pain
"I just need to ask some specific questions to make sure we're not missing anything serious."
S: Severity / Bother Scale
"On a scale of 1 to 10, how much is this diarrhea bothering you or affecting your life?"
