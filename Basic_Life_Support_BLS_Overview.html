BASIC LIFE SUPPORT (BLS) OVERVIEW & TEACHING GUIDE

A. SCENARIO & INITIAL APPROACH (Video 0:20)

  • Typical Task: Demonstrate and teach BLS steps to a junior doctor (e.g., Alex, an FY1) who has not attended a BLS workshop, then assess and provide feedback. (Video 0:27-0:47)
  • Resource Availability: A Resuscitation Council UK chart will likely be present in the cubicle, which can be used as a reference. (Video 0:57-1:06)
  • Initial Interaction (Rapport & Assessment): (Video 1:14-1:41)
    • Build rapport.
    • Assess their existing knowledge of BLS.
    • Understand what they want to learn.
    • Briefly discuss why BLS is performed and on which patients.

B. FUNDAMENTALS OF BLS (Video 1:45)

  • Purpose: To be the heart and lungs in an unconscious patient who is not breathing properly. (Video 1:47-2:05)
  • Key Components: (Video 1:49-1:51)
    • Chest compressions and
    • rescue breaths.
  • UK Resus Guidelines (Important Note): No Pulse check in guidelines for lay rescuers/initial BLS. (Video 2:08-2:25)

C. ADULT BLS STEPS (DRS ABC or DOCTORS ABC) (Video 2:35)

  • D - Danger: (Video 2:39-2:43)
    • Assess the scene for any danger to yourself, the victim, or bystanders. (Video 2:43-2:47)
    • If danger is present (e.g., middle of the road, fire), move the victim to a safe place before starting BLS. (Video 2:47-3:02) Ensure safety for all. (Video 3:01-3:09)
  • R - Response: (Video 3:13)https://chatgpt.com/g/g-69b2b53274108191938d1c506a20943e-plab-2-roleplayer-v11/c/69bcdf8f-cadc-83a2-a263-76ec0183337a
    • Check for response by gently asking "Hello, can you hear me?" (Video 3:14-3:18)
    • Gently tap the shoulders. Be gentle to avoid causing further injury. (Video 3:18-3:35)
  • S - Shout for Help: (Video 3:37-3:43)
    • Call out for assistance from anyone nearby.


  • A - Airway: (Video 3:43-3:48)
    • Open the airway using the head-tilt chin-lift maneuver. (Video 3:48-3:50)
    • If any visible foreign body is present, remove it. (Video 3:50-3:52)
  • B - Breathing: (Video 3:54-3:57)
    • Check for normal breathing for no more than 10 seconds. (Video 4:15-4:18) Be quick. (Video 4:18-4:22)
    • Use Look, Listen, and Feel: (Video 3:58-4:02)
      • Look for chest rise and fall. (Video 4:02)
      • Listen for breath sounds. (Video 4:05)
      • Feel for breath on your cheek. (Video 4:08-4:11)
  • C - Call Emergency Services / Circulation (Compressions):
    • e.g., 999 or 2222 in a hospital). (Video 4:22-4:24)
    • Request an ambulance and an Automated External Defibrillator (AED). (Video 4:28-4:34)
    • Start Chest Compressions: (Video 4:37-4:43)
      • Use the heel of your dominant hand on the lower half of the sternum. (Video 4:43-4:48)
      • Interlock fingers of the other hand on top. (Video 4:48-4:51)
      • Keep elbows straight; pressure should come from the shoulders. (Video 4:51-4:57)
      • Compress the chest to a depth of at least 1/3 of the chest diameter. (Video 4:59-5:03)
      • Allow full chest recoil between compressions. (Video 5:06-5:08)
      • Do not lift hands from the chest during compressions. (Video 5:08-5:12)
      • Count compressions aloud. (Video 5:12-5:15)
      • Perform 30 chest compressions. (Video 5:18-5:23)
    • Rescue Breaths: (Video 5:26-5:28)
      • Perform head-tilt chin-lift again. (Video 5:28-5:30)
      • Pinch the patient's nose. (Video 5:30-5:35)
      • Give mouth-to-mouth resuscitation. Deliver a normal breath (not forceful) until you see chest rise. (Video 5:35-5:43)
      • Allow chest to fall, then deliver a second breath. (Video 5:43-5:48)
      • Exam note: You may be asked to
        • verbally explain rescue breaths rather than demonstrate, or
        • use a sheet over the dummy's face. Follow instructions given. (Video 5:48-6:12)
    • Continue CPR: 30 compressions to 2 rescue breaths (30:2 ratio). (Video 7:40-7:43)
    • AED Arrival: As soon as the AED arrives, switch it on and follow its voice/visual prompts. (Video 7:46-7:51)

D. PEDIATRIC BLS CONSIDERATIONS (Video 9:06)

  • Two protocols: Out-of-hospital and In-hospital. (Video 9:08-9:16) The exam will specify which to teach. (Video 9:30-9:58)
  • Out-of-Hospital Pediatric BLS: (Video 9:58)
    • Designed to be simple, similar to adult BLS for lay rescuers. (Video 10:02-10:10)
    • Check responsiveness, shout for help, open airway, check breathing. (Video 10:28-10:33)
    • Give 5 initial rescue breaths FIRST. Pediatric collapse is often respiratory in origin. (Video 10:37-10:52) These 5 breaths are only given at the beginning. (Video 11:06-11:20)
    • Then, continue with 30 chest compressions to 2 rescue breaths (30:2). (Video 11:12-11:16, 10:10-10:12, 10:24)
    • Calling for help (single rescuer): If alone, perform CPR for 1 minute before going to call emergency services (unless phone with speaker is available for earlier call). (Video 11:29-11:50)
  • In-Hospital Pediatric BLS (for healthcare professionals): (Video 12:06)
    • Call for help using hospital emergency number (e.g., 2222). (Video 12:16-12:28)
    • Follow similar initial steps (responsiveness, airway, breathing).
    • Give 5 initial rescue breaths. (Video 12:32)
    • Then, continue with 15 chest compressions to 2 rescue breaths (15:2). (Video 12:33-12:43)
  • Chest Compression Technique for Children/Infants:
    • Child (approx 1 year to puberty): One hand may be sufficient if the 1/3 chest depth can be achieved. (Video 13:11-13:18)
    • Infant (under 1 year): Two fingers may be used for compressions. (Video 13:18-13:25)
    • The aim is always to compress the chest by at least one-third of its depth. (Video 13:25-13:32)

E. ASSESSING THE LEARNER & PROVIDING FEEDBACK (Video 6:17)

  • After explaining, ask the student (Alex) to demonstrate the BLS steps. (Video 6:19-6:21)
  • Observe their performance. They will likely make a mistake or two. (Video 6:23-6:27)
  • Correcting Mistakes: (Video 6:27-6:29)
    • Correct constructively and politely. Avoid sounding accusatory. (Video 6:31-6:32)
    • Example: If student bends elbows during compressions: "Alex, you're doing good, a very good job. But you know, when you're doing the chest compression, make sure you don't bend the elbow. The force should be coming from the shoulder... Let's give another try. Perfect, you're doing good now." (Video 6:35-7:02)
    • Encourage and correct them as they perform. (Video 8:20-8:30)

F. WHEN TO STOP BLS (Video 7:54)

  • The patient shows signs of life (e.g., starts breathing normally, moving). (Video 7:59-8:01) - If so, place in recovery position. (Video 8:01-8:03)
  • Qualified help arrives and takes over. (Video 8:03-8:05)
  • You become exhausted and unable to continue. (Video 8:08-8:10)

Always refer to the Resuscitation Council UK chart provided during the exam for specific guidelines and sequences. (Video 13:18-13:25)

BASIC LIFE SUPPORT (BLS) TEACHING SESSION

A. INTRODUCTION & UNDERSTANDING OF BLS (Video 0:09)

  • Teacher's Introduction & Learner's Context: The learner (Alex) missed their BLS class and needs to be taught adult BLS. (Video 0:09-0:15)
  • Assessing Learner's Prior Knowledge:
    • Teacher asks: "Could you please tell me what idea you have about BLS?" (Video 0:21-0:22)
    • Learner's response: "I think this is basic life support and we have to do something to it." (Video 0:22-0:30)
  • Defining BLS: (Video 0:36-0:51)
    • It's basic life support.
    • Involves chest compressions and rescue breaths.
    • Purpose: To take over the function of the heart and lungs.
  • Indications for BLS: (Video 0:51-1:08)
    • Any patient who is unconscious.
    • Any patient who is not breathing.
    • Any patient who is not breathing properly.

B. PRE-BLS CHECKS & SAFETY (Video 1:16)

  • Danger Assessment (DRS ABCD - D for Danger): (Video 1:19-1:25)
    • Priority: Ensure safety for yourself, the patient/victim, and any bystanders. (Video 1:25-1:34)
    • Check for environmental hazards (e.g., victim in the middle of the road, live wires). Move to a safe location if necessary. (Video 1:34-1:42)

C. BLS SEQUENCE DEMONSTRATION (Video 1:45)

  • 1. Check Response (R): (Video 1:47-1:52)
    • Tap the victim on the shoulders and shout: "Hello, hello, can you hear me?" (Video 1:59-2:04)
    • If no response, the patient is unconscious. (Video 2:09-2:10)
  • 2. Shout for Help (S): (Video 2:18-2:19)
    • If the patient is unresponsive, shout for help in all directions: "Help! Help!" (Video 2:19-2:26)
  • 3. Airway (A): (Video 2:29-2:31)
    • Open the airway using the head-tilt, chin-lift maneuver. (Video 2:33-2:40)
    • Place one hand on the forehead, use two fingers of the other hand to lift the chin (on the bony part, not soft tissue). (Video 2:40-2:53)
    • Use the thumb to open the mouth if needed to check for foreign bodies. (Video 2:53-2:59)
  • 4. Breathing (B): (Video 3:02-3:05)
    • Maintain head-tilt, chin-lift.
    • Look, Listen, and Feel for normal breathing for no more than 10 seconds: (Video 3:05-3:09)
      • Look for chest rise and fall. (Video 3:13)
      • Feel for breath on your cheek. (Video 3:15)
      • Listen for breath sounds. (Video 3:18-3:20)
  • 5. Call Emergency Services (C - part of Circulation/Compressions): (Video 3:28-3:30)
    • Call 999 (or the appropriate emergency number). (Video 3:30-3:33)
    • If someone is nearby, ask them to call and request an AED (Automated External Defibrillator). (Video 3:33-3:44)
    • If alone, use your phone on speaker mode to call for help. (Video 3:49-3:57)
  • 6. Chest Compressions (C - Circulation): (Video 4:07-4:14)
    • Locate the lower half of the sternum. (Video 4:14-4:20)
    • Place the heel of one (dominant) hand on the lower part of the sternum. (Video 4:20-4:24) Avoid the xiphisternum. (Video 4:26)
    • Interlock the fingers of the other hand on top. (Video 4:28-4:35)
    • Keep elbows straight and shoulders perpendicular to the patient's chest. (Video 4:35-4:41)
    • Perform 30 chest compressions. (Video 4:41-4:46)
    • Rate: 100-120 compressions per minute (approx. 2 per second). (Video 4:53-5:03)
    • Depth: 5-6 cm, or about one-third of the chest depth. (Video 5:49-5:55)
    • Count compressions aloud. (Video 5:21-5:22) (Demonstration shown at Video 5:28-5:47)
  • 7. Rescue Breaths: (Video 6:01-6:04)
    • Perform 2 rescue breaths. (Video 4:46-4:49)
    • Again, use head-tilt, chin-lift. (Video 6:08-6:14)
    • Pinch the nose to prevent air leakage. (Video 6:22-6:24)
    • Form a seal with your mouth over the victim's mouth.
    • Give a normal breath (not forceful) until you see the chest rise. (Video 6:31-6:45)
    • Allow chest to fall, then give the second breath.
  • Continue CPR Cycle: 30 compressions followed by 2 rescue breaths (30:2 ratio). (Video 7:02-7:07)

D. LEARNER ASSESSMENT & FEEDBACK (Video 7:11)

  • Ask the learner (Alex) to demonstrate the BLS steps. (Video 7:31-7:35)
  • Learner Demonstration and Correction:
    • Learner starts compressions. (Video 7:38)
    • Teacher provides feedback on elbow straightness and counting aloud. "Alex, that's good... I just want to make small changes. As I mentioned, our elbows should be straight. And it's better if you will count it loud." (Video 7:49-8:02)
    • Learner continues and is reminded about the 30 compressions. (Video 8:12-8:18)
    • Learner demonstrates rescue breaths.
    • Teacher reminds about pinching the nose. "Alex, just one thing that I suspect, if you pinch the nose, because we should not make the air otherwise leak out." (Video 8:37-8:45)
    • Teacher confirms breaths should be normal. (Video 8:51-8:56)
