    O: Onset (Rapid or chronic)
    • Sudden (acute) onset may suggest allergic reactions, insect bites, or infections.
    • Gradual (chronic) onset may indicate underlying skin conditions like eczema or psoriasis.
    D: Duration (How long the itching has been present)
    • Acute: Less than 6 weeks (e.g., allergic reactions, insect bites).
    • Chronic: More than 6 weeks (e.g., atopic dermatitis, psoriasis).

    • Physical activity or sweating
    • Stress or anxiety

    • weekdays
    C: Character/Type (Description of the itching sensation)
    • Pruritic (general itching)
    • Burning or stinging sensation
    • Crawling or tingling feeling
    R: Radiation
    A: Associated symptoms (Other symptoms accompanying the itching)
    • Rash or skin changes (e.g., redness, swelling, scaling)
    • Discharge or oozing
    • Pain or tenderness
    • Fever or flu-like symptoms
    • Fatigue or body aches
    • Weight loss or loss of appetite (in systemic conditions)
    P: Progression (How the itching has changed over time)
    • Worsening, improving, or staying the same
    • Spreading to other areas or remaining localized
    • Changes in intensity or frequency
    P: Pattern (Distribution and characteristics of itching)
    • Localized (confined to one area)
    • Generalized (widespread across the body)
    • Symmetrical or asymmetrical distribution
    E: Effect on life (Impact on daily activities and quality of life)
    • Disruption of sleep
    • Difficulty concentrating or working
    • Social or emotional distress
    • Skin damage from scratching
    S: Scale of zero to 10 (Severity of itching)
    • Rate the intensity of itching on a scale from 0 (no itching) to 10 (worst itching imaginable).
