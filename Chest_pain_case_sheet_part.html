1ST COMPLAINT: CHEST PAIN

  1. SITE                              - where did you first notice
    1. RETROSTERNAL                                                                                 DIFFUSE [IF POINTS FINGER IT IS NONCARDIAC]

  2. O                                                      WHEN DID U NOTICE cf APPEAR
    1. GRADUAL
    2. SUDDEN (acute coronary syndrome, PE, aortic dissection)
      1. PE [pulmonary embolism]
      2. AFTER TRAUMA (aortic dissection, pneumothorax)
  3. D - DURATION & CHARACTER
    1. PAIN FUL
      1. ACUTE - SHORT=INFLAMMATORY, HAEMOPERITONEUM [PAIN],
    2. PAINLESS
      1. SUB ACUTE - A BIT LONG=MALGNANT [NO PAIN]
      2. CHRONIC - LONG=CHR.INFLAMMATION[LESS PAIN]
      3. LONG=BENIGN [NO PAIN]
  4. A - AGGRAVATING/ASSOCIATED FACTORS
    1. EXERTION (angina, heart failure)
    2. POSITIONAL VARIATION
      1. On lying down (pleuritic, pericarditis)
        1. pericarditis
    3. INSPIRATION, EXPIRATION (pleuritic, pneumothorax)
    4. COUGHING, SNEEZING (pleuritis, pericarditis, PE)
      1. pericarditis
    5. FOOD INTAKE
      1. Spicey food (GERD, gastritis)
      2. Oily food (biliary colic)
        1. Gall bladder (cholecystitis, biliary colic)
      3. Conditions
        1. Post prandial angina
        2. GERD (gastroesophageal reflux disease)
        3. Gall bladder (cholecystitis, biliary colic)
        4. Hiatal hernia (GERD symptoms)

  5. R - RELIEVING FACTORS
    1. NOT RELIEVED  (unstable angina, MI, PE)
      1. UNSTABLE ANGINA
    2. ON REST (stable angina)
    3. POSITIONAL VARIATION
      1. sitting up/forward (pleural effusion, pericarditis)
        1. pericarditis
      2. leaning forward (pericarditis)
        1. pericarditis
    4. nitrates (angina)
    5. Food intake (GERD, gastritis)
    6. Antacids (GERD, peptic ulcer)

  6. C - CHARACTER
    1. DIFFUSE (cardiac ischemia, pericarditis)
    2. SQUEEZING/PRESSURE (angina, MI)
      1. Esophageal spasm
    3. HEAVY (angina equivalent)
    4. SHARP/STABBING (pleuritic, pneumothorax)
    5. DULL/ACHING (GI causes)
    6. TEARING/RIPPING (aortic dissection)
  7. R - RADIATION
    1. INTER SCAPULAR AREA (aortic dissection, MI, biliary colic)
    2. SHOULDER (cardiac, gallbladder)
    3. Lt. Arm - ULNAR BORDER [C8, T1] (cardiac)
    4. JAW/TEETH (cardiac, TMJ)
    5. EPIGASTRIUM (GERD, gastritis, MI)
    6. BACK (aortic dissection, pancreatitis)
  8. A - ASSOCIATED SYMPTOMS
    1. NAUSEA/VOMITING (MI, GERD, gastritis)
      1. VOMITING (GERD, gastritis, MI)
      2. SWEATING (diaphoresis in MI, PE)
    2. SUGGESTIVE OF DYSPEPSIS (GERD, PUD, gastritis)
    3. FEVER (infection, inflammation, malignancy)
      1. Pericarditis
      2. Pneumonia (infectious, aspiration)
      3. Pleuritis (infectious, inflammatory)
    4. FLU LIKE SYMPTOMS (viral pericarditis, myopericarditis)
      1. Pericarditis
    5. SHORTNESS OF BREATH (SOB) (CHF, PE, pneumonia, pneumothorax)
      1. ORTHOPNEA (CHF, pulmonary edema)
      2. PND (paroxysmal nocturnal dyspnea in CHF)
    6. JOINT PAINS/ARTHRITIS (autoimmune, reactive arthritis)
      1. Pericarditis (autoimmune)
    7. VOMITING (GERD, gastritis, MI)
      1. BILIOUS (biliary disease, SBO)
      2. HEMETEMESIS (UGIB, PUD, varices)
    8. DIARRHOEA (gastroenteritis, IBD)
    9. CONSTIPATION (IBS, obstruction)
    10. ALCOHOLISM (gastritis, pancreatitis, liver disease)
    11. REPEATED BLOOD TRANSFUSIONS (hemochromatosis, iron overload)
    12. DIFFICULTY IN RESPIRATION (CHF, PE, pneumonia, pneumothorax)
      1. Gall bladder (referred pain, subphrenic abscess)
    13. DYSPHAGIA (GERD, esophageal spasm, stricture)
    14. PAIN WITH MOVEMENT (musculoskeletal, costochondritis)
    15. RASH/SKIN CHANGES (shingles, vasculitis)
  9. P - PROGRESSION & TIMING
    1. ACUTE ONSET (MINUTES-HOURS)
      1. MI (acute coronary syndrome)
      2. AORTIC DISSECTION (tearing pain)
      3. PULMONARY EMBOLISM (pleuritic chest pain)
      4. PNEUMOTHORAX (sudden pleuritic pain)
    2. SUBACUTE (DAYS-WEEKS)
      1. MALIGNANCY (lung cancer, mets)
      2. PNEUMONIA (infectious process)
      3. PERICARDITIS (viral, autoimmune)
    3. CHRONIC (MONTHS-YEARS)
      1. GERD (chronic reflux)
      2. MUSCULOSKELETAL (chronic pain)
      3. STABLE ANGINA (chronic CAD)
    4. INTERMITTENT/RECURRENT
      1. GERD (post-prandial, nocturnal)
      2. PRINZMETAL'S ANGINA (vasospasm)
      3. PLEURITIC PAIN (recurrent PE, pleuritis)
  10. S - SEVERITY & IMPACT
    1. SEVERE, CRUSHING PAIN (MI, aortic dissection)
    2. PLEURITIC PAIN (PE, pneumonia, pneumothorax)
    3. BURNING EPIGASTRIC PAIN (GERD, PUD)
    4. REPRODUCIBLE WITH PALPATION (musculoskeletal)
  11. RED FLAGS (REQUIRE URGENT EVALUATION)
    1. CANCER RED FLAGS
      1. UNEXPLAINED WEIGHT LOSS >10 LB
      2. NIGHT SWEATS
      3. PERSISTENT FEVER
      4. LYMPHADENOPATHY
    2. PULMONARY/CARDIAC RED FLAGS
      1. HEMOPTYSIS (PE, malignancy, TB)
      2. SYNCOPE/NEAR-SYNCOPE (arrhythmia, MI, PE)
      3. HYPOTENSION (cardiogenic shock, tamponade)
      4. HYPOXIA (PE, pneumothorax, pneumonia)
    3. GASTROINTESTINAL RED FLAGS
      1. Dysphagia
      2. HEMATEMESIS/MELENA (UGIB, varices)
      3. HEMATOCHEZIA (LGIB, ischemic colitis)
      4. PERITONEAL SIGNS (perforation, peritonitis)
    4. SURGICAL EMERGENCIES
      1. AORTIC DISSECTION (tearing pain, pulse deficits)
      2. ESOPHAGEAL RUPTURE (severe pain after vomiting)
      3. TENSION PNEUMOTHORAX (hypotension, tracheal deviation)
      4. CARDIAC TAMPONADE (Beck's triad: hypotension, JVD, muffled heart sounds)
  12. T - TIMING & SEVERITY (1-10 SCALE)
    1. 10 - MOST SEVERE
      1. AORTIC DISSECTION (tearing, ripping pain)
      2. MASSIVE MI (crushing chest pain, diaphoresis)
      3. TENSION PNEUMOTHORAX (hypotension, tracheal deviation)
      4. CARDIAC TAMPONADE (Beck's triad)
    2. 7-9 - MODERATELY SEVERE
      1. MYOCARDIAL INFARCTION (pressure, radiation to arm/jaw)
      2. PULMONARY EMBOLISM (pleuritic pain, SOB)
      3. PERFORATED VISCUS (sudden severe pain, peritonitis)
      4. PNEUMONIA WITH SEPSIS (fever, tachypnea)
    3. 4-6 - MODERATE
      1. UNSTABLE ANGINA (increasing frequency/severity)
      2. PLEURITIS (worse with inspiration)
      3. PEPTIC ULCER DISEASE (burning epigastric pain)
      4. BILIARY COLIC (RUQ pain, post-prandial)
    4. 1-3 - MILD
      1. GASTROESOPHAGEAL REFLUX (heartburn, acid taste)
      2. MUSCULOSKELETAL PAIN (reproducible with palpation)
      3. VIRAL PLEURODYNIA (self-limited, viral prodrome)
      4. ANXIETY/STRESS (hyperventilation, situational)
