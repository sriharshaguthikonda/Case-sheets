1ST COMPLAINT: COLLAPSE / LOSS OF CONSCIOUSNESS

  • S: Site "Was there any preceding focal neurological symptom such as weakness, numbness or speech difficulty before he collapsed?"
  • O: Onset "Did it come on suddenly like a ‘thunder‑clap’ or build up over minutes?"
  • D: Duration "How long was he unresponsive before help arrived?"
  • A/R: Aggravating / Relieving "Did anything trigger the collapse – coughing, exertion, straining? Did positioning make any difference?"
  • C: Character "Did he complain of the worst headache of his life immediately beforehand?"
  • R: Radiation "Did the pain spread to the neck or back?"
  • A: Associated symptoms 
    • Nausea / vomiting
    • Photophobia / neck stiffness
    • Seizure activity
    • Incontinence
  • P: Progression / Pattern "Has his level of responsiveness changed since arrival?"
  • E: Effect on life Pre‑morbid ADLs – was he independent before today?"
  • R: Red flags Persistent GCS < 8, unequal pupils, abnormal posturing noted by ambulance?"
  • S: Severity "If he described a headache, on a scale of 0‑10 how bad did he say it was?"

    MANAGEMENT PLAN – MASSIVE INTRACEREBRAL HAEMORRHAGE (NON‑OPERABLE)

    OBO Urgency

    • Urgent Actions:
      1. Best‑interest meeting &document DNACPR / Ceiling of Care (MCA 2005)
      2. Refer to Hospital Specialist Palliative Care within 1 hour
      3. Check and reverse anticoagulation if applicable (Vit K + PCC)
    • Urgent Referral:
      1. Stroke consultant / Neuro‑ICU outreach – confirm non‑surgical pathway
      2. Stroke & Palliative nurses for bedside family updates
    • Routine Referral:
      1. Bereavement & Counselling services for wife and relatives
      2. Chaplaincy / Local Imam for cultural & spiritual needs
    • Handling In‑House:
      1. Nursing team to implement last‑days‑of‑life comfort care bundle

    Patient‑Specific Plan

    1. Family Partnership
      • Offer bedside presence, phone / video call for children abroad;
      • Provide written summary & leaflets (Headway, Stroke Association)
    2. Assess Mental Capacity→ lacks; wife consulted as next-of-kin
    3. Safeguarding &Support for wife (food, rest, transport)
    4. End‑of‑Life Symptom Control
      • Analgesia ‑ diamorphine 2.5 mg SC prn via syringe driver
      • Anti‑emetic ‑ levomepromazine 6.25 mg SC prn
      • Anti‑secretion ‑ glycopyrronium 0.2 mg SC prn
      • Anxiolytic / terminal agitation ‑ midazolam 2.5 mg SC prn
      • Seizure prophylaxis ‑ levetiracetam 500 mg BD enteral/IV if tolerated
      • Maintain BP comfort target (avoid aggressive lowering once palliative)
    5. Comfort‑Focussed Nursing Care
      • Head elevation 30° for airway patency & ICP comfort
      • Regular repositioning, mouth & eye care, incontinence management
      • Stop routine bloods/obs except symptom‑triggered
    6. Communication & Documentation
      • Progress notes every shift + SBAR nurse handovers
      • Update family at agreed intervals & record conversations
      • Complete Respect/DNACPR form & electronic EoL order set
    7. Legal & Post‑Death Arrangements
      • Consider Organ/Tissue donation referral to SNOD
      • Issue Medical Certificate of Cause of Death promptly
      • Arrange mortuary transfer & faith‑specific last offices
    8. Teach‑Back & Summary
      • Ask wife to repeat understanding of plan & next steps
      • Doctor summarises: cause, irreversibility, comfort focus, support options
    9. follow‑up
      • Daily review by palliative consultant
      • Bereavement follow‑up call within 2 weeks after death
