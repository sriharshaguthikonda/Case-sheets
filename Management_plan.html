  4. Treatment Plan
    • Symptomatic treatment:
      • Analgesia:
        1. Paracetamol 15mg/kg/dose Q4-6H PRN (max 4g/day) or
        2. Ibuprofen 10mg/kg/dose Q6-8H PRN
        3. gastroprotection
        4. omeprazole 20mg PO daily
      • Topical analgesia:
        1. Antipyrine-benzocaine otic drops if TM intact
      • Nasal decongestants:
        1. Consider in children >12 years and adults (e.g., oxymetazoline 0.05% 2-3 sprays per nostril BID for ≤3 days)
    • Specific treatment:
      • Immediate antibiotics if:
        • Age <6 months
        • Severe symptoms (moderate-severe otalgia or fever ≥39°C)
        • Otorrhea
        • Bilateral AOM in children <2 years
        • Immunocompromised
      • First-line antibiotic: Amoxicillin 40-45mg/kg/day PO divided BID (max 1g/dose) for 5-10 days
      • Penicillin allergy or recent amoxicillin use: Cefdinir 14mg/kg/day PO divided QD-BID (max 600mg/day)
      • Treatment failure after 48-72h: Amoxicillin-clavulanate 45mg/kg/day PO divided BID (max 875mg/dose)

  6. Patient Education
    • Warning Signs/Red Flags (Return immediately if):
      • Severe headache, neck stiffness, or photophobia
      • Facial weakness or asymmetry
      • Worsening pain or fever after 48h of antibiotics
      • Signs of mastoiditis (post-auricular swelling, erythema, or tenderness)
      • Dizziness, vertigo, or nystagmus
      • Giant cell arteritis
        1. Vision disturbances
        2. Headache

      • Heavy machinery

  7. Follow Up
    • Frequency:
      • If symptoms worsen or persist >48h on antibiotics
      • Routine follow-up in 2-4 weeks if recurrent AOM or concern for hearing/developmental issues
    • Monitoring
      • Resolution of fever and pain within 48-72h of treatment
      • Hearing assessment if speech/language delay or recurrent AOM
      • TM mobility and appearance at follow-up
