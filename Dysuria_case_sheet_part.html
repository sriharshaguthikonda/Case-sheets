DYSURIA

1ST COMPLAINT: DYSURIA (Painful Urination)

  • S: Site of Pain "Where exactly do you feel the pain? Is it inside, on the outside, in your abdomen, or your back?"
    • URETHRAL / INTERNAL:
      • UTI, Urethritis
    • EXTERNAL (Vulvar/Labial burning):
      • Vaginitis, Herpes, Dermatitis
    • SUPRAPUBIC / PELVIC:
      • Cystitis
      • Interstitial Cystitis
      • Bladder Cancer
    • FLANK / BACK PAIN:
      • Pyelonephritis
      • Kidney Stone
  • O: Onset "When did this begin? Was it sudden or has it been a chronic issue?"
    • ACUTE (Hours-Days):
      • UTI, STI
    • CHRONIC / RECURRENT (Weeks-Months):
      • Interstitial Cystitis
      • Chronic Prostatitis
  • D: Duration "How long does the pain last when it happens?"
  • A/R: Aggravating & Relieving Factors "What makes it worse or better?"
  • C: Character "What does the pain feel like? A burn, a sharp pain, or pressure?"
    • BURNING:
      • UTI / Urethritis
    • SHARP / STABBING:
      • Kidney Stone
    • PRESSURE:
      • Interstitial Cystitis
      • Prostatitis
  • R: Radiation "Does the pain travel anywhere?"
    • LOIN TO GROIN / TESTICLES:
      • Kidney Stone
    • TO PERINEUM / TIP OF PENIS:
      • Prostatitis
  • A: Associated Symptoms "Along with the pain, have you had a fever, changes in your urine, or any discharge?"
    • CLUES FOR UTI
      • Frequency, Urgency, Nocturia
      • Hesitancy
      • Hematuria (blood in urine)
      • Cloudy or foul-smelling urine
    • CLUES FOR PROSTATIC ISSUES (BPH / Prostatitis)
      • Frequency, Urgency, Nocturia
      • Hesitancy, Weak Stream
      • Incontinence, Post-void Dribbling
      • Feeling of incomplete emptying
      • Testicular or Perineal Pain
    • CLUES FOR STI / VAGINITIS
      • Urethral or Vaginal Discharge
      • Genital sores, ulcers, or lesions (Herpes)
      • Vaginal itching or odor (Vaginitis)
      • (See Sexual History for detailed inquiry)
    • SYSTEMIC SYMPTOMS
      • Fever, Chills, Rigors, Nausea, Vomiting -> Pyelonephritis, Acute Prostatitis
  • P: Progression "Since it started, has it gotten worse?"
  • P: Pattern "Is the pain constant or intermittent? When exactly during urination does it hurt?"
    • TIMING OF PAIN
      • Initial (at the start): Suggests Urethritis
      • Terminal (at the end): Suggests Cystitis
    • FREQUENCY
      • Continuous vs. Intermittent
  • E: Effect on Life "How is this affecting your daily life?"
  • R: Red Flags "I just need to ask some specific questions to rule out anything serious."
    • PYELONEPHRITIS
      • Fever, chills, rigors, flank pain, vomiting
    • SUSPICION OF MALIGNANCY (Prostate/Bladder)
      • Gross Hematuria (visible blood)
      • Unexplained weight loss, tiredness
      • New onset bone pain (especially back pain)
      • Palpable lumps/nodes
    • ACUTE URINARY RETENTION (unable to pass urine)
    • IMMUNOCOMPROMISED PATIENT or PREGNANCY
  • S: Severity / Scale "On a scale of 1 to 10, how would you rate the pain?"
