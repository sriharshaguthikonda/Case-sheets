4. Documenting Family History

When documenting family history, include:

  • Specific condition(s)
  • Relationship to patient (e.g., mother, father, sibling, grandparent)
  • Age at diagnosis (if known)
  • Age at death and cause of death (if deceased)
  • Ethnic background (if relevant to condition)

Red Flags in Family History

  • Early-onset diseases (before age 50-60)
  • Multiple affected relatives with same/similar conditions
  • Multiple generations affected
  • Rare conditions or unusual presentations
  • Consanguinity in parents
