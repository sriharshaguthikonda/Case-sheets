• S: Severity/Site - Severity scale, context "On a scale of 1 to 10, how intense is your low mood, with 10 being the worst?" "Is this sadness always there, or does it come up in specific situations?"
  • O: Onset - Start time, sudden/gradual, triggers "When did you first start feeling this low mood?" "Did it come on suddenly, or gradually? Was there a specific event, like a loss?"
  • D: Duration - Episode length, pattern "How long does the low mood last when you feel it?" "Is it constant, or does it come and go? How long has this been happening?"
  • A/R: Aggravating/Relieving Factors "What makes your low mood worse or better?"
    1. Aggravated by: Alone, time, stress, conflict, sleep, substances "Does being alone or stressed make your mood worse?" "Are there times of day, like evenings, when it feels harder?"
    2. Relieved by: Socializing, activities, rest, medication, distraction "Does talking to others or doing certain activities help lift your mood?" "Have you found anything, like rest, that makes it better?"
  • C: Character/Type - Emptiness, hopelessness, guilt "Can you describe what this low mood feels like? Is it emptiness or despair?" "Do you feel guilty, worthless, or tearful at times?"
  • R: Radiation - Impact breadth "Does this low mood affect all parts of your life, like work or family?" "Are there any areas where you still feel okay?"
  • A: Associated Symptoms "Have you noticed other changes alongside your low mood?"
    1. Anhedonia "Have you lost interest in things you usually enjoy?"
    2. Sleep changes "How’s your sleep? Trouble falling asleep, waking up, or sleeping too much?"
    3. Appetite/weight changes "Has your appetite or weight changed recently?"
    4. Fatigue "Do you feel tired or low on energy most days?"
    5. Concentration issues "Is it hard to focus or make decisions right now?"
    6. Psychomotor changes "Do you feel restless or like everything is slowed down?"
    7. Worthlessness/guilt "Do you ever feel worthless or overly guilty about things?"
    8. Suicidal ideation "Have you had thoughts about death or not wanting to be here?" "Have you thought about how you might act on those feelings?"
    9. Anxiety/irritability "Do you feel more anxious or irritable than usual?"
    10. Somatic complaints "Have you had physical symptoms, like headaches or body aches?"
  • R: Red Flags "I need to ask about some serious symptoms to keep you safe."
    1. Suicidal ideation with plan/intent "Have you made any plans to harm yourself or end your life?" "Do you feel you might act on these thoughts?"
    2. Self-harm behaviors "Have you hurt yourself in any way, like cutting or burning?" "How often have these actions happened recently?"
    3. Psychosis symptoms "Have you heard voices or seen things others don’t?" "Do you feel like your thoughts are not your own or are hard to control?"
  • P: Pattern - Timing, frequency "Is your low mood worse in the morning or evening?" "How often does it happen—every day or just some days?"
  • P: Progression - Change over time "Is your low mood getting worse, better, or staying the same?" "Are there times when you feel your normal self?"
  • E: Effect on life - Functional impact "How is this low mood affecting your work or relationships?" "Are you still able to care for yourself or enjoy hobbies?"
  • S: Severity/Scale - Rating, functioning "How would you rate the impact of this mood on your life, from 1 to 10?" "How much is it interfering with your daily routine?"
