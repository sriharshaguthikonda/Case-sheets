Palpitations

S: Sensation & Rhythm (Tap it out)
  • THE "CLINICAL DRUMMING": Ask the patient to tap the rhythm on their chest or the table.
    • Fast & Regular (Racing): SVT (inc. WPW), VT, Sinus Tachycardia.
    • Fast & Irregular (Chaotic): Atrial Fibrillation (AF).
    • "Skipped" or "Dropped" Beats: Atrial/Ventricular Ectopics (PACs/PVCs).
    • "Thud" or "Lurch": Post-ectopic compensatory pause followed by a forceful beat (PVCs).
"Can you tap out on the table what your heart feels like when this happens? Does it feel like a fluttering, a racing, or a missed beat followed by a thud?"
O: Onset & Termination
  • SUDDEN ON/OFF (Switch-like):
    • WPW Syndrome / SVT: Starts instantly (re-entrant circuit). Often noticed since childhood/adolescence.
  • INTERMITTENT / ISOLATED:
    • Ectopics (PACs/PVCs): Random, isolated beats, often at rest.
  • GRADUAL ONSET:
    • Sinus Tachycardia: Response to stress, exercise, fever.
"Does the racing start instantly like someone flipped a light switch, or does it speed up gradually? Did you start getting these when you were young?"
D: Duration & Frequency
  • Seconds/Momentary: Ectopics (PACs/PVCs).
  • Minutes to Hours: WPW / SVT episodes.
  • Continuous: Thyrotoxicosis, Anemia, Anxiety.
"How long do these episodes last? Is it just a split second, or does it race for hours?"
A/R: Aggravating & Relieving Factors
  • AGGRAVATING (Positions):
    • Lying on Left Side: Makes Ventricular Ectopics (PVCs) feel much stronger (heart closer to chest wall).
    • Bending forward: Can trigger arrhythmias.
  • AGGRAVATING (Substances):
    • Caffeine, Alcohol, Stress, Fatigue (Triggers for WPW and Ectopics).
  • RELIEVING (Vagal Maneuvers):
    • Deep breathing, bearing down (Valsalva), cold water.
    • WPW / SVT often terminates abruptly with these.
    • Sinus Tach slows down but speeds back up.
"Do you notice the skipped beats more when you lie on your left side in bed? Can you stop the racing by holding your breath and bearing down?"
C: Character (Forcefulness)
  • "RACING ENGINE": WPW / SVT (Rate > 150 bpm).
  • "HEART STOPPED" / "FLIP-FLOP": Ectopics (PACs/PVCs). The "stop" is the pause; the "thump" is the next beat.
  • "FLUTTERING": Atrial Fibrillation.
  • "POUNDING": Anxiety, Thyrotoxicosis.
"Describe the sensation. Does it feel like a racing engine, or like your heart stops for a second and then kicks you in the chest?"
R: R
"Have you lost weight recently? Do you feel shaky?"
A: Associated Symptoms
  • SYNCOPE / PRE-SYNCOPE:
    • WPW Red Flag: Rapid conduction via accessory pathway.
  • THYROID: Weight loss, heat intolerance, tremor.
  • ANEMIA: Fatigue, pallor.
  • ELECTROLYTES: Low Potassium/Magnesium (causes frequent Ectopics).
  • POLYURIA (Frequent Urination):
    • Common after SVT/WPW episodes (due to release of ANP from stretched atria).
  • CHEST PAIN: Ischemia vs. Anxiety.
  • SHORTNESS OF BREATH: Heart failure vs. Panic.
"After the racing stops, do you find you need to urinate a lot? Do you ever feel like you're going to pass out?"
P: Progression
"Are the episodes happening more often as you get older?"
P: Pattern
  • Nocturnal: Ectopics are often noticed in bed when quiet.
  • Exertional: Exercise-induced arrhythmia (CPVT, Ischemia).
"Does this happen while you are exercising, or only when you are resting?"
E: Effect on life
  • Anxiety is very common with Ectopics (fear of sudden death).
  • Avoidance of exercise (WPW).
"Does this worry you? Are you avoiding exercise because of it?"
R: Red Flags
  • SYNCOPE with Palpitations: High risk for lethal arrhythmia.
  • WPW + ATRIAL FIB: Can degenerate into Ventricular Fibrillation (Cardiac Arrest).
  • FAMILY H/o SUDDEN DEATH: (< 40 years old).
  • STRUCTURAL HEART DISEASE: Previous heart attack or cardiomyopathy (makes PVCs dangerous).
"Has anyone in your family died suddenly at a young age? Have you ever completely passed out from this?"
S: Scale
  • 0-10 Severity.
  • Impact on ability to work/exercise.
"On a scale of 0 to 10, how distressing are these episodes?"
