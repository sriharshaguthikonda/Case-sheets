Acne Vulgaris

1ST COMPLAINT: SPOTS / PIMPLES (Acne)

  • S: Site "Where on your body do you get the spots?"
    • Face (T-zone, cheeks, jawline)
    • Chest
    • Back and Shoulders
  • O: Onset "When did the acne first start? Was it during your teenage years or did it begin in adulthood?"
    • Pubertal / Adolescent Onset (most common)
    • Adult Onset (>25 years old)
  • D: Duration "How long has this been a persistent problem for you?"
  • A/R: Aggravating & Relieving Factors "Have you noticed anything that makes your skin better or worse?"
    • AGGRAVATING
      • Menstrual cycle flares
      • Stress
      • Picking or squeezing spots
      • Occlusive cosmetics or hair products
      • Diet (e.g., high glycemic index foods, dairy - controversial but patient-reported)
      • Medications (e.g., steroids, lithium)
    • RELIEVING
      • Specific treatments (creams, tablets)
      • Sun exposure (can be temporary improvement)
  • C: Character (Lesion Morphology) "What kind of spots do you get? Blackheads, whiteheads, red bumps, pus-filled spots, or deep, painful lumps?"
    • COMEDONAL (Non-inflammatory)
      • Open comedones (Blackheads)
      • Closed comedones (Whiteheads)
    • INFLAMMATORY
      • Papules (small red bumps)
      • Pustules (pus-filled spots)
    • NODULOCYSTIC (Severe)
      • Nodules (large, hard, painful lumps under the skin)
      • Cysts (deep, pus-filled lumps)
    • SEQUELAE
      • Post-inflammatory erythema (red marks)
      • Post-inflammatory hyperpigmentation (dark marks)
      • Scarring (ice-pick, boxcar, rolling, hypertrophic)
  • R: Radiation "Not applicable for this complaint."
  • A: Associated Symptoms "Have you noticed any other symptoms, for example, with your periods or any excess hair growth?"
    • SYMPTOMS OF HYPERANDROGENISM (suggests PCOS)
      • Hirsutism (excess hair on face, chest, or back)
      • Irregular or absent menstrual periods
      • Thinning of hair on the scalp (androgenic alopecia)
      • Difficulty managing weight
  • P: Progression "Has it been getting progressively worse, or does it stay at a certain level?"
  • P: Pattern "Is there a pattern to your flare-ups? For example, does it get worse around your period?"
    • Cyclical - menstrual cycle is common
  • E: Effect on Life "How has the acne been affecting you personally? For example, your confidence or mood?"
    • Impact on self-esteem and body image
    • Anxiety or depression
    • Social avoidance
    • Pain or discomfort
  • R: Red Flags "I just need to ask a few final questions to be thorough."
    • SUDDEN, SEVERE ERUPTION with FEVER/UNWELLNESS
      • Acne Fulminans (a rare, severe form requiring urgent care)
    • SUDDEN ONSET SEVERE ACNE with signs of virilization (e.g., deep voice)
      • Suggests possible androgen-secreting tumor
    • TREATMENT HISTORY
      • What have you tried in the past? (Over-the-counter, prescribed creams, tablets)
      • How did you use them and for how long?
  • S: Severity / Scale "On a scale of 1 to 10, how much would you say the acne bothers you?"
