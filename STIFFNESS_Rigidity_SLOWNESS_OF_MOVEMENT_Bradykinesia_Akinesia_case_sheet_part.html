1. SITE (S) & LATERALITY:
    1. Where do you feel the stiffness or slowness? (e.g., Arms, legs, neck, trunk)
    2. Is it on one side of the body or both? Did it start on one side?
    3. Is it a general feeling of slowness, or difficulty initiating movements?
  2. ONSET (O):
    1. When did you first notice the stiffness or slowness?
    2. Was it sudden or gradual?
  3. DURATION (D) & CHARACTER/TYPE (C):
    1. How long have these symptoms been present?
    2. Are they constant or do they fluctuate?
    3. Character of Stiffness:
      1. Is it a feeling of muscle tightness or resistance to movement?
      2. Is it like a "cogwheel" (jerky resistance) or "lead-pipe" (smooth, constant resistance) if felt by an examiner or noticed by patient?
      3. Is there associated pain or aching with the stiffness?
    4. Character of Slowness (Bradykinesia):
      1. Do you find it takes longer to do everyday tasks?
      2. Difficulty starting movements (akinesia)?
      3. Do movements become smaller or slower with repetition (e.g., finger tapping, handwriting)?
      4. Feeling of "freezing" where you get stuck, especially when starting to walk or turning?
  4. AGGRAVATING/RELIEVING FACTORS (A/R):
    1. What makes the stiffness or slowness worse? (e.g., Cold weather, inactivity, stress)
    2. What makes it better? (e.g., Warmth, gentle exercise, medication - if on any)
  5. RADIATION (R): (Spread of symptoms)
    1. Has the stiffness or slowness spread to other parts of the body since onset?
  6. ASSOCIATED SYMPTOMS (A):
    1. Tremor at rest?
    2. Balance problems, shuffling gait, difficulty turning? (Postural instability)
    3. Difficulty getting out of a chair or turning in bed?
    4. Reduced arm swing when walking?
    5. Changes in handwriting (micrographia)?
    6. Reduced facial expression (masked facies)?
    7. Soft or monotone speech (hypophonia)?
    8. Drooling of saliva?
    9. Fatigue?
    10. Non-Motor Symptoms:
      1. Loss of sense of smell (anosmia)?
      2. Sleep disturbances (e.g., REM sleep behavior disorder - acting out dreams)?
      3. Constipation?
      4. Mood changes (depression, anxiety)?
      5. Cognitive changes (difficulty with attention, planning, memory)?
  7. PROGRESSION (P):
    1. How have these symptoms changed over time?
  8. PATTERN (P):
    1. Are there times of day when the stiffness/slowness is better or worse (e.g., "wearing off" of medication)?
  9. RED FLAGS (R): (As above, for atypical features)
    1. Early severe dementia?
    2. Prominent cerebellar signs (ataxia beyond what's expected from PD)?
    3. Significant gaze palsies?
  10. EFFECT ON LIFE (E):
    1. How do these symptoms limit your daily activities (dressing, bathing, walking, household chores)?
    2. Has it impacted your independence? Your mood?
  11. SCALE (S):
    1. On a scale of 0-10, how would you rate the severity of your stiffness? Your slowness?
