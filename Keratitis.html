FOCUSED HISTORY – SODA-CRAPPERS (Keratitis)

  1. S — Site / Side
    • Laterality (R/L/both), exact location of discomfort on the cornea; any visible white spot. NICE CKS Red eye – red flags
  2. O — Onset
    • When did it start (hours/days)? Sudden vs gradual; any clear trigger (new lenses/solution, trauma, water exposure). NICE CKS – causes & symptom patterns
  3. D — Duration
    • How long symptomatic; continuous vs intermittent; trajectory (better/same/worse) since onset. NICE CKS – when to refer same-day
  4. A — Aggravating / R — Relieving
    • Light sensitivity (photophobia), difficulty opening eye, blinking/wind aggravation; relieved by lens removal/analgesics? NICE CKS – photophobia/pain as red flags
  5. C — Character
    • Pain quality: foreign-body/grit vs deep ache; discharge: watery vs mucopurulent; tearing. NICE CKS – symptom cues
  6. R — Radiation
    • Headache/periocular pain; brow ache (think uveitis/keratitis overlap). NICE CKS – differentiation
  7. A — Associated symptoms
    • ↓ Vision (near/far), halos/glare, marked photophobia, lid swelling; any visible corneal white spot/ulcer. Moorfields – when to seek urgent help
    • History of cold sores or prior “herpes eye”; recurrent unilateral red-eye. NICE CKS – ocular HSV
  8. P — Progression
    • Is redness/white spot enlarging? Pain and vision worsening or improving? Moorfields – progression advice
  9. P — Pattern
    • Constant vs intermittent; diurnal variation; relation to lens wear. College of Optometrists – CMG Microbial keratitis
  10. E — Effect on life
    • Impact on driving/reading/phone work; time off work; safety-critical tasks (machinery). NICE CKS – management context
  11. R — Red flags (must ask early)
    • Any drop in visual acuity, severe pain, photophobia, white corneal spot, pupil irregularity, inability to open eye. NICE CKS – red flags
    • Contact lens wear (esp. overnight), hot-tub/shower/swim with lenses. CMG – Acanthamoeba keratitis
    • Recent trauma/vegetative matter; welding/UV exposure; chemical exposure. NICE CKS – causes
  12. S — Severity (0–10)
    • Pain 0–10; vision vs baseline (can you read phone/number plate?). NICE CKS – same-day referral thresholds

Risk-factor Deep-Dive

  • Contact lens specifics: type (soft/hard), daily/monthly, overnight wear, water exposure (shower/swim), hygiene (solution brand, topping-up/“no-rub”, case age), recent brand/solution change, online purchase. CMG – microbial keratitis risk
  • Trauma/foreign body; vegetative matter (fungal risk); grinding/sanding; UV (welding/skiing). NICE CKS – causes
  • Past ocular history: previous ulcers/abrasions, herpes eye disease, ocular surgery, chronic dry eye/blepharitis/rosacea. NICE CKS – ocular HSV

Medication & Allergy History

  • Any topical steroids, prior antibiotic/anaesthetic drops, antivirals (aciclovir), analgesics; drug allergies (chloramphenicol/fluoroquinolones). AAO PPP – bacterial keratitis (principles)

Systemic & Social History

  • Diabetes; immunosuppression (steroids/DMARDs/HIV/chemo); atopy/blepharitis/rosacea. UK SMI S11 – red/painful eye background
  • Hyperacute purulent keratitis risk: sexual history (possible gonococcal disease). NICE CKS – red eye differentials
  • Occupation/hobbies (welding, grinding, swimming), current driving/vision-critical tasks. NICE CKS – management & safety

ICE & Safety Net Prompts

  • Ideas/Concerns/Expectations (fear of vision loss, infection from lenses, time off work). NHS – urgent advice cues
  • Safety net check now: worsening pain/redness, ↓ vision, enlarging white spot → same-day eye casualty. Moorfields – when to seek help

One-line Summary (to tell the examiner)

  1. “Unilateral acute painful photophobic red eye with reduced vision in a soft contact-lens wearer who sleeps in lenses and swims with them; visible corneal white spot since yesterday — high suspicion of microbial keratitis; same-day ophthalmology required.”
