    HISTORY OF PRESENT ILLNESS: (Elaboration of each complaint using SODA CRAPPERS framework)

    1ST COMPLAINT: LOW MOOD / SADNESS
    • S: Severity/Site - Severity scale, context "On a scale of 1 to 10, how intense is your low mood, with 10 being the worst?" "Is this sadness always there, or does it come up in specific situations?"
    • O: Onset - Start time, sudden/gradual, triggers "When did you first start feeling this low mood?" "Did it come on suddenly, or gradually? Was there a specific event, like a loss?"
    • D: Duration - Episode length, pattern "How long does the low mood last when you feel it?" "Is it constant, or does it come and go? How long has this been happening?"
    • A/R: Aggravating/Relieving Factors "What makes your low mood worse or better?"
      1. Aggravated by: Alone, time, stress, conflict, sleep, substances "Does being alone or stressed make your mood worse?" "Are there times of day, like evenings, when it feels harder?"
      2. Relieved by: Socializing, activities, rest, medication, distraction "Does talking to others or doing certain activities help lift your mood?" "Have you found anything, like rest, that makes it better?"
    • C: Character/Type - Emptiness, hopelessness, guilt "Can you describe what this low mood feels like? Is it emptiness or despair?" "Do you feel guilty, worthless, or tearful at times?"
    • R: Radiation - Impact breadth "Does this low mood affect all parts of your life, like work or family?" "Are there any areas where you still feel okay?"
    • A: Associated Symptoms "Have you noticed other changes alongside your low mood?"
      1. Anhedonia "Have you lost interest in things you usually enjoy?"
      2. Sleep changes "How’s your sleep? Trouble falling asleep, waking up, or sleeping too much?"
      3. Appetite/weight changes "Has your appetite or weight changed recently?"
      4. Fatigue "Do you feel tired or low on energy most days?"
      5. Concentration issues "Is it hard to focus or make decisions right now?"
      6. Psychomotor changes "Do you feel restless or like everything is slowed down?"
      7. Worthlessness/guilt "Do you ever feel worthless or overly guilty about things?"
      8. Suicidal ideation "Have you had thoughts about death or not wanting to be here?" "Have you thought about how you might act on those feelings?"
      9. Anxiety/irritability "Do you feel more anxious or irritable than usual?"
      10. Somatic complaints "Have you had physical symptoms, like headaches or body aches?"
    • R: Red Flags "I need to ask about some serious symptoms to keep you safe."
      1. Suicidal ideation with plan/intent "Have you made any plans to harm yourself or end your life?" "Do you feel you might act on these thoughts?"
      2. Self-harm behaviors "Have you hurt yourself in any way, like cutting or burning?" "How often have these actions happened recently?"
      3. Psychosis symptoms "Have you heard voices or seen things others don’t?" "Do you feel like your thoughts are not your own or are hard to control?"
    • P: Pattern - Timing, frequency "Is your low mood worse in the morning or evening?" "How often does it happen—every day or just some days?"
    • P: Progression - Change over time "Is your low mood getting worse, better, or staying the same?" "Are there times when you feel your normal self?"
    • E: Effect on life - Functional impact "How is this low mood affecting your work or relationships?" "Are you still able to care for yourself or enjoy hobbies?"
    • S: Severity/Scale - Rating, functioning "How would you rate the impact of this mood on your life, from 1 to 10?" "How much is it interfering with your daily routine?"
    2ND COMPLAINT: ANXIETY / PANIC ATTACKS
    • S: Site/Severity - Where is the anxiety felt (e.g., chest tightness, stomach)? How severe (1-10)?
    • O: Onset - When did it start? Specific triggers? Spontaneous?
    • D: Duration - How long do episodes last? How long has the overall problem been present?
    • A/R: Aggravating/Relieving Factors
      1. Aggravated by: Specific situations (social, crowds, enclosed spaces, specific objects/thoughts)? Caffeine? Stress? Being alone?
      2. Relieved by: Avoidance? Reassurance? Relaxation techniques? Medications? Distraction? Being with someone?

    • C: Character/Type - (For Panic Attacks: sudden surge of intense fear/discomfort, peaks within minutes)
      1. Palpitations, pounding heart, accelerated heart rate?
      2. Sweating?
      3. Trembling or shaking?
      4. Sensations of shortness of breath or smothering?
      5. Feelings of choking?
      6. Chest pain or discomfort?
      7. Nausea or abdominal distress?
      8. Feeling dizzy, unsteady, lightheaded, or faint?
      9. Chills or heat sensations?
      10. Paresthesias (numbness or tingling sensations)?
      11. Derealization (feelings of unreality) or depersonalization (being detached from oneself)?
      12. Fear of losing control or "going crazy"?
      13. Fear of dying?
      (For Generalized Anxiety: excessive worry, restlessness, feeling keyed up/on edge)
    • R: Radiation - Not typically applicable.
    • A: Associated Symptoms
      1. Muscle tension?
      2. Sleep disturbance (difficulty falling/staying asleep)?
      3. Irritability?
      4. Difficulty concentrating?
      5. Fatigue?
      6. Avoidance behaviors?
      7. Excessive worry about multiple things?
    • P: Pattern (Timing/Frequency) - How often? Predictable or unpredictable?
    • P: Progression - Worsening, improving, or stable?
    • E: Effect on life - Impact on daily functioning, social life, work/school, avoidance of situations.
    • S: Severity/Scale - Subjective rating, frequency, intensity, level of distress, functional impairment.
    3RD COMPLAINT: HALLUCINATIONS / DELUSIONS
    • S: Site/Sensory Modality (for Hallucinations) - Auditory (voices, sounds), Visual (formed, unformed), Olfactory, Tactile (e.g., formication), Gustatory?
    • O: Onset - When first experienced? Sudden or gradual? Context?
    • D: Duration - How long do they last? How long has the problem been present?
    • A/R: Aggravating/Relieving Factors
      1. Aggravated by: Stress? Isolation? Substance use? Specific times?
      2. Relieved by: Distraction? Medication? Social interaction? Reality testing?

    • C: Character/Type
      1. Hallucinations:
        • Auditory: Voices (1st/2nd/3rd person), running commentary, command hallucinations, derogatory, content? Other sounds?
        • Visual: Formed (people, objects) or unformed (flashes, patterns)?
        • Olfactory/Gustatory: Pleasant/unpleasant smells/tastes?
        • Tactile: Sensation of being touched, things crawling on/under skin?
      2. Delusions (Fixed, false beliefs, not culturally sanctioned):
        • Persecutory (most common)?
        • Grandiose?
        • Referential (ideas of reference)?
        • Somatic?
        • Religious?
        • Nihilistic?
        • Erotomanic?
        • Thought insertion/withdrawal/broadcasting?
        • Delusions of control?
        • Bizarreness of delusion? Systematized?
    • R: Radiation - Not applicable.
    • A: Associated Symptoms
      1. Disorganized speech (loose associations, tangentiality, word salad)?
      2. Disorganized or catatonic behavior?
      3. Negative symptoms (affective flattening, alogia, avolition)?
      4. Mood changes (depression, mania, anxiety)?
      5. Sleep/appetite disturbances?
      6. Social withdrawal?
      7. Impairment in functioning?
      8. Lack of insight?
    • P: Pattern (Timing/Frequency) - How often? Constant or intermittent?
    • P: Progression - Worsening, improving, stable?
    • E: Effect on life - Impact on reality testing, behavior, relationships, self-care, safety.
    • S: Severity/Scale - Degree of conviction in delusions, intrusiveness/distress from hallucinations, functional impairment, risk.

    DRUG HISTORY:
    • Anticholinergic Medications (e.g., Diphenhydramine, Benztropine, Oxybutynin, TCAs):
      • Memory impairment
      • Confusion / Delirium
      • Agitation

    • Dopamine Agonists (e.g., Levodopa, Pramipexole, Ropinirole - for Parkinson's, RLS):
      • Hallucinations (often visual)
      • Confusion, Agitation
      • Impulse control disorders (e.g., gambling, hypersexuality)
      • Psychosis
      • Delusions
      • Mania / Hypomania
    • Corticosteroids (e.g., Prednisone, Dexamethasone):
      • Insomnia
      • Anxiety, Irritability, Agitation
      • Mania / Hypomania ("steroid-induced mania")
      • Depression
      • Cognitive changes (e.g., memory impairment, confusion, "steroid dementia")
      • Psychosis ("steroid-induced psychosis")
    • Beta-Blockers (e.g., Propranolol, Metoprolol):

      • Depression

        • Referral if needed.

        • Case management, vocational rehabilitation, housing support.
        • Family psychoeducation and support.
        • Linkage to community resources and support groups.
        • Lifestyle modifications (sleep hygiene, diet, exercise, stress management).
      • Admission required: Yes/No (Indication as above).
    4. Follow Up:
      • Timing of next appointment (crucial for safety and monitoring if starting new medication).
      • Frequency of subsequent appointments.
      • Monitoring plan (symptoms, side effects, adherence, functioning).
      • Contact information for urgent concerns.
      • Collaboration with other healthcare providers (e.g., GP, therapist).
    5. Patient Education (Reinforce):
      • Nature of the illness.
      • Treatment plan and rationale.
      • Medication information (purpose, dose, how to take, common side effects, when to seek help).
      • Warning Signs/Red Flags for relapse or worsening.
      • Importance of adherence and follow-up.
      • Emergency contact information.
      • Educational materials (leaflets, websites).
