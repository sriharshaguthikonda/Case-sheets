Anxiety

1ST COMPLAINT: ANXIETY / NERVOUSNESS / PANIC

S: Somatic (Physical) Symptoms
  • CHEST / HEART: Palpitations, chest tightness/pain.
    • Must rule out cardiac pathology (ACS, Arrhythmia).
  • RESPIRATORY: Shortness of breath, feeling of "air hunger," hyperventilation.
  • GASTROINTESTINAL: "Butterflies," nausea, churning stomach, loose stools (IBS link).
  • NEUROLOGICAL: Dizziness, lightheadedness, tingling (paresthesia) in hands/lips, tension headaches.
  • MUSCULOSKELETAL: Muscle tension, tremors, shaking.
"I know you feel anxious, but where do you feel it in your body? Do you get a racing heart, a tight chest, or an upset stomach?"
O: Onset & Triggers
  • SUDDEN / "OUT OF THE BLUE":
    • Panic Disorder (Panic attacks peak within minutes).
    • Pheochromocytoma, Arrhythmia, PE.
  • GRADUAL / CHRONIC:
    • Generalized Anxiety Disorder (GAD) - "Worry wart" since childhood.
  • SITUATIONAL (Triggered):
    • Social Anxiety (public speaking, parties).
    • Specific Phobia (heights, spiders).
    • PTSD (triggered by reminders of trauma).
"Did this feeling come on suddenly like a wave, or has it been building up slowly? Was it triggered by a specific situation?"
D: Duration
  • Duration of the current episode?
  • Duration of the overall problem? (GAD requires symptoms > 6 months).
"How long have you been feeling this way? If you have attacks, how long do they last?"
A/R: Aggravating & Relieving Factors
  • AGGRAVATING (Substances):
    • Caffeine, Energy drinks, Alcohol withdrawal, Cannabis, Stimulants (ADHD meds, illicit drugs).
  • AGGRAVATING (Situations):
    • Stress, lack of sleep, conflict, confined spaces (Agoraphobia).
  • RELIEVING (Coping Mechanisms):
    • Avoidance (staying home).
    • Safety behaviors (carrying water, sitting near exits).
    • Substance use (Self-medicating with alcohol/benzos).
"Do you drink much caffeine or alcohol? Is there anything you do that helps calm you down?"
C: Character of Thought (Cognitive Symptoms)
  • "WHAT IF" THINKING: GAD (Worry about future, health, family, finances).
  • IMPENDING DOOM / DYING: Panic Attack, Heart Attack, PE.
  • FEAR OF JUDGMENT: Social Anxiety.
  • INTRUSIVE THOUGHTS / COMPULSIONS: OCD.
  • DEREALIZATION / DEPERSONALIZATION: Feeling detached from reality or oneself.
"What is going through your mind when this happens? Are you worrying about the future, or do you feel like something terrible is about to happen?"
R: Related Comorbidities (Review)
  • SLEEP DISTURBANCE: Insomnia (trouble falling asleep due to racing thoughts).
  • EATING HABITS: Loss of appetite or overeating (comfort eating).
"How have you been sleeping? Has your appetite changed?"
A: Associated Symptoms (Psychiatric & Physical)
  • DEPRESSION (Mood Disorder):
    • Low mood, anhedonia, hopelessness. (Mixed Anxiety-Depressive Disorder).
  • BIPOLAR DISORDER:
    • Periods of elevated mood, racing thoughts, decreased need for sleep. (Mania/Hypomania can present as agitation).
  • PSYCHOSIS:
    • Paranoia, hearing voices, feeling watched. (Severe anxiety stems from paranoid delusions).
  • ADHD:
    • Restlessness, inability to focus, constant fidgeting. (Often misdiagnosed as anxiety).
  • EATING DISORDERS:
    • Intense fear of weight gain, body image distortion.
  • AUTONOMIC / PHYSICAL SIGNS:
    • Sweating, Trembling, Dry mouth, Flushing.
    • Weight loss + Tremor -> Hyperthyroidism.
"Anxiety often comes with other feelings. Have you been feeling low or depressed? Have you had periods where you felt unusually energetic? Do you ever feel paranoid or like people are watching you?"
P: Progression
"Is the anxiety getting harder to control?"
P: Pattern
  • CONSTANT: "Free-floating" anxiety (GAD).
  • EPISODIC: Distinct attacks with calm periods in between (Panic Disorder).
"Are you anxious all the time, or does it come in waves with normal periods in between?"
E: Effect on life (Functional Impairment)
  • Avoidance of places, people, or activities?
  • Impact on work performance or attendance.
  • Strain on relationships.
"Is this anxiety stopping you from doing things you want to do, like going to work or seeing friends?"
R: Red Flags (Organic & Safety)
  • SUICIDALITY / SELF-HARM: Always assess risk.
  • CHEST PAIN ON EXERTION: Suggests Angina rather than anxiety.
  • ORGANIC CAUSES (MIMICS):
    • Hyperthyroidism (Thyrotoxicosis).
    • Pheochromocytoma (Adrenal tumor).
    • Hypoglycemia.
    • Arrhythmias (SVT, A-Fib).
    • Asthma / COPD exacerbation.
  • SUBSTANCE WITHDRAWAL: Alcohol or Benzodiazepines (can be life-threatening).
"I need to ask some important questions to keep you safe. Have you had any thoughts of hurting yourself? Have you noticed any chest pain when you exercise?"
S: Severity / Scale
  • GAD-7 Score (for General Anxiety).
  • Effect on ability to function.
"On a scale of 1 to 10, how severe is the anxiety today?"
