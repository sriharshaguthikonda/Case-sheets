1. SITE (S) & NATURE OF THE PROBLEM:
    1. How would you describe your balance problem or walking difficulty?
      1. Do you feel unsteady on your feet?
      2. Is it a problem with starting to walk (initiating gait), turning, or stopping?
      3. Swaying while walking or standing?
      4. Feeling like you are "about to fall"?
      5. Staggering or veering to one side?
      6. Difficulty walking in a straight line?
      7. Do you feel like you are walking "as if drunk"?
    2. Is it a feeling of dizziness or lightheadedness, or true unsteadiness and imbalance?
      1. Do you experience vertigo (a sensation of the room spinning around you)?
  2. ONSET (O):
    1. When did you first start noticing problems with your balance or walking?
    2. Was the onset gradual or did it come on suddenly?
    3. Was it associated with any specific event, illness, medication change, or injury?
  3. DURATION (D) :
    1. How long has this been an issue? (Days/Weeks/Months/Years)
    2. Is the problem constant, or does it come and go (intermittent)? If intermittent, how long do episodes last and how frequent are they?
  4. AGGRAVATING/RELIEVING FACTORS (A/R):
    1. What makes the balance or gait problems worse?
      1. Walking on uneven ground or in the dark?
      2. Turning quickly or changing direction?
      3. Being in narrow spaces or crowded areas?
      4. When distracted or trying to do two things at once?
      5. Fatigue or stress?
    2. What makes it better or helps you manage?
      1. Does holding onto something or using a walking aid (cane, walker) help?
      2. Are there any cues that help you overcome freezing (e.g., stepping over lines on the floor, rhythmic counting, music)?
      3. Is it better or worse with your eyes open or closed? (Helps differentiate, e.g., Romberg's implication)
      4. Do any medications help?

  5. CHARACTER/TYPE (C):
    1. Character of Gait:
      1. Do you take small, shuffling steps (festinating gait)?
      2. Do you have difficulty picking up your feet when walking?
      3. Do you find yourself speeding up unintentionally and then having trouble stopping (festination)?
      4. Do you experience "freezing" where your feet feel glued to the floor, especially in doorways, when trying to start walking, or when turning?
      5. Have you noticed a reduced arm swing when walking?
      6. Do you have a stooped posture when walking?
    2. Character of Balance:
      1. Do you sway when standing still?
      2. Do you feel you might fall easily, especially when pushed slightly, when turning, or when reaching for something?
  6. RADIATION (R): (Less about radiation of a sensation, more about spread/evolution of the difficulty)
    1. Has the type of balance or walking problem changed or have new difficulties emerged over time?
  7. ASSOCIATED SYMPTOMS (A):
    1. History of falls?
      1. If yes, how many times have you fallen?
      2. What were the circumstances of the falls? (e.g., tripping, turning, freezing)
      3. Did you sustain any injuries from the falls?
    2. Tremor (shaking), especially at rest?
    3. Stiffness and slowness of movements?
    4. Clumsiness in your hands or legs?
    5. Speech changes (e.g., soft, monotone, slurred - dysarthria)?
    6. Swallowing difficulties (dysphagia)?
    7. Vision changes (e.g., blurred vision, double vision, "eyes jumping" - nystagmus)?
    8. Weakness in your legs?
    9. Numbness, tingling, or altered sensation in your feet or legs? (Suggests possible sensory ataxia component)
    10. Dizziness or lightheadedness, especially on standing up (could be orthostatic hypotension)?
    11. Headache, nausea, or vomiting?
    12. Tinnitus (ringing in ears) or hearing loss? (Suggests possible vestibular component)
  8. PROGRESSION (P):
    1. Have your balance and walking problems worsened, improved, or stayed the same over time?
    2. If worsening, how quickly has this happened?
  9. PATTERN (P):
    1. Are there specific situations, environments, or times of day when your balance or walking is noticeably better or worse?
    2. Does it fluctuate with medication timing (if applicable)?
  10. RED FLAGS (R):
    1. Frequent falls occurring early in the disease course (e.g., within the first 1-3 years of symptom onset) can be a red flag for atypical parkinsonism or other conditions.
    2. Rapidly progressive gait and balance deterioration.
    3. Symmetrical onset of severe gait/balance issues without other typical PD signs.
    4. Poor response of gait/balance issues to Parkinson's medications (if tried).
  11. EFFECT ON LIFE (E) & SEVERITY (S):
    1. How have these balance and walking difficulties affected your daily life and independence?
      1. Has it limited your mobility or ability to go out of the house?
      2. Do you need assistance from another person for walking or transfers (e.g., getting out of a chair)?
      3. Do you use any walking aids (cane, walker, wheelchair)?
      4. Has it affected your ability to perform household chores, shop, or participate in hobbies?
    2. Do you have a fear of falling? How does this impact your activities?
    3. On a scale of 0 to 10, with 0 being no problem and 10 being the worst imaginable difficulty:
      1. How would you rate the severity of your balance problems?
      2. How would you rate the severity of your walking difficulty?
