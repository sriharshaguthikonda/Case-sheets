1ST COMPLAINT: BLEEDING

  • S: Site "Where exactly is the bleeding coming from?"
    • GASTROINTESTINAL (GI) TRACT
      • Hematemesis (Vomiting blood)
        • Bright red vs coffee-ground
        • Volume estimation (teaspoons to cupfuls)
      • Melena (Black, tarry stool)
        • Consistency (sticky, foul-smelling)
      • Hematochezia (Fresh blood in stool)
        • Mixed with stool vs coating stool
        • Dripping after defecation
      • Occult bleeding (positive FOBT with no visible blood)
    • RESPIRATORY TRACT
      • Hemoptysis (Coughing up blood)
        • Streaking vs massive (>500mL/24hr)
        • Frothy appearance
    • URINARY TRACT
      • Hematuria (Blood in urine)
        • Gross vs microscopic
        • Timing: Initial, terminal, total stream
        • Clots present? (suggests lower tract)
      • Post-procedural (cystoscopy, catheterization)
    • GYNAECOLOGICAL
      • Abnormal Vaginal Bleeding
        • Intermenstrual
        • Postmenopausal
      • Post-coital bleeding
    • ENT / MOUTH
      • Epistaxis (Nosebleed)
        • Anterior vs posterior source
        • Unilateral vs bilateral
      • Bleeding gums
        • Spontaneous vs brushing-induced
      • Hemotympanum (blood behind eardrum)
    • SKIN/MUCOSAL
      • Petechiae (≤2mm)
      • Purpura (3-10mm)
      • Ecchymosis (>1cm)
      • Bleeding from venipuncture sites
      • Oral mucosal bleeding
    • OTHER SITES
      • Subconjunctival hemorrhage
      • Intracranial bleeding (headache, neuro deficits)
      • Hemarthrosis (joint swelling)
      • Post-surgical/traumatic sites
  • O: Onset "When did this bleeding first start? Was it sudden and heavy, or gradual?"
    • Acute vs chronic/recurrent pattern
    • First episode or recurrent?
    • Relation to any specific event (trauma, procedure)
  • D: Duration "How long does each episode last? How frequently does it occur?"
    • Minutes vs hours vs continuous
    • Frequency: Daily, weekly, monthly
    • Duration of current episode
  • A/R: Aggravating & Relieving Factors "Does anything trigger the bleeding? What makes it better?"
    • AGGRAVATING:
      • Trauma (even minor)
      • Exertion/Valsalva
      • Medications:
        • Anticoagulants (warfarin, DOACs)
        • Antiplatelets (aspirin, clopidogrel)
        • NSAIDs, SSRIs
        • Herbal supplements (ginkgo, garlic)
      • Alcohol consumption
      • Infection (viral/bacterial)
    • Recent infections: Strep throat, skin infection (Post-infectious GN)
    • RELIEVING:
      • Rest, positioning
      • Local pressure
      • Tranexamic acid
      • Cold application
  • C: Character (Amount, Color, Pattern) "Describe the bleeding - color, amount, consistency, pattern"
    • QUANTIFICATION METHODS:
      • GI:
        • Teaspoon (5ml),
        • tablespoon (15ml),
        • cup (250ml)
      • Gynae:
        • Pad/tampon count (soaked/hour),
        • clot size (coin, golf ball)
      • Urine:
        • Dipstick vs gross hematuria
      • Hemoptysis:
        • Streaks vs cupfuls
    • COLOR INTERPRETATION:
      • Bright Red: Active bleeding, lower GI, arterial source
      • Dark Red/Maroon: Venous bleeding, mid-GI
      • Coffee Grounds: Partial digestion (gastric source)
      • Black/Tarry (Melena): Upper GI bleed (>50ml)
      • Frothy/Pink: Pulmonary edema with bleeding
      • Cola-colored urine: Glomerular disease
    • GYNAECOLOGICAL PATTERNS:
      • Menorrhagia (>80ml/cycle, >7 days)
      • Metrorrhagia (irregular bleeding between periods)
      • Postmenopausal bleeding
    • CLOTTING CHARACTERISTICS:
      • Rapid clot formation vs delayed
      • Clot size and stability
  • R: Radiation (of Pain) "Is there pain with the bleeding? Where do you feel it?"
    • Flank pain → renal origin
    • Epigastric pain → upper GI source
    • Pleuritic pain → pulmonary source
    • Migratory joint pain → vasculitis
  • A: Associated Symptoms "What other symptoms have you noticed with the bleeding?"
    • ANEMIA/HYPOVOLEMIA:
      • Fatigue, pallor, dizziness
      • Palpitations, tachycardia
      • Orthostatic hypotension
      • Shortness of breath
    • RENAL-RELATED:
      • Painless hematuria → malignancy until proven otherwise
      • Flank pain → stones/pyelonephritis
      • Dysuria/frequency → UTI
      • Edema, hypertension → glomerulonephritis
      • Smoking history → bladder cancer risk
      • Oliguria/anuria → renal failure
    • GASTROINTESTINAL:
      • Abdominal pain, nausea/vomiting
      • Heartburn, dysphagia
      • Weight loss, anorexia
      • Jaundice, ascites → liver disease
      • Early satiety → gastric malignancy
    • GYNAECOLOGICAL:
      • Dysmenorrhea, dyspareunia
      • Vaginal discharge
      • Pelvic pain/pressure
      • Pregnancy symptoms
      • Hirsutism, acne → PCOS
    • PULMONARY:
      • Cough, wheezing
      • Chest pain, dyspnea
      • Fever, night sweats
      • TB exposure history
    • BLEEDING DIATHESIS CLUES:
      • Excessive menstrual bleeding since menarche
      • Prolonged bleeding after dental procedures
      • Family history of bleeding disorders
      • History of easy bruising
  • P: Progression "How has the bleeding changed over time?"
    • Increasing frequency/severity
    • New sites of bleeding
    • Changing character (e.g., coffee grounds → bright red)
  • P: Pattern "Is there any pattern to the bleeding?"
    • Cyclical (menstrual-related)
    • Post-prandial (after eating)
    • Nocturnal
    • Positional
    • URINARY TIMING:
      • Initial hematuria → urethral pathology
      • Terminal hematuria → bladder neck/prostate
      • Total hematuria → upper tract/bladder
    • Seasonal variations
  • E: Effect on Life "How has this affected your daily life?"
    • Work/school absenteeism
    • Social restrictions
    • Anxiety/depression
    • Financial burden
  • R: Red Flags "I need to ask about some serious symptoms"
    • HEMODYNAMIC INSTABILITY:
      • Syncope or near-syncope
      • Tachycardia >100 bpm
      • SBP <90 mmHg
      • Altered mental status
    • MASSIVE BLEEDING:
      • Hematemesis >500ml
      • Hemoptysis >100ml/24hr
      • Rectal bleeding with hemodynamic changes
    • MALIGNANCY SUSPICION:
      • Unexplained weight loss >10%
      • Postmenopausal bleeding
      • Painless gross hematuria in smoker
      • Iron deficiency anemia in older adult
    • OBSTETRIC EMERGENCIES:
      • Bleeding with abdominal pain in pregnancy
      • Postpartum hemorrhage
    • NEUROLOGICAL RED FLAGS:
      • Severe headache with vomiting
      • Focal neurological deficits
      • Decreased consciousness
  • S: Severity & Patient Concern "How severe is this for you? What worries you most?"
    • Patient's self-rated severity (0-10 scale)
    • Fear of cancer or serious illness
    • Impact on daily functioning
