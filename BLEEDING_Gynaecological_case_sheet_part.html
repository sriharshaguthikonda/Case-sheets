1ST COMPLAINT: BLEEDING

  • S: Site "Where exactly is the bleeding coming from?"
    • GASTROINTESTINAL (GI) TRACT
      • Hematemesis (Vomiting blood)
      • Melena (Black, tarry stool)
      • Hematochezia (Fresh blood in stool)
    • RESPIRATORY TRACT
      • Hemoptysis (Coughing up blood)
    • URINARY TRACT
      • Hematuria (Blood in urine)
    • GYNAECOLOGICAL
      • Abnormal Vaginal Bleeding
    • ENT / MOUTH
      • Epistaxis (Nosebleed)
      • Bleeding gums
    • SKIN
      • Easy bruising (ecchymosis), Petechiae, Purpura
  • O: Onset "When did this bleeding first start? Was it sudden and heavy, or have you noticed it for a while?"
  • D: Duration "How long does each episode of bleeding last?"
  • A/R: Aggravating & Relieving Factors "Does anything seem to trigger the bleeding or make it stop?"
    • AGGRAVATING: Trauma, Exertion, Medications (anticoagulants, NSAIDs), intercourse, stress.
    • RELIEVING: Rest, Pressure, Medications (tranexamic acid).
  • C: Character (Amount, Color) "Can you describe the bleeding? How much is there? What color is it?"
    • AMOUNT (Quantify)
      • GI: Streaks, teaspoons, cupfuls, massive?
      • Gynae: No. of pads/tampons per day, soaking through, clots (coin-sized?)
      • General: Is it enough to make you feel dizzy or weak?
    • COLOR & CONSISTENCY
      • Bright Red (Fresh): Arterial, lower GI, hemoptysis.
      • Dark Red / Maroon: Venous, mid-GI tract.
      • Coffee Grounds / Black Tarry: Digested blood (Upper GI).
      • Frothy / Pink: Hemoptysis.
      • Clots Present? Indicates heavier bleeding.
    • GYNAECOLOGICAL NATURE (from your notes)
      • Menorrhagia (heavy/prolonged, regular)
      • Metrorrhagia (irregular, between periods)
      • Menometrorrhagia (heavy AND irregular)
      • Oligomenorrhea (>35 day cycles) / Polymenorrhea (<21 day cycles)
  • R: Radiation (of Pain) "Is there any pain associated with the bleeding? Does it travel?"
  • A: Associated Symptoms "Besides the bleeding itself, have you noticed any other symptoms?"
    • SYSTEMIC / ANEMIA SYMPTOMS
      • Fatigue, dizziness, pallor, palpitations, shortness of breath
    • GI SYMPTOMS
      • Abdominal pain, heartburn, dysphagia, weight loss, change in bowel habits, jaundice
    • GYNAECOLOGICAL SYMPTOMS (from your notes)
      • Pain (dysmenorrhea), Vaginal discharge/itch/odor
      • Symptoms of PCOS (hirsutism, acne) or Thyroid dysfunction
      • Symptoms of pregnancy (nausea, breast tenderness)
      • Post-coital bleeding, Dyspareunia
    • RESPIRATORY SYMPTOMS
      • Cough, chest pain, dyspnea, fever
    • URINARY SYMPTOMS
      • Dysuria, frequency, flank pain
  • P: Progression "Is the bleeding getting worse, better, or staying the same over time?"
  • P: Pattern "Is the bleeding continuous or does it come and go? Is there a pattern?"
    • e.g., related to menstrual cycle, after coughing, with bowel movements.
  • E: Effect on Life "How has this bleeding impacted your daily activities, work, or emotional well-being?"
  • R: Red Flags "I must ask some specific questions to ensure this isn't an emergency."
    • HEMODYNAMIC INSTABILITY
      • Dizziness when standing, syncope (fainting), severe pallor, hypotension, tachycardia
    • MASSIVE HEMORRHAGE
      • Large volume hematemesis or hemoptysis
    • SUSPICION OF MALIGNANCY
      • Unexplained weight loss, post-menopausal bleeding, change in bowel habits
    • BLEEDING IN PREGNANCY
  • S: Severity / Impact "Overall, how would you rate the severity or impact of this bleeding on a scale of 1 to 10?"
