UNIVERSAL COLLEAGUE CONVERSATION CASE SHEET

INTRODUCTIONS

  • greet "Hi there, Dr.Reba, how are you?"
  • I am good ...thank you for asking!
  • Intro yourself and roles "Hi, I’m Dr. [Your Name], you might have seen around."
  • Thank you "Thank you for meeting me on short notice."
  • build rapport "How’s your day going so far?" "I’m glad we could have this chat to discuss something important."
  • Introduce purpose "Do you have any idea what I’d like to talk about today?" "I’ve noticed something that concerns me, and I want to discuss it to support you."

SCENARIO DESCRIPTION

  • Issue, context, witnesses "I noticed [behavior, e.g., you seemed under the influence during the ward round]." "Others, like [nurse/team], also observed this. Can you share what happened?"

CONVERSATION FRAMEWORK (SPIRES)

  • S: Seek Information - Behavior details, frequency, reasons "Can you tell me more about [behavior, e.g., why you were late]?" "How often has this happened, and is there a reason behind it?"
    1. Behavior "Have you used any substances, like alcohol or drugs, recently?" "How much and how often do you use them?"
    2. Social media
    3. when,
    4. what platform?
    5. visibility setting?
    6. viralness?
    7. Mental health check "How’s your mood been lately? Any stress or challenges?" "Have you had thoughts of harming yourself?"
    8. Personal circumstances "Who do you live with, and how’s your support system?" "Are things okay at home or with friends?"
  • P: Patient Safety
    1. Impact on work "Could this [behavior] affect your work, like decision-making?"
    2. Actions taken "Have you prescribed meds or made decisions today? I’ll need to review them."
  • I: Insight
    1. Awareness "Do you think this [behavior] might impact patient care or your role?"
    2. Willingness to change "Have you considered cutting down or addressing this issue?"
  • R: Red Flags "I need to check for serious concerns to keep everyone safe."
    1. Substance use at work "Have you used any substances before or during work hours?"
    2. Confidentiality breach "Have you shared patient info, like on social media, without consent?"
    3. Patient safety incidents "Are you aware of any incidents caused by [behavior]?"
  • E: Escalate
    1. Senior involvement "I’ll need to inform a senior to ensure we handle this professionally."
    2. Consequences "You may need to speak with a manager or take a test, depending on the issue."
  • S: Support
    1. Resources "You can talk to your GP or supervisor for confidential help."
    2. Follow-up "I’m here to support you, and we’ll check in again soon."
    1. Immediate actions "For now, please avoid clinical duties until we resolve this." "How will you get home safely today?"
    2. Long-term support "Consider a time-management course or counseling if needed." "Let’s schedule a follow-up to see how you’re doing."

NOTES

  • Key outcomes, commitments "Let’s summarize what we’ve agreed on." "I’ll document this and share next steps with you."
