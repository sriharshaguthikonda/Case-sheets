1ST COMPLAINT: BLOATING

  • C: Character "When you say 'bloating', is it a feeling of fullness and pressure, or do you notice your tummy is visibly swollen (distended)?"
    • Sensation of Bloating/Fullness: IBS, Functional Dyspepsia, Food Intolerance
    • Visible Abdominal Distension:
      • Constipation, Bowel Obstruction
      • Ascites (fluid from liver/heart failure, malignancy)
      • Large ovarian cyst or tumor
    • Is it more of a gassy feeling, or a feeling of heaviness?
  • S: Site of Discomfort "Is the bloating felt all over your abdomen, or is it worse in a particular area?"
    • GENERALIZED: IBS, constipation, ascites
    • UPPER ABDOMEN: Functional Dyspepsia, Gastroparesis, H. pylori
    • LOWER ABDOMEN: Gynaecological causes (fibroids, ovarian cysts)
  • O: Onset "When did the bloating start? Did it come on suddenly, or has it been a gradual problem?"
    • ACUTE: Bowel Obstruction, Gastroenteritis
    • CHRONIC / RECURRENT: IBS, Food Intolerance, Ovarian issues
    • Persistent, daily bloating for > 2 weeks in a woman > 50 is a red flag for ovarian cancer.
  • D: Duration "When you get a bout of bloating, how long does it typically last?"
  • A/R: Aggravating & Relieving Factors "What seems to bring it on or make it worse? Does anything help to relieve it?"
    • AGGRAVATING:
      • After eating any meal (Dyspepsia, Gastroparesis)
      • Specific foods (e.g., dairy, wheat, high-FODMAP foods)
      • Stress or anxiety
    • RELIEVING:
      • Passing gas or having a bowel movement (classic for IBS)
      • Fasting / Not eating
  • R: Radiation (of Pain) "If you have any pain with the bloating, does it travel anywhere?"
  • A: Associated Symptoms "Along with the bloating, have you had any change in your bowel habits, pain, or any other symptoms?"
    • GASTROINTESTINAL CLUES:
      • Change in Bowel Habit (Diarrhea, Constipation, or Alternating) -> IBS
      • Abdominal Pain (relieved by defecation?)
      • Nausea, heartburn, or early satiety
      • Excessive flatulence or belching
      • Steatorrhea (greasy stools) -> Malabsorption
    • GYNAECOLOGICAL CLUES:
      • Pelvic pain
      • Abnormal vaginal bleeding (intermenstrual, postmenopausal)
      • Pain during intercourse (dyspareunia)
      • Bloating related to menstrual cycle
    • SYSTEMIC / ALARM SYMPTOMS:
      • Unexplained weight loss
      • Symptoms of anemia (fatigue, shortness of breath)
      • Ankle swelling (edema) -> Ascites (Heart/Liver failure)
      • Shortness of breath -> Ascites
  • P: Progression "Has the bloating been getting progressively worse over time?"
  • P: Pattern "Is there a pattern to the bloating? For example, is it better in the morning and worse by the end of the day?"
    • Worse as the day progresses -> Classic for IBS/Functional Bloating
    • Constant and unremitting -> More concerning for ascites or a mass
    • Related to menstrual cycle -> Hormonal/Gynaecological cause
  • E: Effect on Life "How has this been affecting you? Does it affect what you wear, your diet, or your social life?"
  • R: Red Flags "I just need to ask some specific questions to make sure we're not missing anything serious."
    • Unexplained weight loss
    • Rectal bleeding or signs of anemia
    • New onset of symptoms in a person > 50 years old
    • Persistent or frequent bloating (most days) in a woman, especially > 50 (Ovarian Cancer)
    • Persistent vomiting or difficulty swallowing (dysphagia)
    • Palpable abdominal or pelvic mass
  • S: Severity / Bother Scale "On a scale of 1 to 10, how much does this bloating bother you?"
