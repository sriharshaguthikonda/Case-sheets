Ages below are corrected for gestational age where relevant. Where shown, (NICE) indicates an explicit age threshold from a NICE guideline. Otherwise, thresholds reflect UK NHS/Patient.info best practice.

Gross Motor Milestones: (Age at which absence becomes a red flag)

  • Head holding:
    1. No head control in prone by 4 months (consider hypotonia/weakness; review). (NHS trust consensus)
  • Sitting without support:
    1. Not sitting unsupported by 8 months. (NICE)
  • Crawling:
    1. No specific red flag for ‘not crawling’ (many infants bottom‑shuffle/skip crawling). Review if no attempt to move around by ~12 months.
  • Standing with support / weight‑bearing:
    1. Not weight‑bearing through legs by 12 months.
  • Standing without support:
    1. No specific NICE age. Use walking thresholds below; persistent inability alongside other delays warrants referral.
  • Walking with support:
    1. No specific red‑flag age. Assess in context of independent walking.
  • Walking without support:
    1. Not walking independently by 15 months (girls) or 18 months (boys). (NICE)
  • Running:
    1. Not running by 2 years.
  • Climbing stairs:
    1. No specific NICE age. Typical: walks up a few stairs (two feet per step) by ~2 years; assess if markedly late or with other delays.

Fine Motor Milestones: (Red‑flag ages)

  • Reaching for objects:
    1. Not reaching for toys by ~4–5 months (consider vision/neuromotor concerns).
  • Transferring objects hand to hand:
    1. Not transferring by 9 months.
  • Pincer grasp (mature/immature):
    1. No pincer grasp by 12 months.
  • Scribbling / marking with a crayon:
    1. Not making marks by ~18–24 months.
  • Building tower of cubes:
    1. Not building a 3‑block tower by ~2 years (or <6 blocks by ~2½–3 years).
  • Copying shapes (circle, cross, square, triangle):
    1. Circle ~3 yrs; cross ~4 yrs; square ~5 yrs; triangle ~5–6 yrs. Concern if >6–9 months behind plus other delays.
  • Hand preference:
    1. Early, fixed hand preference before 12 months → consider hemiplegia. (NICE)

Language Milestones: (Red‑flag ages)

  • Cooing:
    1. No cooing by 3 months.
  • Babble / monosyllables (ba, da, ma):
    1. No babble by 6 months.
  • Double syllables ("mama/dada" etc.):
    1. No double‑syllable babble by 12 months.
  • Speaking meaningful single words:
    1. <6 words at 18 months (or <10 words at 2 years → consider autism features). (NICE/UK consensus)
  • Simple sentences (2–3 words):
    1. No 2–3 word phrases by 2½ years.
  • Telling stories / asking questions / intelligibility:
    1. Speech largely unintelligible by 4 years (or regression at any age).
  • Responds to name / gestures:
    1. Reduced response to name, lack of pointing/waving/showing by 12–18 months → consider autism features. (NICE)

Social-Emotional Milestones: (Red‑flag ages/features)

  • Social smile:
    1. No social smile by 6–8 weeks; or reduced responsive social smiling thereafter. (NICE/UK consensus)
  • Recognises primary caregiver:
    1. Poor response to main caregiver beyond early infancy (consider global delay/attachment/vision/hearing).
  • Stranger anxiety:
    1. Absent alongside other social‑communication concerns (contextual — not an isolated red flag).
  • Waves bye‑bye / social gestures:
    1. Not using gestures (pointing, waving, showing) by 12–18 months. (NICE)
  • Plays simple games (peek‑a‑boo):
    1. No interactive/social play by ~12 months (consider autism features if persistent).
  • Dry by day / night (toileting):
    1. Daytime wetting or not reliably dry by ~5 years (assess; manage constipation). Night‑time bedwetting common <5; assess if persistent >5 years or secondary.
  • Bladder/Bowel control:
    1. Soiling/constipation with wetting at any age → treat constipation first; consider referral.
  • Dresses/Undresses self (with/without help):
    1. No specific NICE age. Consider referral if markedly delayed and other domains are affected.
  • Plays cooperatively with other children:
    1. Persistent lack of social interest/reciprocity vs peers (consider autism features per NICE).
