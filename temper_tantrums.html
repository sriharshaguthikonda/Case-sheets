FOCUSED HISTORY – SODA-CRAPPERS for Toddler Tantrums (Age 3)

  1. S – Site / Setting
    • Where do meltdowns occur most (home rooms, car, shops, nursery)?
    • With which caregiver(s)? Consistency across caregivers?
  2. O – Onset
    • Started ~3–4 months ago; clarify exact onset vs nursery start; any precipitating change (move, sibling, illness)?
  3. D – Duration
    • Typical length of an episode; longest; time to calm; post-episode fatigue.
  4. A – Aggravating / R – Relieving
    • Antecedents (tired, hungry, transitions, denied items, demands, screens switching off).
    • What helps (quiet space, ignoring, comfort hold, sensory tools, snack, nap)?
    • Use an ABC diary (Antecedent–Behaviour–Consequence) to map patterns.
  5. C – Character
    • Behaviours: screaming, dropping, kicking, biting/hitting, head-banging, breath-holding.
    • Injury risk to self/others; objects thrown; broken skin when biting.
  6. R – Radiation
    • Generalisation: now in new settings? spreading from home → nursery or vice-versa?
  7. A – Associated
    • Development: speech (words/phrases, following commands), social (eye contact, pointing, pretend play), play with peers, response to name, any regression. NICE CG128 (Autism recognition & referral)
    • Hearing/vision concerns; recurrent ear infections; selective hearing at home/nursery.
    • Sleep: bedtime routine, sleep-onset delay, night wakings, snoring/pauses (OSA risk). NHS: Sleep & young children
    • Diet/hunger timing; juice/sugary snacks; constipation/pain at stooling.
    • Medical: pain states (teeth, ears, eczema itch); meds/allergies; iron deficiency risk.
  8. P – Progression/Pattern
    • Pattern: improving, static or escalating; clustered times of day; weekdays vs weekends.
  9. E – Effect on life
    • Impact on family routines, outings, parent–child relationship; nursery concerns (incidents, SENCO involvement).
  10. R – Red flags (ask & safety-net)
    • Developmental red flags or regression; consider ASD indicators → discuss HV/GP referral if present. NICE CG128 recommendations
    • Episodes >15–20 min, very frequent daily tantrums, or child unable to settle between episodes.
    • Concern for maltreatment if behaviour/emotional state not consistent with age/development. NICE CKS: Child maltreatment – emotional/behavioural states
    • Biting with broken skin, head-banging causing injury → immediate safety steps.
  11. S – Scale
    • Parent-rated severity 0–10; most/least severe day in last week.

Wider Context

  • Recent life events: move, new sibling, illness, bereavement, parental stress, screen-time increase.
  • Caregiver wellbeing & supports; HV involvement; parenting programmes interest.
  • Nursery collateral: settling pattern, staff concerns, behaviour support plan.

Safeguarding & Consent

  • Safety during episodes; safe space; remove projectiles.
  • Sensitive screen for domestic abuse/harsh discipline; act per GMC child-protection/0–18 guidance. GMC: Protecting children & young people • GMC: 0–18 years

Parent Education (history prompts)

  • Normalising brief toddler tantrums; consistent calm responses; avoid reinforcing with attention. NHS: Temper tantrums

MANAGEMENT PLAN – Toddler Tantrums (Age 3)

1 | Urgency & Red-flags (safety-net)

  • Injury risk (head-banging causing injury; biting with broken skin); violent behaviour unsafe to manage at home → same-day clinical review/ED.
  • Episodes very frequent or >15–20 min, child does not settle between episodes → contact GP/HV for review.
  • Loss of skills (language/social regression), poor eye contact/response to name, or hearing concerns → assess & refer (see Referrals).
  • Any concern for maltreatment or harsh/physical discipline → follow local safeguarding pathway (GMC/NICE CG89/CKS). GMC child protection • NICE CG89 • NICE CKS

2 | Explain & Reassure

  • Brief normalisation: tantrums are common at 2–4 years; they reflect immature self-regulation, often triggered by tiredness/hunger/transitions. NHS: Temper tantrums
  • Set shared goal: reduce frequency/severity, improve calm-down time, and protect safety/relationships.

3 | Core Behaviour Strategies (home)

  • 1) Co-regulate first: get to child’s level, name feelings (“You’re cross the TV’s off”), calm voice, slow breathing; offer a calm corner. Start for Life
  • 2) Planned ignoring for attention-seeking behaviours only; keep child safe; avoid lecturing during peak upset; reconnect once calm. NHS: Temper tantrums
  • 3) Don’t give in to the original demand during a tantrum; after calm, use a brief, pre-agreed consequence (e.g., small privilege loss) and immediately praise the next positive behaviour. Healthier Together: Tantrums
  • 4) Catch good behaviour: specific praise (“Thanks for using your quiet voice”) ≥5×/day; sticker chart for one target (e.g., “gentle hands”).
  • 5) Choices & transitions: 2 good options (“brush teeth first or PJs first?”); give 2-minute warnings before switching off screens/toys.

4 | Routine & Triggers

  • Sleep: consistent bedtime routine; wind-down; adequate nap; address snoring/night-waking if present. NHS: Sleep & young children
  • Diet: regular meals/snacks; limit sugary drinks/juice; maintain hydration.
  • Screens: consistent limits; expect protest when switching off—use timers and transition cues.
  • ABC diary: log Antecedent–Behaviour–Consequence for 1–2 weeks to spot patterns and wins.

5 | Nursery/Setting Collaboration

  • Share the same 3–4 strategies with nursery; request SENCO input if concerns persist; ask for a simple Behaviour Support Plan.
  • Ask nursery for hearing/speech observations and incident patterns.

6 | Development & Health Checks

  • If language delay/hearing concerns → refer audiology + Speech & Language Therapy.
  • If autism red-flags (limited eye contact/pointing/pretend play, regression) → discuss and follow local ASD referral pathway (NICE CG128). NICE CG128

7 | Support & Parenting Programs

  • Offer Health Visitor review and evidence-based parenting courses (e.g., Incredible Years) via Early Help/Children’s Centre; provide local referral routes. NHS: Incredible Years (example)
  • Provide NHS self-help links: Temper tantrums • Help your toddler manage emotions

8 | Follow-up & Safety-Netting (document & share)

  • Agree goals (e.g., reduce bites from 3/week → 1/week; calm-down < 5 min).
  • Follow-up with GP/HV in 4–6 weeks with ABC diary; earlier if red-flags.
  • Emergency advice: if risk of serious harm or prolonged inconsolable episode → NHS 111/ED.

9 | Documentation

  • Record assessment, red-flags asked, co-created plan, resources given, referrals, and safety-net.
