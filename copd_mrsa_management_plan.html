COMPREHENSIVE MANAGEMENT PLAN

0. Current Location & Urgency

  • Already admitted to respiratory ward in single side‑room.
  • Escalate to HDU/ICU if worsening type 2 respiratory failure, sepsis or reduced consciousness.

1. First‑Hour Actions

  1. Airway & Breathing: Target SpO2 88–92 % (Venturi 24–28 %), ABG on admission & if deteriorates.
  2. Bronchodilators: Nebulised Salbutamol 2.5 mg + Ipratropium 0.5 mg 4‑hourly PRN.
  3. Sepsis Screen: FBC, U&E, CRP, lactate, two blood cultures, sputum culture (include MRSA sensitivity), CXR.
  4. Systemic steroids: Prednisolone 30 mg PO OD for 5 days, or IV hydrocortisone if unable to swallow (as per NICE NG115).
  5. Empirical antibiotics for COPD exacerbation if purulent sputum – doxycycline 200 mg stat then 100 mg OD × 4 days; adjust to sensitivities (NG114 table 1).
  6. MRSA status: Colonisation only – commence decolonisation protocol (Mupirocin 2 % nasal TDS + 4 % Chlorhexidine wash daily × 5 days) & inform Infection‑Control.

2. In‑Patient Monitoring

  • NEWS2 obs 4‑hourly, escalate if ≥5.
  • Repeat ABG 30 min after oxygen changes.
  • Daily review of isolation, wound/line sites, and decollonisation compliance.

3. MDT & Support

  • Physiotherapist – airway clearance techniques, early mobilisation.
  • Respiratory Nurse Specialist – inhaler technique, COPD self‑management plan.
  • Infection‑Control team – audit PPE, arrange follow‑up clearance swabs.
  • Smoking Cessation advisor.
  • Dietitian if low BMI or recent weight loss.
  • Clinical psychologist / Liaison for anxiety management of patient & spouse.

4. Optimise Long‑Term COPD Therapy

  • Check inhaler prescription: SABA reliever + LABA/LAMA ± ICS as per GOLD/NICE NG115 stepwise approach.
  • Review adherence & inhaler technique with placebo devices.
  • Consider long‑term oxygen therapy assessment if PaO2 ≤ 7.3 kPa when stable.
  • Arrange pulmonary rehabilitation referral post‑discharge.
  • Vaccinations: offer influenza, COVID‑19 booster & PPSV‑23 before discharge if due.

5. Infection‑Control Measures

  • Contact precautions: gloves & gown for staff; visitors – hand hygiene, no PPE unless providing direct care.
  • Dedicated equipment (stethoscope, BP cuff); enhanced daily room clean.
  • Inform domestic services of isolation status.
  • Removal of isolation after 2 consecutive negative swabs 48 h apart post‑decolonisation.

6. Patient & Family Communication

  • Explain colonisation vs infection; provide Trust MRSA leaflet & COPD action plan.
  • Arrange daily video call until visiting allowed.
  • Signpost to British Lung Foundation & UKHSA MRSA resources.

7. Discharge & Follow‑Up

  1. Criteria: stable obs < NEWS 3, completing decollonisation, walking safely.
  2. Clearance swabs in community at day 6 & week 4.
  3. Respiratory clinic review in 6 weeks – repeat spirometry, inhaler optimisation.
  4. Letter to GP: MRSA colonisation, decolonisation given, advise screening if re‑admitted.
  5. Safety‑net: seek urgent help for worsening breathlessness, fever > 38 °C, new chest pain or confusion; call 111/999 if severe.

8. Documentation

  • Accurate contemporaneous notes of discussions, consent, MRSA status, antibiotics, and decolonisation start/end dates.
