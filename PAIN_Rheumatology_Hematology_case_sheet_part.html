• S - Site:
    • Exact location? (Point)
    • Localized or Widespread/Diffuse?
    • Specific joints involved? (Small/Large)
      • Rheum: Symmetrical involvement?
    • Muscle, bone, or nerve path?
  • O - Onset:
    • When did it start? (Specific date/duration)
    • Sudden or Gradual?
    • Any specific trigger? (Injury, illness, medication)
    • If injury-related:
      • Type of injury (MVA, fall, sports, assault, etc.)
      • Speed and direction of impact (for MVA)
      • Position in vehicle (driver, passenger)
      • Use of seatbelt/airbags
      • Loss of consciousness (duration if yes)
  • C - Character:
    • Describe the pain: (Aching, Sharp, Burning, Throbbing, Dull)
      • Rheum: Associated stiffness? (Especially morning)
    • Constant or Intermittent?
  • R - Radiation:
    • Does it travel/spread anywhere? Where?
  • A - Associated Symptoms:
    • Injury-related symptoms:
      • pain else where
      • Neck stiffness
      • Arm pain, numbness, or tingling
      • Dizziness or balance problems
      • Visual disturbances
      • concentration or memory issues
      • Sleep disturbances
    • Systemic:
      • Fever, Chills, Night sweats
      • Fatigue, Malaise
      • Weight loss, Appetite change
    • Rheumatology Specifics:
      • tingling, numbness
        • Raynaud's phenomenon
      • Joint: Swelling, Redness, Warmth, Morning stiffness (duration?), ↓ROM
      • Skin: Rash (type?), Nodules, Photosensitivity
      • Sicca symptoms (Dry eyes/mouth)
      • Muscle weakness
      • Enthesitis (e.g., heel pain)
      • Inflammatory back pain features
      • Eye inflammation (redness, pain)
    • Hematology Specifics:
      • Bleeding: Easy bruising, Petechiae, Epistaxis, Gum bleeding, Menorrhagia, Hematuria, GI bleed
      • Thrombosis: Limb swelling/pain (DVT?), SOB/Chest pain (PE?)
      • Anemia: Pallor, Dyspnea on exertion, Dizziness
      • Lymphadenopathy (location, painful?)
      • Hepatosplenomegaly
      • Bone pain (if not primary site, character?)
      • Recurrent/unusual infections
      • Pruritus
  • T - Timing / Treatment Tried / Trauma:
    • Timing:
      • When is it worse? (Morning, night, with activity, at rest)
      • Does it wake you from sleep?
      • Pattern over time? (Flare-ups, remissions)
      • Changes in symptoms since injury
  • E - Exacerbating / Relieving Factors:
    • Makes it worse? (Movement, activity, posture, rest - specify for inflammatory vs mechanical)
    • Makes it better? (Rest, activity, meds, position, heat/cold)
    • Specific movements that aggravate injury symptoms
    • Positions that provide relief
  • S - Severity / Significance:
    • Severity: Pain score (0-10: current, worst, best, average)
    • WAD (Whiplash Associated Disorder) Grade:
      1. Neck pain, stiffness, or tenderness only
      2. Neck symptoms + musculoskeletal signs
      3. Neck symptoms + neurological signs
      4. Neck symptoms + fracture/dislocation
    • Significance (Impact on Life):
      • Daily activities (ADLs)
      • Work / Hobbies / Social life
      • Sleep
      • Mood
      • Driving ability
      • Exercise tolerance
      • Work status changes
  • R - Red Flags (General & Specific):
    • Serious Injury Red Flags:
      • Severe, unrelenting pain
      • neurological sx
      • osteoporosis or bone disorders
      • Recent infection or fever
      • malignancy
      • corticosteroids
      • Age >50 with significant trauma
    • Giant cell arteritis GCA
      1. Temporal pain
      2. visual disturbances
      3. jaw claudication
    • Systemic: fever, night sweats, significant weight loss.
    • Rheum: rapidly progressive neurological deficit.
      1. Acute arthritis (hot swollen joint)
    • Hem: Symptoms of acute leukemia (severe fatigue, bleeding, infection), spinal cord compression, bleeding/thrombosis.
