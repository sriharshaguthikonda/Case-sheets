III. HISTORY OF PRESENT ILLNESS (HPI)

    (Elaborate on each chief complaint using a framework like SODA CRAPPERS or similar. If trauma, detail mechanism of injury.)

    MECHANISM OF INJURY (If applicable)

    • Date and time of injury:
    • Place of injury:
    • How did it happen? (Detailed description: e.g., fall from height, road traffic accident - role, speed, impact; sports injury - type of movement, direct blow, twisting)
    • Position of limb/body at time of injury:
    • Force involved (High energy/Low energy):
    • Any protective gear used?
    • Immediate symptoms: (Pain, swelling, deformity, inability to bear weight, sound heard e.g. pop/snap)
    • First aid received: (Immobilization, ice, analgesia)
    • Events since injury: (Progression of symptoms, any interventions)
  1. SITE (S):
    1. Which shoulder is painful (Right/Left/Both)?
    2. Can you point with one finger to the exact spot of maximum pain?
    3. Is the pain felt:
      1. Anteriorly (front of the shoulder)?
      2. Laterally (outer aspect, over the deltoid region)?
      3. Posteriorly (back of the shoulder)?
      4. Superiorly (top of the shoulder, e.g., AC joint)?
      5. Deep inside the joint? Or in the armpit (axilla)?
  2. ONSET (O):
    1. When did the shoulder pain first start?
    2. Was the onset sudden (e.g., after a fall, direct blow, sudden lift or reach) or gradual (insidious)?
    3. If sudden, describe the mechanism of injury. (e.g., fall onto outstretched hand, direct impact, specific movement).
    4. Was it associated with a new or repetitive activity (e.g., overhead work, sports)?
  3. DURATION (D):
    1. How long have you had this shoulder pain? (Days, weeks, months, years)
    2. Is the pain constant or intermittent?
    3. If intermittent, what is the frequency and duration of episodes?
  4. AGGRAVATING/RELIEVING FACTORS (A/R):
    1. Aggravating Factors: What makes the pain worse?
      1. Specific movements:
        • Reaching overhead?
        • Reaching behind your back (e.g., doing up a bra, tucking in a shirt)?
        • Lifting objects? (Specify weight/activity)
        • Throwing motion?
        • Abduction (lifting arm out to the side)?
      2. Lying on the affected shoulder (night pain)?
      3. Activities requiring rotation (e.g., combing hair, dressing)?
      4. Sudden or jarring movements?
    2. Relieving Factors: What makes the pain better?
      1. Rest or avoiding specific movements?
      2. Pain medications (OTC or prescribed)? Which and effectiveness?
      3. Ice or heat?
      4. Specific positions (e.g., supporting the arm)?
  5. CHARACTER/TYPE (C):
    1. How would you describe the pain?
      1. Dull, aching? (Common for rotator cuff issues, OA)
      2. Sharp, stabbing, catching? (May indicate impingement, labral tear)
      3. Burning?
      4. Deep, poorly localized ache?
  6. RADIATION (R):
    1. Does the pain radiate or travel from the shoulder?
      1. Down the lateral aspect of the arm (deltoid insertion area)? (Common with rotator cuff pathology)
      2. Into the neck? Or from the neck to the shoulder?
      3. Into the hand or fingers? (May suggest cervical radiculopathy or thoracic outlet syndrome)
  7. ASSOCIATED SYMPTOMS (A):
    1. Weakness in the shoulder or arm? (Difficulty lifting objects, initiating movement?)
    2. Stiffness or loss of range of motion? (Difficulty with daily activities like dressing, combing hair?) (Consider adhesive capsulitis)
    3. Clicking, popping, grinding (crepitus) with movement?
    4. Instability or sensation of the shoulder "slipping out" or feeling loose? (History of dislocations/subluxations?)
    5. Locking or catching sensation?
    6. Numbness or tingling (paresthesia) in the arm, hand, or fingers?
    7. Swelling or bruising around the shoulder?
    8. Deformity (e.g., prominence of clavicle, "Popeye" sign for biceps rupture)?
    9. Fever, chills, redness, warmth (suggests infection)?
    10. Pain in other joints, rash, or systemic symptoms (suggests inflammatory arthritis)?
    11. Shortness of breath or chest pain (cardiac pain can refer to shoulder)?
  8. PROGRESSION (P):
    1. How has the pain and functional limitation changed since onset? (Worsening, improving, stable?)
    2. Any changes in the nature or location of pain?
  9. PATTERN (P):
    1. Is the pain worse at certain times (e.g., night pain is a hallmark of rotator cuff issues, also concerning for tumor)?
    2. Is there a "painful arc" of movement (pain typically between 60-120 degrees of abduction, suggesting impingement/rotator cuff)?
  10. EFFECT ON LIFE (E):
    1. How does the shoulder pain affect your ability to perform daily tasks (dressing, hygiene, cooking, reaching for objects)?
    2. Impact on work, sports, hobbies, or recreational activities?
    3. Does it disturb your sleep? (Especially inability to lie on the affected side)
    4. Impact on mood or independence?
  11. RED FLAGS (R):
    1. Unexplained Swelling/Mass in the shoulder region.
    2. Fever, chills, systemic illness with acute shoulder pain (septic arthritis).
    3. History of Cancer with new onset, persistent, or night shoulder pain (metastasis).
    4. Unexplained weight loss, night sweats.
    5. Significant trauma with deformity or inability to move the arm (fracture, dislocation).
    6. Sudden inability to lift the arm (massive rotator cuff tear, neurological injury).
    7. Progressive neurological deficit (weakness, numbness) in the arm.
    8. Signs of vascular compromise (pale, cold limb, absent pulses â€“ rare with isolated shoulder pain but consider after trauma).
    9. Pain referred from chest or abdomen (cardiac, diaphragmatic irritation).
  12. SCALE (S) (Severity):
    1. On a scale of 0 to 10:
      1. Shoulder pain right now? At its worst? On average?
      2. Pain during specific movements (e.g., reaching overhead)?

NEGATIVE HISTORY (Pertinent negatives)

  • No history of recent trauma (if pain is non-traumatic).
  • No fever, weight loss, night sweats.
  • No morning stiffness (if considering non-inflammatory cause).
  • No neurological symptoms (numbness, tingling, weakness, bladder/bowel changes).
  • No history of [relevant systemic diseases, e.g., inflammatory arthritis, cancer].
