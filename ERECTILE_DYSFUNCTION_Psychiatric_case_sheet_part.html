S: Site / Nature of Problem
  • INITIATION PROBLEM: Difficulty achieving an erection from a flaccid state.
  • MAINTENANCE PROBLEM: Inability to sustain the erection for satisfactory sexual activity.
  • RIGIDITY PROBLEM: Erections are present but not firm enough for penetration.
"Could you describe the main problem? Is it difficulty getting an erection, keeping one, or is it that they aren't as hard as they used to be?"
O: Onset
  • SUDDEN:
    • Often suggests a psychogenic cause (new stressor, new relationship, performance anxiety).
    • Can be related to pelvic trauma or surgery.
  • GRADUAL (over months to years):
    • More typical for organic causes (vasculogenic, diabetes, low testosterone).
"When did you first notice this issue? Did it start suddenly, or has it been a slow, gradual change over time?"
D: Duration
"How long has this been going on for?"
A/R: Aggravating & Relieving Factors
  • AGGRAVATING:
    • Stress, fatigue, performance anxiety.
    • Alcohol, smoking, certain medications.
    • With a specific partner vs. alone.
  • RELIEVING / DISSOCIATING:
    • Presence of erections during masturbation?
    • Presence of erections upon waking (Nocturnal/Morning Erections)? -> Their presence strongly suggests psychogenic ED. Their absence points to an organic cause.
"Is there anything that makes it worse, like stress or alcohol? Are there situations where erections are better, for example, when you wake up in the morning?"
C: Character (Same as Site/Nature)
  • This section describes the specific nature of the dysfunction as detailed under 'Site'.
"Let's be specific about the erections you do have. How would you describe them?"
R: Related Sexual Function
  • LIBIDO (SEX DRIVE): Any change in desire for sex?
    • Decreased libido can point to low testosterone, depression, or relationship issues.
  • EJACULATION & ORGASM: Any problems like being premature, delayed, or absent? Is it painful? Is orgasm still pleasurable?
"Apart from the erections, have you noticed any changes in your sex drive or with ejaculation?"
A: Associated Symptoms
  • VASCULAR: Leg pain on walking (claudication), chest pain (angina).
  • DIABETIC: Increased thirst, frequent urination, fatigue, tingling/numbness in feet (neuropathy).
  • NEUROLOGICAL: Numbness, tingling, balance problems, weakness elsewhere.
  • ENDOCRINE: Fatigue, low mood, reduced muscle mass, gynecomastia (low testosterone); heat/cold intolerance (thyroid).
  • LOCAL PENILE SYMPTOMS: Curvature, pain, or palpable lumps/plaques on the penis.
    • Peyronie's Disease
"Have you had any other symptoms like leg pain when you walk, numbness, or any pain or curvature of the penis itself?"
P: Progression
  • Is it progressively worsening, stable, or fluctuating?
"Since this started, has the problem gotten progressively worse, or has it stayed about the same?"
P: Pattern
  • CONSTANT: Present in all situations (partner, masturbation, nocturnal). -> Suggests organic cause.
  • SITUATIONAL: Present in some circumstances but not others. -> Suggests psychogenic cause.
"Does this happen every single time, or only in certain situations, for instance, only with a partner?"
E: Effect on Life
  • PSYCHOLOGICAL: Impact on mood, self-esteem, confidence. Symptoms of anxiety or depression?
  • RELATIONSHIP: Strain on relationship, communication with partner, partner's reaction.
  • CONCERNS & EXPECTATIONS: Main fears (e.g., relationship ending, underlying disease)? Goals for treatment?
"How has this been affecting you personally and in your relationship? What are your biggest concerns about it?"
R: Red Flags
  • SUDDEN ONSET with NEUROLOGICAL SYMPTOMS: Saddle anesthesia, leg weakness, bladder/bowel changes (Cauda Equina Syndrome).
  • PENILE SYMPTOMS: Pain, curvature, or nodules (Peyronie's disease).
  • CONSTITUTIONAL SYMPTOMS: Significant weight loss, fatigue suggesting undiagnosed systemic illness or malignancy.
  • MENTAL HEALTH: Symptoms of severe depression or suicidality.
"I need to ask some important safety questions. Have you had any numbness in the groin area, or any changes with your bladder or bowels?"
S: Severity / Scale
  • Mild / Moderate / Severe.
  • Can be formally assessed with questionnaires like the IIEF-5 (International Index of Erectile Function).
"On a scale of 1 to 10, how would you rate your confidence in getting and keeping an erection?"
