• NATURE OF ERECTILE PROBLEM:
    • Difficulty achieving an erection?
    • Difficulty maintaining an erection until completion of sexual activity?
    • Reduced rigidity of erections?
    • Is it a new problem or a worsening of a pre-existing issue?
  • ONSET & DURATION:
    • When did you first notice this problem? (Specific timeframe)
    • Was the onset:
      • Sudden? (Often suggests psychogenic or acute vascular event)
      • Gradual? (More typical for organic causes like vascular disease, diabetes)
    • Has it been continuous or intermittent since onset?
  • SEVERITY & CONSISTENCY:
    • How would you rate the severity? (e.g., mild, moderate, severe; or scale if appropriate)
    • Does it happen every time you attempt sexual activity?
    • Are erections present in some situations but not others?
      • e.g., Present with masturbation but not with a partner?
      • Present on waking (nocturnal/morning erections)? (Absence may suggest organic cause)
  • ASSOCIATED SEXUAL SYMPTOMS:
    • Libido (Sex Drive):
      • Any change in your desire for sex? (Increased, decreased, absent)
    • Ejaculation:
      • Any problems with ejaculation? (Premature, delayed, absent, painful)
    • Orgasm:
      • Any difficulty reaching orgasm?
      • Is orgasm pleasurable?
    • Pain during intercourse (dyspareunia) for self or partner?
  • PSYCHOLOGICAL & RELATIONSHIP FACTORS:
    • Current mood? (Symptoms of depression, anxiety)
    • Recent stressors? (Work, financial, family)
    • Performance anxiety related to ED?
    • Relationship status and quality?
      • Any recent changes or difficulties in the relationship?
      • Communication with partner about the ED? Partner's reaction?
    • Past sexual trauma or negative experiences?
  • MEDICAL HISTORY & LIFESTYLE:
    • Past Medical History:
      • Cardiovascular disease (hypertension, heart attack, stroke, peripheral vascular disease)
      • Diabetes Mellitus
      • Neurological conditions (e.g., MS, spinal cord injury, Parkinson's)
      • Endocrine disorders (e.g., thyroid problems, low testosterone)
      • Pelvic surgery or trauma (e.g., prostatectomy, pelvic radiation)
      • Peyronie's disease (penile curvature/pain)
      • Mental health conditions (depression, anxiety disorders)
    • Medications:
      • Prescribed medications? (Antihypertensives, antidepressants, antipsychotics, antiandrogens are common culprits)
      • Over-the-counter medications or supplements?
    • Lifestyle Factors:
      • Smoking history? (Quantity, duration)
      • Alcohol consumption? (Quantity, frequency)
      • Recreational drug use? (e.g., cannabis, cocaine, opiates)
      • Level of physical activity/exercise?
      • Diet? Obesity?
  • PATIENT'S PERSPECTIVE & IMPACT:
    • What are your main concerns about this problem?
    • How is it affecting you and/or your relationship?
    • What are your expectations from treatment?
    • Have you tried any treatments for ED before? If so, what and was it effective?
  • RED FLAGS (Consider):
    • Sudden onset with neurological symptoms (e.g., saddle anesthesia, leg weakness - cauda equina)
    • Symptoms suggestive of undiagnosed systemic illness (e.g., significant weight loss, fatigue with diabetes symptoms)
    • Penile pain, curvature, or nodules (Peyronie's disease)
    • Symptoms of severe depression or suicidality.
