MANAGEMENT PLAN — Suspected Adult-Onset Asthma

1. Investigations

  • Spirometry ± reversibility
  • FeNO > 40 ppb (eosinophilic)
  • Serial peak-flow diary (2 weeks)
  • Blood eosinophils, IgE
  • Baseline U&Es / LFTs
  • Occupational-health referral (wood dust, COSHH)

2. Initial Pharmacological Therapy (Step 0-1)

  • Low-dose budesonide-formoterol DPI 200/6 µg PRN (AIR)
  • or: Low-dose ICS (beclometasone 100 µg 2 puffs BD) + SABA
  • Provide spacer if pMDI
  • Oral prednisolone 40 mg × 5 days for acute exacerbation

3. Education, Technique & Safety-Net

  • Demonstrate inhaler & spacer technique – video 0:40
  • Issue Personal Asthma Action Plan + QR videos
  • Red-flags: PEF < 50 %, reliever > 10 puffs / 4 h, speech difficulty → 999
  • Peak-flow diary – video 0:30
  • Describe spirometry – video 0:25
  • Offer influenza & pneumococcal vaccine
  • Smoking/vaping advice & cessation referral

4. Lifestyle & Occupational Measures

  • Address anxiety; sign-post CBT resources
  • Employer COSHH: LEV, FFP3 fit-test, health surveillance
  • Healthy weight & graded exercise once controlled

5. Follow-Up & Escalation

  • Review 6–8 weeks with diary + FeNO
  • If uncontrolled: MART or add LTRA
  • Refer to specialist if ≥2 steroid bursts / yr or FEV₁ < 70 %

11-POINT QUALITATIVE FEEDBACK

  • Explain asthma proof = spirometry / FeNO, not blood – clip 3:30
  • Use UK terms: salbutamol reliever + steroid preventer, not “adrenaline-like”
  • Reflect patient emotions before new questions
  • Explicit empathy: acknowledge daily-life impact
  • Sign-post consultation stages early
  • Give evidence-based info; correct blood-test claim
  • Offer coping strategies for attack anxiety
  • Copy plan to GP / asthma-nurse & occupational-health
  • Arrange inhaler-technique check; know when to escalate
  • Document red-flags & 999 advice clearly
  • Keep sentences ≤ 25 words to save time & phenomes
