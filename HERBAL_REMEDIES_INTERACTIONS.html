  5. Many have unknown safety in pregnancy.
  6. St. John's Wort (Hypericum perforatum)
    Enzyme INDUCTION Effects:
    • CYP3A4 induction → ↓ Drug concentrations
      • Cyclosporine (transplant rejection)
      • Oral contraceptives (contraceptive failure)
      • Simvastatin (↓ cholesterol control)
      • Indinavir (HIV treatment failure)
    • P-glycoprotein induction → ↓ Drug absorption
      • Digoxin (↓ cardiac control)
      • Fexofenadine (↓ allergy control)
    Enzyme INHIBITION Effects:
    • CYP1A2 inhibition → ↑ Drug concentrations
      • Theophylline (toxicity risk)
      • Clozapine (CNS depression)
    Other Mechanisms:
    • Serotonin reuptake inhibition → Serotonin syndrome
      • SSRIs (fluoxetine, sertraline)
      • SNRIs (venlafaxine)
      • Tramadol
  7. Ginkgo Biloba
    Enzyme INHIBITION Effects:
    • CYP2C9 inhibition → ↑ Drug concentrations
      • Warfarin (↑ bleeding risk)
      • Phenytoin (CNS toxicity)
    • CYP3A4 inhibition → ↑ Drug concentrations
      • Midazolam (excessive sedation)
      • Calcium channel blockers (hypotension)
    Other Mechanisms:
    • Platelet aggregation inhibition → Bleeding risk
      • Aspirin
      • Clopidogrel
      • NSAIDs (ibuprofen)
  8. Goldenseal (Hydrastis canadensis)
    Enzyme INHIBITION Effects:
    • CYP2D6 inhibition → ↑ Drug concentrations
      • Codeine (↓ conversion to morphine)
      • Tamoxifen (↓ anti-cancer efficacy)
      • Beta-blockers (metoprolol toxicity)
    • CYP3A4 inhibition → ↑ Drug concentrations
      • Cyclosporine (nephrotoxicity)
      • Statins (myopathy risk)
    Other Mechanisms:
    • P-glycoprotein inhibition → ↑ Drug absorption
      • Digoxin (toxicity)
  9. Garlic (Allium sativum)
    Enzyme INHIBITION Effects:
    • CYP2E1 inhibition → ↑ Drug concentrations
      • Acetaminophen (hepatotoxicity)
      • Isoniazid (neurotoxicity)
    Enzyme INDUCTION Effects:
    • CYP3A4 induction → ↓ Drug concentrations
      • Saquinavir (HIV treatment failure)
    Other Mechanisms:
    • Platelet inhibition → Bleeding risk
      • Warfarin
      • Enoxaparin
  10. Ginseng (Panax ginseng)
    Enzyme INDUCTION Effects:
    • CYP3A4 induction → ↓ Drug concentrations
      • Nifedipine (↓ BP control)
    Enzyme INHIBITION Effects:
    • CYP2D6 inhibition → ↑ Drug concentrations
      • Haloperidol (EPS symptoms)
    Other Mechanisms:
    • Hypoglycemic effects
      • Insulin
      • Sulfonylureas
    • Monoamine oxidase inhibition
      • Phenelzine (hypertensive crisis)
  11. Echinacea
    Enzyme INDUCTION Effects:
    • CYP1A2 induction → ↓ Drug concentrations
      • Caffeine (↓ effect)
      • Olanzapine (↓ antipsychotic effect)
    Enzyme INHIBITION Effects:
    • CYP3A4 inhibition → ↑ Drug concentrations
      • Midazolam (excessive sedation)
  12. Valerian Root
    Enzyme INHIBITION Effects:
    • CYP3A4 inhibition → ↑ Drug concentrations
      • Alprazolam (respiratory depression)
    Other Mechanisms:
    • GABA potentiation → CNS depression
      • Barbiturates
      • Alcohol
      • Zolpidem
  13. Green Tea (Camellia sinensis)
    Enzyme INHIBITION Effects:
    • CYP3A4 inhibition → ↑ Drug concentrations
      • Simvastatin (rhabdomyolysis risk)
    • COMT inhibition → ↑ Catecholamines
      • Levodopa (dyskinesia)
    Other Mechanisms:
    • Vitamin K antagonism → ↓ Warfarin efficacy

Interaction Risk Levels:

  • High Risk: Life-threatening or severe outcomes
  • Moderate Risk: Clinically significant effects
  • Low Risk: Mild or theoretical interactions

Enzyme Systems Explained:

  • CYP3A4: Metabolizes 50% of clinical drugs
  • CYP2D6: Affects psychotropics and beta-blockers
  • CYP2C9: Crucial for warfarin metabolism
  • P-glycoprotein: Governs drug transport across membranes
