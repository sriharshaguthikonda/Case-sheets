1ST COMPLAINT: PAIN IN THE THROAT (Sore Throat)

  • S: Site "Can you point to where the pain is worst? Is it on one side or all over?"
    • GENERALIZED / CENTRAL: Viral Pharyngitis, Bacterial Tonsillitis
    • UNILATERAL (one-sided): Peritonsillar Abscess (Quinsy)
    • RETROSTERNAL / BURNING: Gastroesophageal Reflux Disease (GORD)
  • O: Onset "When did the sore throat start? Did it come on suddenly?"
    • ACUTE (< 2 weeks): Viral or Bacterial Infection
    • CHRONIC (> 3 weeks): Red flag - consider malignancy, GORD, Chronic tonsillitis
    • Triggered by sick contacts?
  • D: Duration "How many days have you had this for?"
  • A/R: Aggravating & Relieving Factors "Does anything make the pain better or worse?"
    • AGGRAVATING: Swallowing (Odynophagia), talking, eating
    • RELIEVING: Simple analgesia (paracetamol, ibuprofen), warm drinks
  • C: Character "What does the pain feel like? Is it scratchy, sharp like swallowing glass, or more like a lump?"
    • SCRATCHY / RAW: Typical for viral pharyngitis
    • SEVERE / SHARP: Suggests bacterial tonsillitis or quinsy
    • BURNING: Suggests GORD
    • "LUMP" SENSATION: Globus pharyngeus, or a mass
  • R: Radiation "Does the pain travel anywhere, for example, to your ears?"
    • Referred otalgia (ear pain) is common in severe tonsillitis/pharyngitis
  • A: Associated Symptoms "Along with the sore throat, have you had a fever, cough, runny nose, or any difficulty opening your mouth?"
    • CLUES FOR BACTERIAL (Strep Throat - Centor/FeverPAIN criteria):
      • Fever
      • Tonsillar exudates (pus on tonsils)
      • Tender anterior cervical lymph nodes
      • Absence of cough
    • CLUES FOR VIRAL:
      • Cough, coryza (runny nose), sneezing -> Common Cold
      • Profound fatigue, widespread lymph nodes -> Infectious Mononucleosis (Glandular Fever)
      • Hoarseness -> Laryngitis
    • CLUES FOR DEEP INFECTION (Quinsy):
      • Muffled "hot potato" voice
      • Trismus (difficulty opening the jaw)
      • Drooling / inability to swallow saliva
    • CLUES FOR GORD:
      • Heartburn, regurgitation, symptoms worse when lying down or after meals
  • P: Progression "Since it started, has it been getting progressively worse?"
  • P: Pattern "Is the pain constant or intermittent?"
  • E: Effect on Life "How has this affected you? Are you able to eat and drink normally?"
    • Assess for risk of dehydration if odynophagia is severe
  • R: Red Flags "I need to ask some specific questions to make sure we're not missing anything serious."
    • AIRWAY COMPROMISE: Stridor (noisy breathing), difficulty breathing, drooling
    • DEEP NECK SPACE INFECTION: Muffled voice, trismus, severe unilateral swelling, neck stiffness
    • SUSPECTED MALIGNANCY: Sore throat > 3 weeks, persistent hoarseness, unexplained weight loss, neck lump
    • Systemic illness: Non-blanching rash, signs of sepsis
    • Inability to swallow fluids (risk of dehydration)
  • S: Severity / Scale "On a scale of 1 to 10, how would you rate the pain?"
