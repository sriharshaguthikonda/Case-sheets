TEACHING STATION FRAMEWORK

A universal guide for conducting any examination or procedure in a teaching setting.

Phase 1: Pre-Procedure Protocol (The Setup)

1. Introduction & Identification
  • Greet the patient warmly.
  • State your name and role clearly (e.g., "I'm Dr. Smith, a senior doctor.").
  • Introduce the student by name and their role (e.g., "This is Alex, a third-year medical student working with me today.").
  • Confirm patient's identity (e.g., "Could you please confirm your full name and date of birth for me?").
2. Explanation & Consent
  • Explain the Indication: Briefly state why the procedure/examination is necessary (e.g., "To better understand your abdominal pain, we need to perform an examination of your tummy.").
  • Explain the Procedure: Clearly describe what will happen in simple terms. Mention what the patient will feel (e.g., "I will be gently pressing on different parts of your abdomen. It shouldn't be painful, but please let me know if you feel any discomfort.").
  • Check Contraindications: (Clinician to self/student) "Are there any reasons we shouldn't do this? Any recent surgery or specific conditions to be aware of?"
  • Gain Verbal Consent: Ask for permission explicitly (e.g., "Are you happy for us to proceed with the examination?").
3. Preparation & Positioning
  • Offer a Chaperone: "Would you feel more comfortable if we had another member of staff, a chaperone, present for the examination?"
  • Ensure Adequate Exposure: Explain what clothing needs to be adjusted or removed and why. Provide a blanket or sheet to maintain dignity.
  • Position the Patient: "I'll need you to lie flat on your back for this. Are you comfortable in that position?" (Adjust as needed, e.g., pillows, angle of bed).
  • Check for Pain: "Before we begin, are you in any pain at the moment?"

Phase 3: Instructional Dialogue (For the Student)

5. Explain & Teach

(This dialogue occurs between the instructor and the student, while always maintaining respect for the patient.)

  • Demonstrate Technique: "Notice how I hold the stethoscope..."
  • Explain Findings: "We can hear a normal 'lub-dub' sound, which corresponds to the first and second heart sounds. I don't hear any extra murmurs."
  • Ask Probing Questions: "Why do you think we check for a pulse in this location?" or "What would it mean if we found..."
  • Correct Technique: "Try adjusting your grip slightly like this for better control."

Phase 4: Post-Procedure Protocol (The Wrap-Up)

6. Conclusion & Closure
  • Thank the Patient: "Thank you very much for your time and for helping us with our teaching today."
  • Ensure Comfort: Help the patient to get dressed or cover up. Ask if they are comfortable.
  • Summarize Findings (to patient): "Everything from that examination seems normal." or "Based on that, we will..."
  • Address Doubts: "Do you have any questions for me? (To patient)" ... "Do you have any questions? (To student)"
