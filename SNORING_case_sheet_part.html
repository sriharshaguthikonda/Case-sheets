10TH COMPLAINT: SNORING AND SLEEP DISTURBANCES

S: Site
  • SNORING SOURCE
    • Nasal obstruction
    • Throat/Pharyngeal obstruction
    • Soft palate vibration
    • Tongue base obstruction
"Where do you feel the main issue is - in your nose or throat?"
O: Onset
  • SUDDEN ONSET
    • Recent upper respiratory infection
    • Allergic reaction
    • Trauma
  • GRADUAL ONSET
    • Weight gain
    • Aging
    • Anatomical changes
    • Hypothyroidism
  • LIFELONG
    • Anatomical factors
    • Genetic predisposition
"When did you first start snoring? Did it begin suddenly or develop gradually over time?"
D: Duration & Pattern
  • SHORT DURATION (weeks-months)
    • Acute condition
    • Recent weight gain
  • LONG DURATION (years)
    • Chronic anatomical issues
    • Long-standing obesity
  • PATTERN
    • Every night
    • Worse with fatigue
"How long have you been snoring? Is it every night or only sometimes? Does your position affect it?"
A: Aggravating Factors
  • ALCOHOL CONSUMPTION
  • SEDATIVE MEDICATIONS
  • SLEEPING ON BACK
  • FATIGUE/EXHAUSTION
  • WEIGHT GAIN
  • SMOKING
  • ALLERGIES/COLD
"Does anything make your snoring worse, such as alcohol, certain sleeping positions, or medications?"
R: Relieving Factors
  • SLEEPING ON SIDE
  • ELEVATED HEAD POSITION
  • WEIGHT LOSS
  • NASAL DECONGESTANTS
  • CPAP DEVICE
  • DENTAL APPLIANCES
"Is there anything that helps reduce your snoring?"
C: Character
  • SNORING QUALITY
    • Soft/quiet
    • Loud/disturbing
    • Intermittent
    • Continuous
    • High-pitched
    • Low-pitched/rumbling
  • BREATHING PATTERN
    • Regular
    • Irregular with pauses
    • Gasping/choking episodes
"How would you describe your snoring? Is it loud or soft? Does your breathing stop at times?"
R: Radiation
  • NO RADIATION (localized symptom)
"Does the snoring seem to come from your nose, throat, or both?"

  • DAYTIME SLEEPINESS
    • Falling asleep during activities
    • Morning grogginess
    • Poor concentration
  • MORNING SYMPTOMS

    • Chest pain
  • SLEEP DISTURBANCES

    • Night sweats

    • Insomnia
  • PSYCHOLOGICAL SYMPTOMS
    • Irritability/mood changes
    • Depression
    • Anxiety

  • CARDIOVASCULAR SYMPTOMS
    • Hypertension
    • Palpitations

  • PARTNER REPORTED SYMPTOMS

    • Gasping/choking sounds

  • NASAL SYMPTOMS
    • Nasal obstruction
    • Mouth breathing
    • Post-nasal drip
"What other symptoms do you experience? Do you feel tired during the day? Have you noticed any breathing pauses during sleep?"
P: Progression
  • WORSENING
    • Weight gain progression
    • Aging changes
    • Worsening nasal obstruction
  • STABLE
    • Chronic stable condition
  • IMPROVING
    • Weight loss
    • Treatment response
"Has your snoring changed over time? Is it getting worse, better, or staying the same?"
P: Pattern
  • POSITIONAL
    • Worse supine
    • Better lateral
  • TEMPORAL
    • Worse in early sleep cycles
    • Worse in REM sleep
    • Worse with alcohol
"Does your sleeping position affect your snoring? Is it worse at certain times during the night?"
E: Effect on Life
  • SOCIAL IMPACT
    • Partner disturbance
    • Relationship strain
    • Avoidance of shared sleeping
  • OCCUPATIONAL IMPACT
    • Decreased productivity
    • Increased errors
    • Safety concerns
  • QUALITY OF LIFE
    • Reduced energy
    • Limited activities
    • Health concerns
"How does your snoring affect your daily life, relationships, or work?"
R: Red Flags
  • WITNESSED APNEAS > 10 seconds
  • FREQUENT GASPING/CHOKING
  • EXCESSIVE DAYTIME SLEEPINESS (Epworth > 16)
  • UNCONTROLLED HYPERTENSION
  • CHEST PAIN/PALPITATIONS
  • FALLING ASLEEP WHILE DRIVING
  • MORNING HEADACHES
  • CONCENTRATION DIFFICULTIES
  • DEPRESSION/ANXIETY
  • CHILDREN: POOR SCHOOL PERFORMANCE, HYPERACTIVITY
"I need to ask some important questions to ensure we're not missing any serious issues. Have you ever stopped breathing during sleep? Do you feel extremely sleepy during the day?"
S: Severity / Scale
  • SNORING INTENSITY
    • Mild (occasional, quiet)
    • Moderate (frequent, noticeable)
    • Severe (constant, very loud, room-shaking)
  • DAYTIME SLEEPINESS SCALE
    • Epworth Sleepiness Scale
    • 0-10: Normal
    • 11-15: Mild sleepiness
    • 16-24: Severe sleepiness
"On a scale of 1-10, how severe would you rate your snoring? How sleepy do you feel during the day on a scale of 1-10?"

RISK FACTOR ASSESSMENT

  • ANATOMICAL FACTORS
    • Neck circumference (>17" men, >16" women)
    • BMI > 30 kg/m²
    • Crowded airway
    • Low, thick soft palate
    • Elongated uvula
    • Large tonsils/adenoids
    • Retrognathia/micrognathia
    • Deviated nasal septum
  • LIFESTYLE FACTORS
    • Alcohol consumption
    • Smoking
    • Sedentary lifestyle
    • Irregular sleep schedule
  • MEDICAL COMORBIDITIES
    • Hypothyroidism
    • Hypertension
    • Cardiovascular disease
    • Heart failure
    • Arrhythmias
    • Diabetes mellitus
    • Stroke/CVA

DIFFERENTIAL DIAGNOSIS

  • PRIMARY SNORING (Simple snoring without OSA)
  • OBSTRUCTIVE SLEEP APNEA (Mild, Moderate, Severe)
  • UPPER AIRWAY RESISTANCE SYNDROME
  • CENTRAL SLEEP APNEA
  • MIXED SLEEP APNEA
