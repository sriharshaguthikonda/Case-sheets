• S: Site
    • Where do you feel the cough originating from? (e.g.,
      • deep in the chest,
      • back of the throat)
  • O: Onset
    • When exactly did the cough start?
    • Was the onset sudden (acute) or did it develop over time (gradual/chronic)?
    • What were you doing when it first started?
  • D: Duration
    • How long has the cough been present in total (days, weeks, months)?
      • < 3 wks
      • 3-8 wks
      • > 8 wks

  • A/R: Aggravating / Relieving Factors
    • Aggravating Factors: What makes the cough worse? (e.g., lying down, exercise, cold air, dust, talking, eating, time of day like morning or night)
      • DIURINAL VARIATIONS
      • SEASONAL
      • POSITIONAL
      • Medication
      • TALKING
      • EATING
      • ASTHMA TYPE Qs
      • EXERCISE
      • COLD AIR
      • DUST
    • Relieving Factors: What makes the cough better? (e.g., specific positions like sitting up, warm drinks, medications, steam/humidity)
      • DIURINAL VARIATIONS
      • SEASONAL
      • POSITIONAL
  • C: Character/Type
    • dry
    • productive
    • mixed
  • R: Radiation
    • radiation


  • A: Associated Symptoms
    • Fever, chills, night sweats
    • Shortness of breath (dyspnea), wheezing
    • Chest pain, palpitations
    • Runny/stuffy nose, sore throat, hoarseness
    • Heartburn, acid taste in mouth (reflux)
    • Edema
  • P: Progression
    • How has the cough changed over time? Is it getting better, worse, or staying the same?
    • Has the character changed (e.g., from dry to productive)?
    • (Especially for post-treatment: How has the cough changed since starting the new medication/therapy?)
  • P: Pattern
    • Continuous: Is the cough present all the time?
    • Intermittent: Does it come and go?
      • frequency of episodes
      • Duration of episode
    • Is there a pattern related to time of day (e.g., only at night) or season?
  • E: Effect on Life
    • How is the cough impacting your quality of life, sleep, work, ability to exercise, or social activities?
    • Does it ever cause vomiting or lightheadedness?
  • R: Red Flags
    • Cancer
    • Have you coughed up any blood (hemoptysis)?
    • Are you having significant, persistent SOB?
    • Have you had any unintentional weight loss?
    • lumps or bumps anywhere?
    • TB
    • Is the cough associated with a high fever?
    • Do you have drenching night sweats?
    • Are you experiencing any chest pain?
  • S: Scale of Zero to 10
    • On a scale from 0 (no cough) to 10 (the worst cough imaginable), how would you rate its severity right now?
