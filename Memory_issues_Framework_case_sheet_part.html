1ST COMPLAINT: MEMORY ISSUES / COGNITIVE CONCERNS

Collateral History Source
  • Establish source and relationship. Confirm patient's consent for collateral history.
"Hello, thank you for coming in today with [Patient's Name]. Since we're talking about memory, the information you can provide as a [partner/child/carer] is incredibly valuable. What are the main changes you've noticed?"
O: Onset
  • ACUTE / SUBACUTE (Hours to Weeks):
    • Delirium (secondary to infection, metabolic cause, medication).
  • INSIDIOUS / GRADUAL (Months to Years):
    •  neurodegenerative process like Dementia.
"When did you first start noticing these memory problems? Was it a sudden change over a few days, or a very gradual decline over months or years?"
P: Progression
  • Steadily Progressive: Classic for Alzheimer's Disease.
  • Stepwise Decline: Suggests Vascular Dementia.
  • Fluctuating Course: Suggests Delirium or Dementia with Lewy Bodies (DLB).
"Since the problems started, have they been getting progressively worse, or have they been fluctuating day-to-day?"
C: Character of Cognitive Deficits
  • Memory (Amnesia):
    • Difficulty learning new information (repeating questions, forgetting recent events)?
    • Forgetting names of family, well-known facts?
  • Language (Aphasia):
    • Word-finding difficulties? Using wrong words or vague terms?
  • Executive Function:
    • Problems with planning, managing finances, cooking a familiar meal, problem-solving?
  • Visuospatial Skills:
    • Getting lost in familiar places? Difficulty judging distances or recognizing objects/faces?
  • Skilled Tasks (Apraxia):
    • Difficulty with familiar tasks like using a remote control or getting dressed?
    • are you finding it difficult to do taks that you used to do?
  • Behaviour
  • personality
  • psychosis
"Let's talk about the memory itself. What kind of things are being forgotten? And have you noticed any other difficulties, for example with language or tasks?"
A: Associated Symptoms
  • Psychiatric / Behavioural:
    • Depression (low mood, apathy, tearfulness) - "Pseudodementia"
    • Anxiety, agitation, or aggression
    • Hallucinations
      • (especially visual, suggests DLB) or delusions (paranoia)
    • Changes in personality or social inhibition
      • FTD
  • Neurological:
    • Gait disturbance (shuffling, unsteady) or falls -> 
      • NPH, 
      • Parkinson's, 
      • DLB,
      • Vascular
      • B12
    • Parkinsonism (tremor, stiffness, slowness) -> 
      • Parkinson's ,
      • DLB
    • Headaches, seizures, focal weakness
  • Systemic (Screen for Reversible Causes):
    • Fatigue, weight changes, feeling cold (Hypothyroidism)
    • (B12 deficiency)
      • Pins and needles,
      • balance issues
      • Anemia sx
    • Snoring, witnessed apneas, daytime sleepiness (Sleep Apnea)
    • (Delirium)
      • Fever or other infections
  • Electrolyte Disturbances
    • Na
    • Ca
"Alongside the memory problems, have you noticed any changes in mood, behaviour, or physical abilities?"
E: Effect on Life (Function)
  • Instrumental ADLs: Managing money, shopping, cooking, transportation, using the phone.
  • Basic ADLs: Bathing, dressing, grooming, toileting, feeding.
  • Safety Concerns: Driving, wandering, leaving the stove on.
  • (Note: Impairment in IADLs/ADLs is what separates dementia from Mild Cognitive Impairment).
"How are these changes affecting day-to-day life? Is there any difficulty with managing finances, medications, cooking, or personal care like washing and dressing?"
R: Risk Factors
  • Family history of dementia
  • Cardiovascular risk factors (Hypertension, Diabetes, High Cholesterol, Smoking)
  • History of stroke or TIA
  • Significant past head injury
  • History of depression
  • Alcohol excess
"I need to ask about some past history. Is there a family history of dementia? Any history of stroke, heart disease, or head injuries?"
Insight
  • Anosognosia (poor insight) is common in many dementias.
  • Patients with depression often have good insight and are very worried about their memory.
(To patient) "Are you aware of any problems with your memory?" (To informant) "Do they seem aware or concerned about these changes?"
R: Red Flags
  • Rapid cognitive decline (over weeks to months)
  • Early onset (< 65 years old)
  • Associated new focal neurological signs (weakness, visual loss)
  • Signs of NPH Normal Pressure Hydrocephalus: Cognitive impairment, gait disturbance, and urinary incontinence ("Wet, Wacky, and Wobbly")
  • Signs of delirium (acute, fluctuating course)
"I'm just summarizing the key warning signs I'm looking for."
S: Score
  • (Perform and document a cognitive screen, e.g., GPCOG, MOCA, ACE-III)
  • MMSE
(Clinician to self/patient) "I'm just going to ask a few simple questions to get a baseline of your memory and thinking."
