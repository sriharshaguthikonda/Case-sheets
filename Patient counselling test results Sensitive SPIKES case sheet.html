    MANAGEMENT PLAN (SPIKES Framework)

    • S: Setting up the Interview
        Environment: Private, quiet
        "This room is private so we can speak freely."
        Rapport: Build trust
        "I’m here to support you through this."
        Participants: Confirm attendees
        "Would you like anyone else here, like family?"
    • P : pause at places!
    • P: Perception (Assess patient’s understanding)
        Open-ended questions
        "Can you tell me what you understand so far?"
        Identify misconceptions
        "It sounds like you think [misconception]; let’s clarify."
    • I: Invitation (Patient’s preference for information)
        Information level
        "Do you want all the details or just the main points?"
        Specific concerns
        "Is there something specific you’re worried about?"
    • K: Knowledge (Provide clear information)
        Simple language
        "The tests show [diagnosis] which means [explanation]."
        Avoid jargon
        "Instead of ‘metastasis,’ it means the cancer has spread."
        Chunk and check
        "Does that make sense? Any questions so far?"
    • E: Emotions (Address emotional reactions)
        Acknowledge feelings
        "It’s okay to feel upset or scared about this."
        Offer support
        "Take your time; I’m here for you."
    • S: Strategy and Summary
        Next steps
        "We can explore [option, e.g., palliative care] to help you."
        Summarize
        "To recap, we discussed [key points]. Is that correct?"
        Resources
        "Here’s a pamphlet on [topic] for more info."
        Follow-up
        "Let’s meet again on [date] to check in."

    SPECIFIC SCENARIOS

    • Breaking Bad News
        Diagnosis/prognosis
        "The cancer is advanced, so we’ll focus on comfort."
        Emotional support
        "This is a lot to take in; how are you feeling?"
    • Capacity and Consent:
        Mental state
        “David, just to check we’re on the same page—can you tell me today’s date and where we are now?”
        Understanding
        “In your own words, what does this antibiotic drip aim to do for your chest infection?”
        Retention
        *(two minutes later)* “Can you remind me what we said might happen if we stop the antibiotics today?”
        Balancing pros & cons
        “When you think about the good and the downside of continuing treatment, how do they weigh up for you?”
        Expressing a decision
        “What feels like the right plan for you just now? You can say it, nod, or even squeeze my hand.”
        Voluntariness
        “Has anyone—family or staff—made you feel pushed one way or the other about treatment?”
        Insight into consequences
        “If your breathing worsened without the drip, what would you like us to do at that point?”
        Consistency of choice
        “Is this how you’ve felt over the last day or two, or has anything changed your mind today?”
        Support to decide
        “Shall we close the curtain and take our time so it’s easier to chat? Let me know if you’d like your glasses or a rest.”
        Presumption of capacity
        *(internal stance—no specific question; start by assuming David can decide unless proven otherwise)*
        Best-interest planning (if capacity lacking)
        “If ever you couldn’t tell us your wishes, we’d focus on keeping you comfortable and avoiding anything overly burdensome—does that sit right with you?”
    • Confidentiality / DVLA / Legal Disclosures
        Explain duty
        "For safety, I must report your seizures to DVLA."
        Support patient
        "I’ll help you with transport options."
    • Safeguarding & Domestic Violence
        Assess risk
        "Can you tell me more about what happened?"
        Report and support
        "We’ll contact a social worker to keep you safe."
    • Difficult Management Conversations
        Treatment planning
        "Here are your options; what feels right for you?"
        Error disclosure
        "I’m sorry, there was a mistake with [issue]; we’re fixing it."
    • End-of-Life Care Discussions
        Preferences
        "Would you prefer to focus on comfort at home?"
        Advance care planning
        "Have you thought about a DNR order?"
    • Discussing Prognosis
        Be honest
        "We can’t predict exactly, but it may be months."
        Address uncertainty
        "We’ll keep monitoring and update you."
    • Addressing Non-Compliance
        Explore reasons
        "I see you’re hesitant about surgery—can you tell me why?"
        Respect autonomy
        "It’s your choice, and I’m here to support you."
