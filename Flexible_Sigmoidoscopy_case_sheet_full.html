FOCUSED HISTORY – SODA CRAPPERS Framework

  1. S – Site
    • Per-rectal bleeding location [NICE NG12]
  2. O – Onset
    • First noticed bleeding? Sudden/gradual
  3. D – Duration
    • Number of weeks / episodic vs. continuous
  4. A – Aggravating / R – Relieving
    • Straining, constipation, NSAID use
    • Effect of laxatives / stool-softeners
  5. C – Character
    • Bright-red vs. dark / mixed-in / clots
  6. R – Radiation
    • Left-lower-quadrant or back pain suggestive of diverticular disease
  7. A – Associated symptoms
    • Change in bowel habit, mucus, tenesmus
    • Fatigue, SOB on exertion (anaemia)
    • Weight loss, appetite changes
  8. P – Progression / Pattern
    • Increasing frequency or volume over time
  9. E – Effect on life
    • Anxiety, planning day around toilets
    • Time off work / carer responsibilities
  10. R – Red-flags
    • Syncope, haemodynamic compromise – consider admission [NHS Endoscopy]
    • Iron-deficiency anaemia on FBC
  11. S – Severity / Scale
    • Patient-rated 0–10 impact or concern level

Background & Risk Assessment

  • Medications: anticoagulants / antiplatelets [BWCE Leaflet]
  • Allergies: latex, lignocaine, sedatives
  • Comorbidities: cardiopulmonary, diabetes (sedation risk) ([V2] 4:40)
  • Previous GI investigations / surgeries
  • Family history: bowel/other GI cancers
  • Social: smoking, alcohol, diet (fibre)

Capacity & Consent Checks

  • Assess understanding & recall of information – GMC *Decision Making & Consent*
  • Confirm accompaniment home post-sedation
  • Offer chaperone / interpreter

MANAGEMENT PLAN

1 | Urgency & Setting

  • Elective day-case flexible sigmoidoscopy (current scenario)
  • Escalate to on-call colorectal team if massive ongoing PR bleed or haemodynamic instability – colonoscopy/theatre

2 | Pre-Procedure Checks

  1. Confirm indication & rule-out need for full colonoscopy (age ≥50 + rectal bleeding per NICE NG12)
  2. FBC, U&Es; INR / anti-Xa if on anticoagulants – liaise for bridging
  3. Medication review: stop iron 7 d pre, hold oral hypoglycaemics morning of test
  4. Allergy screen (latex, lignocaine, pethidine/midazolam)
  5. Bowel prep: phosphate enema 2 h pre-scope; fast solids 6 h, clear fluids 2 h ([V1] 0:40)
  6. Capacity check & consent (verbal + electronic record) – patient can withdraw at any time

3 | Procedure Explanation (teach-back)

  • Flexible tube with camera passed ~60 cm via rectum; CO₂ insufflation for view ([V1] 0:28, [V1] 1:40)
  • Options: No sedation, Entonox (Gas & Air), or IV midazolam ± fentanyl – discuss pros/cons ([V1] 1:15, [V2] 4:40)
  • Possible actions: biopsy, polypectomy, tattoo for follow-up colonoscopy ([V1] 1:48, [V1] 1:55)
  • Material risks: discomfort/colic common ([V1] 2:08); bleeding ≈ 1 in 4000 ([V1] 2:40, [V2] 3:25), perforation ≈ 1 in 1000–3000 ([V1] 2:40, [V2] 2:25), missed lesion ≈ 5 %
  • Benefits: direct visual diagnosis, same-session biopsy/polypectomy, rapid results ([V2] 1:05)
  • Alternatives: CT colonography, full colonoscopy, watch-and-wait with FIT depending on risk profile ([V2] 1:40)

4 | Intra-Procedure Safety

  • Continuous pulse-ox & BP monitoring if sedated
  • O₂ via nasal specs if sats <94 %
  • Resus drugs & perforation kit available

5 | Post-Procedure Care & Discharge

  1. Observe 30 min or until Aldrete ≥ 9 ([V1] 2:20)
  2. Give written result summary; biopsies pending 7–14 days ([V1] 2:33)
  3. Red-flag advice (return A&E if): severe abdo pain, temp >38.5 °C, persistent bleeding >50 ml, syncope ([V1] 2:53)
  4. Sedation after-effects: no driving, signing legal docs, alcohol or machinery for 24 h
  5. Escort home confirmed (wife present)

6 | Follow-Up Plan

  • Telephone clinic in 10 days for histology ± referral to colorectal MDT if dysplasia/cancer ([V1] 2:33)
  • Arrange full colonoscopy if scope incomplete or proximal pathology suspected
  • GP copy with FIT result & any medication changes

7 | Patient Education & Resources

  • NHS leaflet – ‘Flexible Sigmoidoscopy: your guide’ (King’s College Hospital)
  • Crohn’s & Colitis UK, Bowel Cancer UK helplines
  • 24 h Endoscopy Unit number for queries

8 | Documentation

  • Smart-phrase note: history, consent discussion, risks/benefits, sedation dose, findings, safety-net advice
  • SNOMED code: ‘Flexible sigmoidoscopy with biopsy’

11-Point Qualitative Feedback

  1. Language: avoid slang (“scope”), clarify size & comfort.
  2. Listening: reflect fear of serious findings.
  3. Empathy: validate anxiety, avoid minimising phrases.
  4. Issues: elicit main patient goal upfront.
  5. Structure: sign-post agenda transitions.
  6. Professionalism: avoid trivialising duration.
  7. Consistency with chosen form of address.
  8. Emotion handling: offer coping strategies.
  9. Team-working: brief nursing team on plan.
  10. Self-awareness: verify risk statistics if unsure.
  11. Teach-Back: confirm understanding of red-flags.
