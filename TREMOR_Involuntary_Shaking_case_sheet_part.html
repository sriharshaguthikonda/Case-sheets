1. SITE (S):
    1. Where does the tremor occur? (e.g., Hands, fingers, arm, leg, jaw, chin, lips, tongue)
    2. Is it on one side of the body (unilateral) or both (bilateral)?
    3. If bilateral, did it start on one side and then spread to the other? Which side started first?
    4. Is it more prominent in the upper or lower limbs?
  2. ONSET (O):
    1. When did you (or someone else) first notice the tremor? (Years/Months ago)
    2. Was the onset sudden or did it develop gradually over time?
    3. Was there any specific event, illness, or medication started around the time it began?
  3. DURATION (D) :
    1. How long have you had the tremor?
    2. Is the tremor present all the time, or does it come and go (intermittent)?
    3. If intermittent, how long do episodes last? How frequent are they?
  4. AGGRAVATING/RELIEVING FACTORS (A/R):
    1. What makes the tremor worse?
      1. Stress, anxiety, fatigue?
      2. When concentrating on a task with another limb?
      3. When walking?
    2. What makes the tremor better or temporarily disappear?
      1. Voluntary movement or action of the affected limb?
      2. Sleep? (Tremor usually absent during sleep)
      3. Alcohol? (Less typical for PD tremor, more for essential tremor)
      4. Any medications?
  5. CHARACTER/TYPE (C) :
    1. Is it more noticeable when your limb is at rest (Resting Tremor)? (e.g., when your hand is resting in your lap)
    2. Does it improve or disappear when you are using the limb for an action (e.g., reaching for an object, writing)?
    3. Does it worsen when you are holding a posture (Postural Tremor)? (e.g., holding arms outstretched)
    4. Can you describe the movement? (e.g., "pill-rolling" movement of fingers, back-and-forth)

  6. RADIATION (R): (Less applicable for tremor itself, more about spread)
    1. Has the tremor spread from its original site to other parts of the body over time? If so, where?
  7. ASSOCIATED SYMPTOMS (A):
    1. Stiffness or rigidity in the limbs? (See next complaint)
    2. Slowness of movement? (Bradykinesia - see next complaint)
    3. Difficulty with balance or walking?
    4. Changes in handwriting (micrographia - becoming smaller and more cramped)?
    5. Changes in speech (softer, monotone, hesitant - hypophonia)?
    6. Difficulty with fine motor tasks (e.g., buttoning, cutting food)?
    7. Masked facies (reduced facial expression)?
    8. Pain or discomfort associated with the tremor or stiffness?
  8. PROGRESSION (P):
    1. How has the tremor changed since it first started? (Become more noticeable, spread, amplitude increased?)
    2. Has it changed in frequency or character?
  9. PATTERN (P):
    1. Is it continuous when at rest, or does it have periods of being better and worse throughout the day?
  10. RED FLAGS (R): (For PD, red flags might indicate atypical parkinsonism or other conditions)
    1. Rapid progression of symptoms?
    2. Early onset of severe balance problems or falls?
    3. Early severe autonomic dysfunction (e.g., severe postural hypotension, urinary incontinence)?
    4. Symmetrical onset of tremor?
    5. Lack of response to Parkinson's medications (if tried)?
    6. Presence of other neurological signs (e.g., cerebellar signs, pyramidal signs, eye movement abnormalities not typical for PD)?
  11. EFFECT ON LIFE (E):
    1. How does the tremor affect your daily activities (e.g., eating, dressing, writing, work, hobbies)?
    2. Does it cause you embarrassment or social withdrawal?
    3. How much does it bother you?
  12. SCALE (S):
    1. On a scale of 0 to 10, with 0 being no tremor and 10 being the worst imaginable tremor, how would you rate its severity at its worst? And on average?
