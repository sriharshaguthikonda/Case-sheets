• S: Site
    • Which nipple? (Right/Left/Bilateral)
    • From a single duct or multiple ducts? (Ask patient if they can identify)
  • O: Onset
    • When did the discharge start?
    • Is it spontaneous or only on expression/squeezing?
  • D: Duration
    • How long has the discharge been occurring?
    • Is it persistent or intermittent? Frequency of episodes?
  • A/R: Aggravating / Relieving Factors
    • Aggravating Factors: Does anything trigger or increase it (e.g.,
      •  pressure,
      • Pregnancy
      • lactation
      • Hormones
      • Medicines
      • Any specific movements, or activities that make it more prominent or painful?
    • Relieving Factors: Does anything reduce it?
  • C: Character/Type (Quality & Colour) & Quantity
    • Quantity: Scant (stains bra), moderate, profuse.
    • Colour and Consistency:
      • Watery (Serous): Common in Fibroadenosis, Duct Ectasia; can also be Intraductal Papilloma, early Cancer.
      • Pus (Purulent): Suggests Mammary abscess, Mastitis.
      • Sero-sanguinous (pinkish/blood-tinged watery): Concerning for Duct Papilloma, Duct Ectasia, or Cancer.
      • Blood (Sanguinous - frank blood): Highly suspicious for Duct Papilloma or Cancer.
      • Altered blood (dark brown/greenish): Duct Ectasia, resolving hematoma within duct.
      • Milk (Galactorrhea): Normal during/post lactation; Galactocele (milky cyst); Mammary fistula. Pathological if unrelated to pregnancy/lactation.
    • Odour (if any).
  • R: Radiation (Not applicable to discharge itself).
  • A: Associated Symptoms
    • Presence of a breast lump (as per 1st complaint).
    • Breast pain (as per 2nd complaint).
    • Nipple retraction or inversion (as per 4th complaint).
    • Skin changes on the breast or nipple/areola (e.g., eczema-like rash for Paget's disease).
    • Fever (if infective cause).
    • Symptoms of galactorrhea (if milky discharge): Headaches, visual disturbances, amenorrhea (suggesting possible pituitary cause).
    • Relation to menstrual cycle.
  • P: Progression
    • Has the amount, colour, consistency, or frequency of the discharge changed over time?
  • P: Pattern
    • Is it continuous, intermittent, or only with manipulation?
  • E: Effect on life
    • Staining of clothes, need for pads.
    • Embarrassment, anxiety about the cause.
  • R: Red Flags (for nipple discharge)
    • Spontaneous, unilateral, single-duct discharge.
    • Bloody (sanguinous) or sero-sanguinous discharge.
    • Associated with a palpable breast mass.
    • Persistent discharge in a non-lactating, non-pregnant woman, especially if over 40.
    • Associated nipple retraction or skin changes.
  • S: Scale (Not directly applicable, perhaps a bother score for the patient).
