1. SITE (S):
    1. Which ear is affected (Right/Left/Both)?
    2. If both, is it equal or worse in one ear?
    3. Does the sound seem to be located in the ear(s) or more generally in the head?
  2. ONSET (O):
    1. When did the tinnitus first start?
    2. Was the onset sudden or gradual?
    3. Was it related to any specific event (e.g., loud noise exposure, head injury, ear infection, medication change, period of stress)?
  3. DURATION (D):
    1. How long have you had the tinnitus?
    2. Is it constant or intermittent? If intermittent, how long do episodes last and how often do they occur?
  4. AGGRAVATING/RELIEVING FACTORS (A/R):
    1. Aggravating Factors: What makes the tinnitus worse or more noticeable?
      1. Quiet environments?
      2. Stress, anxiety, fatigue, lack of sleep?
      3. Certain foods or drinks (e.g., caffeine, salt, alcohol, MSG)?
      4. Loud noise exposure?
      5. Certain head or neck movements or positions?
      6. Clenching jaw or dental problems?
    2. Relieving Factors: Does anything make the tinnitus better or less noticeable?
      1. Background noise (e.g., fan, music, white noise machine)?
      2. Relaxation techniques or mindfulness?
      3. Distraction through activities?
      4. Any medications or supplements?
      5. Masking devices (if used)?
  5. CHARACTER/TYPE (C):
    1. How would you describe the sound(s) you hear?
      1. Ringing, buzzing, hissing, whistling, clicking, roaring, humming, cicada-like, rushing water?
      2. Is it pulsatile (like a heartbeat or whooshing in time with your pulse)?
      3. Is it a single sound or multiple different sounds?
      4. Is it high-pitched or low-pitched?
      5. Does the sound vary in intensity or pitch?
      6. Is it a tonal sound or more like noise?
  6. RADIATION (R): (Not typically applicable)
  7. ASSOCIATED SYMPTOMS (A):
    1. Hearing loss (in one or both ears)?
    2. Dizziness or vertigo (sensation of spinning or unsteadiness)?
    3. Ear pain (otalgia)?
    4. Ear discharge (otorrhea)?
    5. Feeling of fullness or pressure in the ear?
    6. Headache?
    7. Sensitivity to loud sounds (hyperacusis)?
    8. History of significant loud noise exposure (occupational, recreational, acoustic trauma)?
    9. Use of ototoxic medications?
    10. Symptoms of Temporomandibular Joint (TMJ) disorder (jaw pain, clicking, limited opening)?
    11. Symptoms suggestive of Meniere's disease (episodic vertigo, fluctuating hearing loss, aural fullness)?
    12. Symptoms of anxiety, depression, or insomnia?
    13. Neck pain or stiffness?
    14. History of head or neck trauma?
  8. PROGRESSION (P):
    1. Has the tinnitus changed in character, intensity, or frequency since it started?
    2. Is it getting worse, better, or staying the same?
    3. Has its impact on your life changed?
    4. Response to any previous treatments?
  9. PATTERN (P):
    1. Is it more noticeable at certain times of the day (e.g., at night when it's quiet)?
    2. Is it always present (continuous) or does it come in episodes (intermittent)?
  10. EFFECT ON LIFE (E):
    1. How much does the tinnitus bother you or interfere with your life?
    2. Does it affect your sleep?
    3. Does it impact your concentration, ability to work, or enjoy quiet activities?
    4. Does it affect your mood (e.g., cause annoyance, frustration, anxiety, depression)?
    5. Has it led to social withdrawal?
  11. RED FLAGS (R):
    1. Unilateral tinnitus (especially if new onset and persistent).
    2. Pulsatile tinnitus (rhythmic with heartbeat - may indicate vascular abnormality, glomus tumor).
    3. Tinnitus associated with sudden or rapidly progressive hearing loss.
    4. Tinnitus accompanied by neurological symptoms (e.g., facial weakness, numbness, visual disturbances, significant balance problems/vertigo, severe headache).
    5. Tinnitus after head trauma.
    6. Objective tinnitus (audible to the examiner).
  12. SCALE (S) (Severity/Bothersomeness):
    1. On a scale of 0-10, where 0 is no tinnitus and 10 is the loudest imaginable tinnitus, how would you rate its loudness?
    2. On a scale of 0-10, where 0 is not at all bothersome and 10 is extremely bothersome/distressing, how much does it bother you?
