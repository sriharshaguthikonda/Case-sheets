COMPLAINT: OBSESSIVE-COMPULSIVE DISORDER (OCD)

  • S: Symptom Domains "To start, could you tell me about the main thoughts that bother you, and then describe the things you feel you have to do in response?"
    • Contamination (germs, dirt, chemicals)
    • Harm/Responsibility (fear of causing harm to self or others)
    • Symmetry & Ordering ("just right" feelings)
    • Unacceptable Thoughts (often sexual, religious, or violent in nature)
  • O: Onset & Triggers "When do you think this first started? Was there a particularly stressful event in your life around that time?"
    • Age of onset (childhood, adolescence, early adulthood)
    • Associated with a trigger? (e.g., trauma, illness, major life change)
  • D: Duration "When you are in the middle of a ritual, how long can it last? And how long has this been a significant problem for you?"
  • A/R: Aggravating & Relieving Factors "What situations tend to trigger these thoughts? What, if anything, provides relief, even for a short time?"
    • AGGRAVATING: Specific triggers (e.g., touching a doorknob, seeing a knife, stress)
    • RELIEVING: Performing the compulsion/ritual provides temporary relief from anxiety
  • C: Character (Obsessions & Compulsions) "Let's break this down. First, tell me more about the thoughts themselves..."
    • OBSESSIONS (The Thoughts)
      • Nature: Are they unwanted thoughts, images, or urges that are intrusive and repetitive?
      • Content: Fear of germs? Fear of harming someone? Need for things to be perfect? Blasphemous thoughts?
      • Ego-dystonic: Do these thoughts feel foreign to you, or against your true values?
    • COMPULSIONS (The Rituals / Behaviours)
      • Nature: "What do you feel you *have* to do to get rid of the anxiety from these thoughts?"
      • Content:
        • Washing/Cleaning
        • Checking (locks, appliances, that you haven't harmed someone)
        • Repeating/Counting
        • Ordering/Arranging
        • Mental compulsions (e.g., praying, replacing a "bad" thought with a "good" one)
        • Reassurance seeking
  • Insight "Is there a part of you that recognizes that these thoughts or behaviours might be excessive or irrational?"
    • Good/Fair Insight vs. Poor/Absent Insight
  • A: Associated Symptoms (Comorbidity) "Alongside these issues, have you been feeling low in your mood, or particularly anxious in general?"
    • Symptoms of Depression (low mood, anhedonia, sleep/appetite changes)
    • Symptoms of other Anxiety Disorders (panic attacks, social anxiety)
    • Tics or Tourette's Syndrome
    • In children: any link to a recent streptococcal infection? (PANDAS)
  • P: Progression "Over time, would you say the obsessions and compulsions have gotten worse, better, or stayed the same?"
  • P: Pattern "Is this a constant battle every day, or do you have good days and bad days?"
  • E: Effect on Life (Functional Impairment) "How much time would you say these thoughts and rituals take up each day? How do they interfere with your work, relationships, or social life?"
    • Time Consuming (e.g., > 1 hour per day) - Key diagnostic criterion
    • Avoidance of situations, people, or objects
    • Impact on work/school performance
    • Strain on family and social relationships
  • R: Red Flags & Risk "With everything that's been going on, I need to ask, have you had any thoughts of harming yourself or that life isn't worth living?"
    • Suicidal Ideation / Self-Harm
    • Severe depression or hopelessness
    • Symptoms of psychosis (if insight is absent)
    • Extreme functional impairment (e.g., unable to leave the house)
  • S: Severity / Scale "On a scale of 1 to 10, how much distress do these obsessions cause you, and how much do the compulsions interfere with your life?"
    • (Clinician note: Consider formal rating with Y-BOCS - Yale-Brown Obsessive Compulsive Scale)
