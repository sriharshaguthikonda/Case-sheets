FOCUSED HISTORY – SODA CRAPPERS Framework

  1. S – Situation / Site
    • Context of vomiting (bathroom, after meals, alone/with others) [NICE NG69]
  2. O – Onset
    • Started 8 months ago after (trigger?) exams / bullying / breakup
  3. D – Duration & Frequency
    • 2-3× daily vomiting – clarify binge episode length & calories
  4. A – Aggravating / R – Relieving
    • Aggravated by stress, high-calorie foods; Relieved temporarily by sense of “control”
  5. C – Character
    • Self-induced emesis with fingers / object? Any blood or coffee-grounds?
  6. R – Radiation
    • Throat pain radiating to chest (possible oesophagitis/M-W tear)
  7. A – Associated Sx
    • Amenorrhoea × 5–6 mo
    • Palpitations / presyncope
    • Dry skin, hair thinning, lanugo
    • Low mood, anxiety, self-harm thoughts?
  8. P – Progression / Pattern
    • Increasing purge frequency; weight trend ↓?
  9. P – Precipitants / Pattern
    • Intermittent vs continuous cycle; weekend binges?
  10. E – Effect on life
    • School absence, social withdrawal, sports stopped
  11. R – Red-flags
    • Syncope, chest pain ➔ risk of arrhythmia / electrolyte collapse
    • Haematemesis ➔ Mallory-Weiss tear
  12. S – Severity scale
    • Distress 0-10; functional impairment score

SYSTEMIC & BACKGROUND HISTORY

1 | General & Psychiatric
  • Screen low mood (PHQ-9) / anhedonia / suicidal ideation [NICE CKS]
  • Anxiety (GAD-7) / OCD traits
  • Body-image distortion questions (mirror checking, “fat” despite under-weight)
  • Laxative, diuretic, diet-pill, caffeine-tablet misuse
  • Excess exercise (> 60 min cardio post meals)
2 | Cardiovascular & Respiratory
  • Palpitations, dizziness, chest pain, exertional intolerance
  • Orthostatic hypotension symptoms
3 | Gastrointestinal
  • Reflux / heartburn, epigastric pain, abdominal bloating
  • Constipation vs diarrhoea, laxative dependence
  • Dental pain / enamel erosion [NICE CKS]
4 | Endocrine & Gynaecology
  • Amenorrhoea, oligomenorrhoea, menarche age
  • Bone-health risk: fractures, stress fractures? (DEXA later)
  • Sexual activity, contraception, pregnancy possibility
5 | Social & Safeguarding
  • School performance, absenteeism, peer relationships
  • Bullying (in-person/online), body-shaming, abuse enquiry
  • Substance use: alcohol, cannabis, stimulants, vaping
  • Support network – mother aware, friends, counsellor
6 | Past / Family / Drug History
  • Past medical conditions (coeliac, diabetes, thyroid)
  • Regular meds / OTC / supplements
  • Allergies & intolerances
  • Family history: eating disorders, depression, anxiety

MANAGEMENT PLAN

1 | Urgency & Risk

  • Immediate hospital admission if: BMI < 15, K⁺ < 2.5 mmol/L, pulse < 40 bpm, QTc > 470 ms, syncope, haematemesis, suicidal intent [Junior MARSIPAN]
  • If any red-flag present, discuss with on-call paediatrics & arrange same-day transfer

2 | Investigations

  • Baseline bloods: FBC, U&Es (Na, K, Mg, PO₄), LFTs, bone profile, glucose, TFTs, serum amylase [NICE NG69]
  • 12-lead ECG – look for prolonged QT, arrhythmia
  • β-hCG (pregnancy screen)
  • DEXA if amenorrhoea > 6 months or fracture risk
  • Repeat labs weekly until stable; consider lying/standing BP at each review

3 | Specialist Referrals

  • Urgent (<7 days) CAMHS Eating-Disorder team via e-RS (Access & Waiting-Time Standard) [NHSE 2015]
  • CBT-E therapist (20 sessions) – first-line treatment [NICE NG69]
  • Family-based therapy for eating disorders (FBT-ED) – offer to parent(s)/carer
  • Dietitian – meal plan & electrolyte re-feeding protocol
  • Dentist – enamel protection, fluoride varnish
  • School nurse – attendance support & exam concessions

4 | Non-pharmacological Management

  • Psycho-education: cycle of binge-purge, effects on heart, teeth, bones
  • Regular, structured meals; avoid “pro-ana” social-media triggers
  • Crisis plan & coping skills (BEAT “STRIPED” cards, distress-tolerance)
  • Encourage gradual re-engagement with friends, hobbies, PE if safe

5 | Pharmacological (Adjunct)

  • Fluoxetine 60 mg OD if persistent bulimic symptoms after CBT-E **or** moderate-to-severe depression (check K⁺ & QTc first) [NICE NG69]

6 | Safety-Netting

  • Seek ED urgently for chest pain, palpitations, blackout, severe weakness
  • Attend if vomiting blood or severe abdominal pain
  • 24/7 crisis line & CAMHS out-of-hours number if suicidal thoughts

7 | Patient / Carer Resources

  • BEAT Youth Helpline & online chat rooms
  • NHS Choices Bulimia page (leaflet printed)
  • “Bulimia & Me” self-monitoring app

8 | Follow-up & Monitoring

  • GP review in 1 week with results + weight/BMI
  • Weekly weight, vitals, U&Es until BMI > 18.5 & labs stable, then monthly
  • CAMHS therapist review schedule per CBT-E protocol

9 | Documentation

  • ICE summary, risk assessment, investigations ordered, referrals made
  • Confidentiality discussion & Gillick competence recorded
  • Copy of care plan given to patient (and parent if consented)
