1ST COMPLAINT: ERECTILE DYSFUNCTION

S: Site & Character of the Problem
  • Difficulty achieving an erection (Initiation)
  • Difficulty maintaining an erection (Maintenance)
  • Reduced rigidity of erections
  • Is there a change in your desire for sex (libido)?
"To start, could you tell me a bit more about the problem? Is the main issue getting an erection, or is it maintaining it?"
O: Onset
  • SUDDEN ONSET: Suggests a psychological cause (e.g., new relationship, stress, performance anxiety).
  • GRADUAL ONSET: Suggests an organic cause (e.g., vascular, neurological, hormonal).
"When did you first notice this was happening? Did it start suddenly, or has it been a gradual change over time?"
D: Duration
"How long has this been a concern for you?"
P: Pattern & Context
  • Presence of Nocturnal/Morning Erections -> Strongly suggests a psychological cause (the physical 'machinery' is working).
  • Absence of Nocturnal/Morning Erections -> Suggests an organic cause.
  • Does it occur in some situations but not others? (e.g., present with masturbation but absent with a partner?)
"This is a very important question: do you ever wake up with an erection in the morning, or have one during the night?"
A: Associated Symptoms
  • VASCULAR CLUES:
    • Pain in your calves when you walk that gets better with rest (claudication)?
    • Any chest pain, palpitations, or shortness of breath?
  • NEUROLOGICAL CLUES:
    • Any numbness, tingling, or weakness in your legs or groin area?
    • Any significant back pain or history of back injury/surgery?
    • Any changes to your urination or bowel control?
  • ENDOCRINE CLUES:
    • Decreased sex drive (libido), reduced energy?
    • Breast tissue development (gynecomastia)?
    • Loss of body hair, smaller or softer testes?
  • PSYCHOLOGICAL CLUES:
    • Feeling low, hopeless, or losing interest in things? (Depression)
    • Feeling worried, stressed, or on edge? (Anxiety)
    • Any major life stressors or relationship issues?
"Have you noticed any other symptoms, even if they seem unrelated?"
R: Risk Factors & History
  • Medical History: Hypertension, Diabetes, High Cholesterol, Heart Disease.
  • Surgical History: Prostate, bladder, or rectal surgery? Spinal surgery?
  • Medications: Beta-blockers, Thiazide diuretics, SSRIs, Finasteride.
  • Lifestyle: Smoking status? Alcohol intake? Recreational drug use?
"I need to ask about your general health, lifestyle, and any past operations."
E: Effect on Life
  • Impact on self-esteem and confidence
  • Strain on intimate relationships
  • Impact on desire to conceive
"How has this been affecting you and your relationship?"
R: Red Flags
  • New onset of chest pain on exertion (Angina).
  • New back pain with associated leg weakness, numbness around the groin, or bladder/bowel problems (Cauda Equina Syndrome).
  • Signs of prostate cancer (e.g., bony pain, urinary symptoms in the context of a new diagnosis).
  • Loss of secondary sexual characteristics (suggests significant hormonal issue).
"I just need to ask some specific questions to make sure we're not missing a serious underlying cause."
S: Severity / Scale
  • (Clinician note: Consider using a formal score like the IIEF-5 / SHIM).
"On a scale of 1 to 10, how much is this issue bothering you?"
