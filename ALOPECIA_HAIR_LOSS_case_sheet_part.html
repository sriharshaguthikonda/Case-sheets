Alopecia (Hair Loss)

1ST COMPLAINT: HAIR LOSS / THINNING

S: Site & Pattern
  • PATCHY LOSS (Discrete circles):
    • Alopecia Areata (smooth, round patches).
    • Tinea Capitis (scaly patches, broken hairs, ringworm).
    • Trichotillomania (hair pulling, irregular shapes).
  • DIFFUSE SHEDDING (General thinning):
    • Telogen Effluvium (generalized shedding).
    • Thyroid disease, Iron deficiency.
  • PATTERNED LOSS:
    • Androgenetic Alopecia (Male: receding hairline/vertex; Female: widening of part width).
  • MARGINAL (Hairline):
    • Traction Alopecia (tight braids/ponytails).
    • Frontal Fibrosing Alopecia (scarring).
"Where is the hair coming out? Is it coming out in clumps leaving bald spots, or is it thinning all over? Have you noticed your hairline moving back?"
O: Onset & Triggers
  • SUDDEN SHEDDING:
    • Alopecia Areata.
    • Telogen Effluvium (often starts 3 months after a trigger: childbirth, severe illness, surgery, crash diet).
    • Chemotherapy (Anagen effluvium - days/weeks).
  • GRADUAL THINNING:
    • Androgenetic Alopecia (years).
    • Scarring Alopecias (Lichen Planopilaris).
"When did you first notice the loss? Did it happen suddenly, or have you been losing hair slowly over years? Did anything stressful happen about 3 months before this started?"
D: Duration
  • Acute (< 6 months) vs. Chronic (> 6 months).
"How long has this been going on?"
A/R: Aggravating & Relieving Factors
  • AGGRAVATING (Hair Care):
    • Tight hairstyles (braids, cornrows).
    • Chemical treatments (bleach, relaxers, perms).
    • Heat styling.
  • AGGRAVATING (Lifestyle):
    • Poor diet (low protein/iron), stress, smoking.
  • MEDICATIONS:
    • Retinoids, beta-blockers, anticoagulants, lithium, hormonal contraceptives.
"Do you wear your hair in tight styles or use chemicals? Have you started any new medications recently?"
C: Character of Shedding
  • ROOTS PRESENT (White bulb):
    • Suggests Telogen (resting phase) hair. Normal in Telogen Effluvium.
  • BREAKAGE (No root):
    • Suggests hair shaft damage (over-processing) or Tinea Capitis.
  • SCARRING (Shiny skin, no follicles):
    • Cicatricial (Scarring) Alopecia (Lichen Planopilaris, DLE). Hair will not grow back.
"When the hair falls out, do you see a little white bulb at the end, or does it look like it's snapping off? Does the skin where the hair was look smooth and shiny like a scar?"
R: Related Body Hair
  • LOSS ELSEWHERE: Eyebrows, eyelashes, body hair.
    • Alopecia Totalis (entire scalp) / Universalis (entire body).
    • Thyroid disease (loss of outer third of eyebrow).
"Are you losing hair from anywhere else, like your eyebrows, eyelashes, or body?"
A: Associated Symptoms
  • SCALP SYMPTOMS:
    • Itching/Burning/Pain: Suggests inflammation/infection (Tinea, Scarring Alopecia). Androgenetic alopecia and Telogen effluvium are usually painless.
    • Scaling/Dandruff: Seborrheic dermatitis, Psoriasis, Tinea.
  • NAIL CHANGES:
    • Pitting/Ridging: Strongly associated with Alopecia Areata and Psoriasis.
  • SIGNS OF HYPERANDROGENISM (PCOS):
    • Acne, Hirsutism (excess facial hair), irregular periods. (Suggests hormonal cause for pattern loss).
  • SYSTEMIC SIGNS:
    • Fatigue, weight changes, cold intolerance (Thyroid/Iron deficiency).
    • Joint pain, sun sensitivity (Lupus).
"Does your scalp itch, burn, or hurt? Have you noticed any changes in your nails? For women: Have you had any acne, facial hair growth, or irregular periods?"
P: Progression
"Is the bald patch getting bigger? Is the thinning spreading?"
P: Pattern (Timeline)
  • CYCLICAL: Seasonal shedding.
  • RECURRENT: Patches that grow back and fall out again (Alopecia Areata).
"Does the hair ever grow back on its own and then fall out again?"
E: Effect on life
  • Significant psychological distress, loss of confidence, social withdrawal.
"How is this affecting your confidence and daily life? Do you feel the need to cover your head?"
R: Red Flags
  • SCARRING (CICATRICIAL) ALOPECIA: Redness, scaling, and loss of follicular openings. Irreversible if not treated early.
  • DISCOID LUPUS: Red, scaly, pigmented plaques with scarring.
  • VIRILIZATION: Rapid onset balding with deep voice/clitoromegaly (Androgen-secreting tumor).
  • KERION: Boggy, pus-filled lump in Tinea Capitis (risk of permanent scarring).
"I need to check for signs of scarring. Have you noticed any smooth, shiny skin where the hair used to be?"
S: Severity / Scale
  • Pull Test: How many hairs come out with a gentle tug? (Active shedding).
  • Savin/Ludwig Scale (Female Pattern).
  • Hamilton-Norwood Scale (Male Pattern).
  • SALT Score (Alopecia Areata).
"If you run your fingers through your hair gently, how many strands usually come out? A few, or a handful?"
