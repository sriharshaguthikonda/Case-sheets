1ST COMPLAINT: NECK SWELLING/LUMP

S: Site
  • ANTERIOR TRIANGLE
    • Thyroid nodule
    • Thyroglossal cyst
    • Lymphadenopathy
  • POSTERIOR TRIANGLE
    • Lymphadenopathy
    • Branchial cyst
    • Cystic hygroma
  • MIDLINE
    • Thyroglossal cyst
    • Dermoid cyst
    • Goiter
  • SUBMANDIBULAR
    • Salivary gland stone/tumor
    • Lymphadenopathy
"Where exactly is the lump? Can you point to it?"
O: Onset
  • SUDDEN
    • Reactive lymph node
    • Infected cyst
    • Hematoma
  • GRADUAL
    • Goiter
    • Tumor (benign/malignant)
    • Chronic lymphadenopathy
"When did you first notice the lump? Did it appear suddenly or grow over time?"
D: Duration & Painlessness
  • SHORT DURATION + PAIN
    • Inflammatory
  • LONGER DURATION + PAINLESS
    • Malignant
  • LONG DURATION + LESS PAIN
    • Chronic Inflammation
  • LONG DURATION + PAINLESS
    • Benign
"How long has the lump been there? And is it painful?"
A: Aggravating Factors
"Does anything seem to make the lump larger or more painful?"
R: Relieving Factors
"Is there anything that makes it smaller or less uncomfortable?"
C: Character
  • SOFT/FLUCTUANT
    • Cyst
    • Thyroglossal cyst
    • Branchial cyst
  • FIRM
    • Thyroid nodule
    • Lymphadenopathy
  • HARD
    • Malignancy
    • Calcified node
  • MOBILITY: Is it attached to the skin or underlying structures?
"What does the lump feel like? Is it soft, firm, or hard? Is it mobile?"
R: Radiation
"Do you feel any pain or sensation spreading from the lump?"
A: Associated Symptoms
  • VOICE CHANGES (Hoarseness)
    • Thyroid cancer
    • Laryngeal issue
  • DIFFICULTY SWALLOWING (Dysphagia)
    • Goiter
    • Pharyngeal pouch
    • Esophageal issue
  • DIFFICULTY IN RESPIRATION
    • Airway compression
  • WEIGHT CHANGES
    • Hyper/Hypothyroidism
    • Malignancy
  • FEVER, NIGHT SWEATS
    • Lymphoma
    • Tuberculosis
    • Systemic infection
  • RECENT INFECTION (e.g., sore throat)
    • Reactive lymphadenopathy
  • OTHER SWELLINGS
    • Neurofibromatosis
    • Hodgkin's disease
  • SECONDARY CHANGES: Softening, Ulceration, Fungation, Inflammation
  • IMPAIRMENT OF FUNCTION: e.g., neck movement
"Have you noticed any other symptoms along with the neck lump?"
P: Progression
  • VERY RAPID
    • Inflammatory
  • RAPIDLY
    • Malignant
  • SLOWLY
    • Benign
  • REGRESSIVE
    • Inflammatory
  • STATIC
    • Benign
  • STATIC -> RAPID
    • Benign -> Malignant transformation
"Has the lump changed in size since you first noticed it?"
P: Pattern
"Does the size of the lump change? Does it come and go?"
E: Effect on Life
"How has this lump affected you? Is it causing any concern, discomfort or disfigurement?"
R: Red Flags
  • HARD, FIXED, NON-TENDER LUMP
    • Malignancy
  • PERSISTENT HOARSENESS
    • Laryngeal cancer
    • Recurrent laryngeal nerve palsy
  • WEIGHT LOSS, FEVER, NIGHT SWEATS
    • Lymphoma
    • Metastatic cancer
    • TB
    • AIDS
  • ULCERATION OR SKIN CHANGES OVER THE LUMP
    • Malignancy
  • STRIDOR (noisy breathing)
    • Airway compression - medical emergency!
"I need to ask a few specific questions to make sure we're not missing anything important."
S: Severity / Scale
"On a scale of 1 to 10, how much is this lump bothering you?"
