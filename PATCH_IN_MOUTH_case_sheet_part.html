1ST COMPLAINT: PLAQUE / SORE / PATCH IN MOUTH

S: Site
  • Lateral border of the tongue (High risk)
  • Floor of the mouth (High risk)
  • Buccal mucosa (inner cheek)
  • Gingiva (gums)
  • Palate (roof of mouth)
"Can you show me exactly where the patch is located?"
O: Onset
  • Was it found incidentally (e.g., by a dentist) or did you notice it yourself?
  • Any preceding trauma? (e.g., sharp tooth, ill-fitting denture)
"When did you first notice this patch or sore?"
D: Duration
  • Any ulcer or sore present for > 3 weeks is a red flag for malignancy.
"How long has it been there? Has it been present continuously?"
A/R: Aggravating & Relieving Factors
"Does anything seem to irritate it, like spicy foods?"
C: Character
  • APPEARANCE
    • White Patch (Leukoplakia): Cannot be rubbed off.
    • Red Patch (Erythroplakia): High risk of malignancy.
    • Mixed Red & White (Erythroleukoplakia): Very high risk.
    • White patch that CAN be rubbed off, leaving a raw base -> Oral Candidiasis (Thrush)
  • SYMPTOMS
    • Painful vs. Painless: Painless --> malignancy.
    • Does it bleed easily (friable)?
    • Is it flat, slightly raised, or ulcerated?
    • Is the base hard to the touch (indurated)?
"Can you describe what it looks and feels like? Is it painful?"
R: Radiation (of Pain)
  • Referred otalgia (ear pain) can be a sign of nerve involvement from a tongue or pharyngeal cancer.
"If there is any pain, does it travel anywhere, for example, to your ear?"
A: Associated Symptoms & Risk Factors
  • LOCAL SYMPTOMS (Red Flags)
    • A new lump in the neck (cervical lymphadenopathy)
    • Unexplained loose teeth
    • Numbness of the tongue or lip (nerve invasion)
    • Difficulty opening the jaw (trismus)
    • Change in voice (hoarseness) or speech
    • Difficulty or pain with swallowing (dysphagia/odynophagia)
  • RISK FACTOR ASSESSMENT
    • Smoking History: Pack-years?
    • Alcohol History: Units per week?
    • Use of chewing tobacco, betel nut, or paan?
    • Poor dental hygiene or ill-fitting dentures
    • History of immunosuppression (e.g., HIV, transplant)
  • SYSTEMIC
    • Unexplained weight loss, fatigue
"Have you noticed any other changes in your mouth or your general health?"
P: Progression
  • Any new areas of ulceration or bleeding?
"Has the patch changed in any way since you first noticed it - in size, shape, color, or texture?"
P: Pattern
"Is it always there, or does it seem to come and go?"
E: Effect on Life
"How has this been affecting you? Does it interfere with your eating, drinking, or talking?"
R: Red Flags (Summary)
  • An oral ulcer or sore that has not healed in over 3 weeks.
  • A red (erythroplakia) or mixed red/white (erythroleukoplakia) patch.
  • A hard, fixed lump in the mouth or a new lump in the neck.
  • Unexplained loose teeth, numbness, or difficulty swallowing.
"I'm just going to summarize the key points I'm looking for."
S: Severity / Bother Scale
"On a scale of 1 to 10, how much is this sore or patch bothering you?"
