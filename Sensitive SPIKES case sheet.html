    MANAGEMENT PLAN (SPIKES Framework)

    • S: Setting up the Interview
      • Environment: Private, quiet "This room is private so we can speak freely."
      • Rapport: Build trust "I’m here to support you through this."
      • Participants: Confirm attendees "Would you like anyone else here, like family?"
    • P: Perception (Assess patient’s understanding)
      • Open-ended questions "Can you tell me what you understand so far?"
      • Identify misconceptions "It sounds like you think [misconception]; let’s clarify."
    • I: Invitation (Patient’s preference for information)
      • Information level "Do you want all the details or just the main points?"
      • Specific concerns "Is there something specific you’re worried about?"
    • K: Knowledge (Provide clear information)
      • Simple language "The tests show [diagnosis] which means [explanation]."
      • Avoid jargon "Instead of ‘metastasis,’ it means the cancer has spread."
      • Chunk and check "Does that make sense? Any questions so far?"
    • E: Emotions (Address emotional reactions)
      • Acknowledge feelings "It’s okay to feel upset or scared about this."
      • Offer support "Take your time; I’m here for you."
    • S: Strategy and Summary
      • Next steps "We can explore [option, e.g., palliative care] to help you."
      • Summarize "To recap, we discussed [key points]. Is that correct?"
      • Resources "Here’s a pamphlet on [topic] for more info."
      • Follow-up "Let’s meet again on [date] to check in."

    SPECIFIC SCENARIOS

    • Breaking Bad News
      • Diagnosis/prognosis "The cancer is advanced, so we’ll focus on comfort."
      • Emotional support "This is a lot to take in; how are you feeling?"
    • Capacity & Consent Discussions
      • Assess capacity "Can you explain what this treatment involves?"
      • Involve others "Since it’s hard for you, we’ll ask your [relative]."
    • Confidentiality / DVLA / Legal Disclosures
      • Explain duty "For safety, I must report your seizures to DVLA."
      • Support patient "I’ll help you with transport options."
    • Safeguarding & Domestic Violence
      • Assess risk "Can you tell me more about what happened?"
      • Report and support "We’ll contact a social worker to keep you safe."
    • Difficult Management Conversations
      • Treatment planning "Here are your options; what feels right for you?"
      • Error disclosure "I’m sorry, there was a mistake with [issue]; we’re fixing it."
    • End-of-Life Care Discussions
      • Preferences "Would you prefer to focus on comfort at home?"
      • Advance care planning "Have you thought about a DNR order?"
    • Discussing Prognosis
      • Be honest "We can’t predict exactly, but it may be months."
      • Address uncertainty "We’ll keep monitoring and update you."
    • Addressing Non-Compliance
      • Explore reasons "I see you’re hesitant about surgery—can you tell me why?"
      • Respect autonomy "It’s your choice, and I’m here to support you."

    FOLLOW-UP PLAN
