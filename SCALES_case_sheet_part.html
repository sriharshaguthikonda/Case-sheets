1ST COMPLAINT: SCALY SKIN / RASH

S: Site & Distribution
  • Specific Locations are Key:
    • Extensors (Elbows, Knees), Scalp, Lower Back, Navel -> Psoriasis
    • Flexures (Creases of elbows, behind knees), Face, Hands -> Atopic Eczema
    • Scalp, Eyebrows, Sides of Nose, Chest -> Seborrhoeic Dermatitis
    • Groin (Jock Itch), Feet (Athlete's Foot), Body (Ringworm) -> Tinea (Fungal)
    • Sun-Exposed Areas (Face, Forearms, Dorsal Hands) -> Actinic Keratosis, SCC/BCC
  • Symmetrical or Asymmetrical?
"Where on your body did the scaly patches first appear? And where are they now?"
O: Onset
  • Gradual
  • sudden
  • Recent sore throat? (Can trigger Guttate Psoriasis)
  • Started any new medications? (Drug eruption)

"When did this start? Was there any trigger, like an illness or stress?"
D: Duration
"How long has this been a problem for you?"
A/R: Aggravating & Relieving Factors
  • AGGRAVATING:
    • Weather (worse in winter),
    • stress,
    • alcohol,
    • scratching.
    • medications
    • sore throat
  • RELIEVING:
    • Moisturizers,
    • sunlight (often improves psoriasis but worsens lupus),
    • steroid creams.
"Does anything seem to make it better or worse, like weather, sunlight, or any creams?"
C: Character
  • Itch (Pruritus):
    • Very itchy -> Eczema, Lichen Planus, Scabies
    • Sometimes itchy -> Psoriasis
    • Itchy at the edges -> Tinea
  • Scale Appearance:
    • Thick, silvery-white -> Psoriasis
    • Fine, flaky, "dusty" -> Eczema
    • Greasy, yellowish -> Seborrhoeic Dermatitis
    • Hard, adherent, "sandpaper" texture -> Actinic Keratosis
  • Underlying Lesion:
    • Well-demarcated, salmon-pink/red plaque -> Psoriasis
    • Ill-defined, red, weepy, or dry patch -> Eczema
    • Annular (ring-shaped) with a raised, active border -> Tinea Corporis
    • Purple, polygonal papules with fine white lines (Wickham's Striae) -> Lichen Planus
"Can you describe the patches? What color are they? What do the scales look like? And most importantly, is it itchy?"
R: Radiation (Spread)
  • e.g., A single "herald patch" followed by a widespread rash on the trunk in a "Christmas tree" pattern? (Pityriasis Rosea)
"How has the rash spread since it first appeared?"
A: Associated Symptoms & History
  • Joint Pain / Swelling / Stiffness: Crucial question for Psoriatic Arthritis.
  • Nail Changes: Pitting, lifting of the nail (onycholysis), or thickening -> Classic for Psoriasis.
  • Personal or Family History of Atopy: Eczema, Asthma, or Allergic Rhinitis -> Suggests Atopic Eczema.
  • Systemic : Fever, weight loss, fatigue (rare, but consider for lupus or malignancy).
  • Oral Lesions: Lacy white patterns inside the cheeks (Lichen Planus).
  • Sun Exposure History: Significant lifetime sun exposure, history of sunburns (Risk for skin cancer).
"Have you noticed any other symptoms, for example in your joints or nails? Do you or your family have a history of skin problems, asthma, or hay fever?"
P: Progression
"Has any patch changed recently, for example started to bleed, ulcerate, or grow rapidly?"
P: Pattern
"Does it tend to come and go in flares, or is it always present?"
E: Effect on Life
"How has this skin condition been affecting you? Does it affect your sleep, confidence, or what you wear?"
R: Red Flags
  • Dangerours Diseases
  • Complications of suspected Diseases - ABCDE
  • Psoriasis
    • Pus
  • A lesion that is growing, changing, bleeding, or not healing.
  • cancer.

  • New onset of severe joint pain with a scaly rash.
  • Widespread rash with fever, blistering, or feeling generally unwell.

S: Severity / Bother Scale
"On a scale of 1 to 10, how much does this condition bother you?"
