1ST COMPLAINT: LOSS OF CONSCIOUSNESS / COLLAPSE

  • S: Situation / Setting "What exactly were you doing just before you lost consciousness?"
    • STANDING / PROLONGED SITTING
      • Vasovagal Syncope
      • Orthostatic Hypotension
    • EXERTION (Exercise, Climbing Stairs)
      • Cardiac Outflow Obstruction (HOCM, Aortic Stenosis)
      • Arrhythmia (VT, SVT)
    • POST‑PRANDIAL / HOT ENVIRONMENT
      • Vasovagal
    • AT REST / DURING SLEEP
      • Seizure
      • Nocturnal Arrhythmia (Brugada)
  • O: Onset & Prodrome "Did it come on suddenly or did you get any warning, like feeling hot, light‑headed or seeing spots?"
    • SUDDEN, NO WARNING
      • Cardiac Arrhythmia
      • Seizure
    • GRADUAL, WITH PRODROME (Nausea, Sweating, Tunnel Vision)
      • Vasovagal Syncope
      • Orthostatic Hypotension
    • AURAS (Odd Smell/Taste, Déjà‑vu)
      • Focal Seizure Generalising
  • D: Duration of Unconsciousness "Roughly how long were you out for? Seconds, minutes or longer?"
    • SECONDS (< 30 s)
      • Syncope
      • Transient Arrhythmia
    • 1‑2 MINUTES
      • Generalised Tonic‑Clonic Seizure
      • Prolonged Arrhythmia
    • PROLONGED (> 5 MIN / FAILED TO RECOVER)
      • Hypoglycaemia
      • Stroke / Structural CNS
  • A: Associated / Precipitating Factors "Was there anything else going on around that time—like chest pain, palpitations, severe headache, illness, alcohol, missed meals or strenuous exercise?"
    • PALPITATIONS / CHEST PAIN
      • Arrhythmia
      • Acute Coronary Syndrome
    • HEADACHE / NECK PAIN
      • Subarachnoid Haemorrhage
      • Vertebrobasilar TIA
    • FEVER
      • Septic shock
      • Septic Encephalopathy
    • ALCOHOL / DRUGS / TOXINS
      • Intoxication
      • Withdrawal Seizure
    • FASTING / INSULIN / EXERCISE
      • Hypoglycaemia
  • R: Recovery Phase / Post‑Event State "How did you feel afterwards? Did you come round quickly, feel confused or sleepy, or have any weakness?"
    • IMMEDIATE, FULL RECOVERY
      • Syncope
    • POST‑ICTAL CONFUSION, MYALGIA
      • Seizure
    • FOCAL DEFICIT PERSISTS
      • Stroke / TIA
  • C: Collateral / Witness Account "Did anyone see the episode? What did they say happened?"
    • JERKING LIMBS / TONIC‑CLONIC MOVEMENTS
      • Seizure
    • PALE, SWEATY, SLUMPED
      • Syncope
    • TONGUE BITE (LATERAL) OR INCONTINENCE
      • Seizure
    • EYES ROLLED BACK OR OPEN
      • Seizure
  • E: Effect on Life & Safety "Have these episodes affected your work, driving or daily activities?"
  • R: Red Flags "I need to ask a few serious‑sounding questions just to rule out anything dangerous."
    • LOC DURING EXERTION OR WHILE LYING FLAT (Suggests Cardiac Cause)
    • FAMILY HISTORY OF SUDDEN DEATH, ARRHYTHMIA OR CHANNELOPATHY
    • SEVERE THUNDERCLAP HEADACHE (SAH)
    • NEW‑ONSET NEUROLOGICAL DEFICIT / DROOP
    • RECURRENT EPISODES WITH INJURY
  • S: Severity / Frequency "How many episodes have you had and how serious have they been?"

    • Stokes-Adams attacks

    Episodes of ventricular asystole may complicate complete heart block or Mobitz type II second-degree AV block, or occur in patients with sinoatrial disease (see Fig. 18.50). This may cause recurrent syncope or 'Stokes-Adams' attacks.
