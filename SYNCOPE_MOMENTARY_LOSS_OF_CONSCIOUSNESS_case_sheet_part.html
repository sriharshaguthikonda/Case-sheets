1ST COMPLAINT: LOSS OF CONSCIOUSNESS / COLLAPSE

S: Situation / Setting
  • STANDING / PROLONGED SITTING
    • Vasovagal Syncope
    • Orthostatic Hypotension
  • EXERTION (Exercise, Climbing Stairs)
    • Cardiac Outflow Obstruction (HOCM, Aortic Stenosis)
    • Arrhythmia (VT, SVT)
  • POST‑PRANDIAL / HOT ENVIRONMENT
    • Vasovagal
  • AT REST / DURING SLEEP
    • Seizure
    • Nocturnal Arrhythmia (Brugada)
"What exactly were you doing just before you lost consciousness?"
O: Onset & Prodrome
  • SUDDEN, NO WARNING
    • Cardiac Arrhythmia
    • Heart block (Stokes-Adams attacks)
    • Seizure
  • GRADUAL, WITH PRODROME (Nausea, Sweating, Tunnel Vision)
    • Vasovagal Syncope
    • Orthostatic Hypotension
  • AURAS (Odd Smell/Taste, Déjà‑vu)
    • Focal Seizure Generalising
"Did it come on suddenly or did you get any warning, like feeling hot, light‑headed or seeing spots?"
D: Duration of Unconsciousness
  • SECONDS (< 30 s)
    • Syncope
    • Transient Arrhythmia
    • Stokes-Adams attack (transient complete heart block)
  • 1‑2 MINUTES
    • Generalised Tonic‑Clonic Seizure
    • Prolonged Arrhythmia
  • PROLONGED (> 5 MIN / FAILED TO RECOVER)
    • Hypoglycaemia
    • Stroke / Structural CNS
"Roughly how long were you out for? Seconds, minutes or longer?"
A: Associated / Precipitating Factors
  • PALPITATIONS / CHEST PAIN
    • Arrhythmia
    • Acute Coronary Syndrome
  • HEADACHE / NECK PAIN
    • Subarachnoid Haemorrhage
    • Vertebrobasilar TIA
  • FEVER
    • Septic shock
    • Septic Encephalopathy
  • ALCOHOL / DRUGS / TOXINS
    • Intoxication
    • Withdrawal Seizure
  • FASTING / INSULIN / EXERCISE
    • Hypoglycaemia
"Was there anything else going on around that time—like chest pain, palpitations, severe headache, illness, alcohol, missed meals or strenuous exercise?"
R: Recovery Phase / Post‑Event State
  • IMMEDIATE, FULL RECOVERY
    • Syncope
  • POST‑ICTAL CONFUSION, MYALGIA
    • Seizure
  • FOCAL DEFICIT PERSISTS
    • Stroke / TIA
"How did you feel afterwards? Did you come round quickly, feel confused or sleepy, or have any weakness?"
C: Collateral / Witness Account
  • JERKING LIMBS / TONIC‑CLONIC MOVEMENTS
    • Seizure
  • PALE, SWEATY, SLUMPED
    • Syncope
  • TONGUE BITE (LATERAL) OR INCONTINENCE
    • Seizure
  • EYES ROLLED BACK OR OPEN
    • Seizure
"Did anyone see the episode? What did they say happened?"
E: Effect on Life & Safety
"Have these episodes affected your work, driving or daily activities?"
R: Red Flags
  • LOC DURING EXERTION OR WHILE LYING FLAT (Suggests Cardiac Cause)
  • FAMILY HISTORY OF SUDDEN DEATH, ARRHYTHMIA OR CHANNELOPATHY
  • KNOWN OR SUSPECTED HEART BLOCK / PACEMAKER MALFUNCTION
  • SEVERE THUNDERCLAP HEADACHE (SAH)
  • NEW‑ONSET NEUROLOGICAL DEFICIT / DROOP
  • RECURRENT EPISODES WITH INJURY
"I need to ask a few serious‑sounding questions just to rule out anything dangerous."
S: Severity / Frequency
"How many episodes have you had and how serious have they been?"

Stokes-Adams attacks

Episodes of ventricular asystole may complicate complete heart block or Mobitz type II second-degree AV block, or occur in patients with sinoatrial disease (see Fig. 18.50). This may cause recurrent syncope or 'Stokes-Adams' attacks.
